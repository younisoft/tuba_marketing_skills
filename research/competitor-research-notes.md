## Aqar (aqar.fm / sa.aqar.fm)

**Entity confirmed:** Saudi Arabia's largest real estate listings marketplace, founded 2013–2014, HQ Riyadh, Chairman & CEO Ibrahim Ashshohail. Distinct from unrelated same-named entities in Qatar/Jordan/Iraq/UAE.

**Positioning:** "المنصة العقارية الأولى في المملكة" ("The Kingdom's #1 Real Estate Platform") — consistent X/Twitter bio. "The one who wants to understand real estate, starts with Aqar" (2025 blog line). Leads with scale/authority claims (12M+ downloads, 400M monthly searches, 35M monthly listing views, SAR 233bn listed value, ~2M MAU) repeated near-verbatim across every channel.

**Visual identity (verified directly from live production code):**
- Primary color `#008236` (deep forest/emerald green), confirmed as literal SVG fill and `--color-brand` CSS token
- Secondary tokens: `--color-brand-darker: #006f2e`, `--color-brand-lighter: #01943e`, info-blue `--color-info: #367cde`, danger-red `--color-danger: #b91f1f`, link-blue `#4d8ee6`
- Full light/dark mode token pairs exist
- Typography: IBM Plex Sans Arabic; custom "sarfont" for the SAR currency symbol
- Logo: solid green wordmark/icon, simple geometric linework, no gradient — functional tech-brand aesthetic, not luxury
- UI: RTL-first, card-based dense listing grid, minimal muted-gray line icons, utilitarian classifieds aesthetic (closer to a Gulf Zillow/Rightmove hybrid than a lifestyle brand)
- Photography: 100% user/agent-submitted listing photos — Aqar does not control imagery mood/quality (structural brand-consistency weakness)

**App store:** "عقار Aqar | Saudi Real Estate," 4.6/5 (~239K ratings). Features: interactive map search, Nafath-verified advertisers, Ejar digital rental contracts, "Aqar Plus" premium tier, BNPL rent financing partners ("Rise," "Ijari").

**Social (approx., third-party indexed):** Instagram ~85K, X ~158K, TikTok ~96K, **Snapchat ~262K (largest platform)**, LinkedIn ~18.7K, YouTube unverified subscriber count. Snapchat dominance signals genuine understanding of the Saudi audience beyond an Instagram-first default. TikTok content uses casual dialect + #اكسبلور (Explore) discovery hashtags.

**Flagship campaign:** 95th Saudi National Day — **airplane banner flyover over Riyadh**, claimed as first Saudi company to do this. CEO quote: "National Day is about our heritage and belief in our future." Strong earned-media/PR play; no comparable Ramadan campaign evidence found (gap, not confirmed absent).

**Content:** Recurring branded market-data report series "زبدة العقار" (H1 2024: 106,085 transactions / ~SAR 127bn) — an authority-building content property. Blog taxonomy: neighborhood ratings, project showcases, "Discover Saudi Arabia," decor, real estate news, construction/regulation explainers, structured reports.

**Trust stack:** REGA licensing + Nafath (national SSO) advertiser verification + Ejar rental-contract integration + Ministry of Tourism license number + VAT compliance indicator. No individual listing/agent rating or review layer found — trust is institutional, not socially proven listing-by-listing.

**Strategist's read — strengths:** disciplined "authority by numbers" narrative; genuinely hard-to-replicate gov-integration trust moat; bold earned-media stunt appetite; channel-appropriate tone shifting (formal/patriotic for big moments, casual for TikTok, data-authoritative for blog); real Snapchat-first channel intelligence.

**Strategist's read — weaknesses:** visual identity is purely functional, not an ownable "advertising creative" world (no photography direction, no illustration style, no motion identity); uncontrolled listing photography caps premium/emotional potential; on-site copy is nearly all transactional ("Search," "Add") with no distinctive verbal brand voice; no individual-listing social proof; thin/inconsistent seasonal campaign presence beyond the one National Day stunt; authority narrative is numbers-first, leaving emotional/warmth territory unclaimed.

## Bayut Saudi Arabia (bayut.sa)

**Entity confirmed:** Part of Dubizzle Group (formerly EMPG), Dubai-origin (founded 2008). bayut.sa launched May 2019 on the acquired Lamudi Middle East Saudi assets — a "leverage UAE playbook, buy local incumbent" market-entry strategy, not an organic Saudi build.

**Positioning:** CEO framing = exporting proven UAE capability into a larger, synergistic market. Named taglines: **"Journey to your Dream Home"** (first KSA-specific campaign, UX-focused); **"Buy, Sell, Rent, Properties"** (2025 "Mega Brand Campaign," OOH + digital, "Saudi Green" backdrop, spans X/IG/TikTok/Snapchat/LinkedIn); **"Real Price, Real Photos, Real Properties"** (UAE OOH, built around TruCheck™, likely a recurring group-level template); **"REAL HOMES LIVE HERE"** (found, detail inaccessible). Bio tension: Arabic bio claims *regional* ("largest in the Middle East") scale; English/App Store copy claims *local* ("#1 in the Kingdom") — inconsistent unified promise across languages.

**Visual identity:** Signature color is **green**, explicitly reframed for the 2025 KSA campaign as "Saudi Green" (flag-adjacent messaging) rather than a Saudi-native color choice — exact hex **not verifiable via search** (no public brand book found; site blocked automated fetch, 403). Logo described secondhand as having "curves reminiscent of Arabic curves" for friendliness — **not independently verified**. Proprietary trust-badge visual system: "Checked"/"TruCheck™," "Super Lister," "Quality Lister," "Responsive Broker," "TruBroker™" — a productized, ownable trust-badge vocabulary threaded through UI and campaigns alike.

**Website structure:** `/en/`, `/to-rent/`, `/new-projects/`, `/invest-in-saudi/` (dedicated foreign-investor/residency funnel), `/companies/` & `/brokers/` directories, `/trubroker/`, `/advertise-with-us/` (B2B), plus a `/blog/` with sub-verticals: saudi-market-insights, invest-in-saudi, bayut-academy, market-intelligence, bayut-news. Structural trust mechanic: REGA integration verifies seller identity, broker FAL license, and property deed before any ad goes live.

**App store (confirmed via direct fetch):** "Bayut KSA – Real Estate App," subtitle **"Buy, Rent, or Sell Simplified,"** 4.8/5 (379 ratings). Features: property search, detailed listings w/ floor plans, favorites/alerts, full Arabic support, direct agent contact (phone/SMS/WhatsApp), Invest in Saudi hub, Daily Rentals (short-stay), New Projects (off-plan). Review sentiment: praised for reliability/image quality/"no fraud issues," but recurring complaints about fake/outdated listings still slipping through and unresponsive agents — i.e., the trust *badge system* outruns actual enforcement.

**Social (approx.):** Instagram Arabic @bayutksa ~61K/1,080 posts vs. Instagram English @bayutksa.en ~648/50 posts (Arabic >> English, by a wide margin); TikTok @bayutsa ~63.4K; LinkedIn ~40.7K (market updates, milestones, culturally-timed posts); X @BayutKsa active; YouTube channel exists, scale unverified; Snapchat campaign-only presence (no static handle surfaced); Facebook exists.

**Flagship campaigns:** "Journey to your Dream Home" (first KSA campaign); 2025 "Mega Brand Campaign" (largest KSA branding effort to date, OOH + digital, Saudi Green, riding Vision-2030/hyper-growth narrative rather than emotional storytelling); **Snapchat AR Lens campaign** — best-documented, quantified KSA result: 13–53% lower CPM, 18% higher CTR, 33–70% higher save rate, 42–100% higher share rate, 6.5x earned reach uplift. UAE-origin templates likely to recur regionally: "Real Price, Real Photos, Real Properties" OOH (TruCheck-anchored, agency Mediaplus ME); TruBroker billboard campaigns spotlighting individual verified agents (~12x lead gen for featured agents, one board 150m+ on Hessa Street) — the recurring creative logic is **making the trust mechanism itself the campaign hero**, not lifestyle/aspiration advertising.

**Content:** Heavy content-marketing/authority model — "Saudi Market Insights" + "Market Intelligence" blog verticals (price indices, transaction volumes), "Bayut Academy" (practical how-to for sellers/agents, incl. photography-standards coaching), investor/foreign-ownership hub, agent/broker B2B funnel, lifestyle/decor tips, company/culture news on LinkedIn.

**Trust stack:** REGA + FAL license verification (structural, required before listing goes live) + proprietary "Tru-" badge family + Bayut Awards (agent/agency recognition, UAE-level, KSA edition unconfirmed) + recurring market reports as soft authority signal.

**Strategist's read — strengths:** genuinely ownable, productized trust vocabulary (not just a claim — a named, badge-based system reaching into agent incentives); regulatory-embedded credibility tightly localized to actual Saudi law; real content/data authority layer; demonstrated platform-native creative investment (the Snapchat AR Lens case is a strong, quantified example); high always-on campaign cadence (10+ campaigns/year, 13M+ visits in 2024); genuinely larger Arabic-first social distribution than English.

**Strategist's read — weaknesses:** brand voice reads as **imported, not indigenized** — "Buy, Sell, Rent, Properties" and "Saudi Green" feel like a relabeled UAE system, not a ground-up Saudi identity — a real strategic opening for a challenger to feel more authentically "of" Saudi Arabia; trust *claims* outrun trust *enforcement* per user reviews; English brand presence is thin/secondary despite an investor-facing value prop that needs it; emotional/lifestyle storytelling is thin relative to functional/macroeconomic messaging (Vision 2030, giga-projects) — a clear white space for warmer, identity/belonging-driven territory; no confirmed distinctly-Saudi visual identity beyond a relabeled color; inconsistent self-positioning across languages (regional vs. local #1 claims).

## Wasalt (wasalt.sa)

**Entity:** Founded 2020, REGA-licensed, subsidiary of **Quara Holding** (the brief's "Kinan" association could not be verified — flagged explicitly as unconfirmed). Reported scale: 500K+ users, 200+ partner brokers.

**Positioning/tagline:** **"في وصَلت… تأكد إنك وصلت"** ("At Wasalt… make sure you've arrived") — a wordplay on the brand name itself (Wasalt = "you arrived"), positioning the platform as the destination/endpoint of the property journey. **Notable pattern: name-as-verb wordplay as a Saudi copywriting convention** — worth testing whether "Tuba" supports a similar device. English app copy emphasizes AI-matching, verified/exclusive/documented properties, and an unusually broad service stack over single-purpose listing search.

**Service architecture (super-app pattern):** Wasalt Properties (search), Wasalt Matchmaker/"Shabeek" (request-first: post requirements, brokers respond — a demand-first model), Wasalt Projects (off-plan bookings), Wasalt Masterplans (land-parcel investment), Wasalt Auctions (e-wallet-funded electronic auctions — case: "Jeddah Gains" auction, 5 assets, 32+ bids fully on-platform), Wasalt Business (separate app for offices/marketers). April 2025: **Christie's International Real Estate x Wasalt** partnership launching a luxury brokerage arm targeting HNW/international buyers (e.g., Neptune Villas, interiors by Mouawad) — a deliberate stretch from mass-market into luxury/prestige.

**Visual identity:** Not verifiable via search (site blocked automated fetch; Instagram profile shows a geometric/stylized logomark, not legible in detail).

**Digital presence:** Website + separate corporate site + separate auction subdomain; consumer app + separate Business app; Instagram @wasaltofficial ~78K/1,118 posts; TikTok @wasalt; X @WasaltOfficial; LinkedIn; educational blog (blog.wasalt.sa) on e-auctions, financing, small-project support.

**Campaigns:** No specific, named Ramadan/National Day campaign verifiable via search.

## Daleel — two distinct entities (disambiguation important)

## Sakani (sakani.sa) — the category's psychological trust benchmark

Not a commercial competitor — a **Ministry of Municipal and Rural Affairs and Housing (MOMRAH)** platform under Saudi Vision 2030's Housing Program, launched 2017. Scale: 4.6M+ registered users, 1.2M+ 2024 app downloads, 117K+ families benefited in 2024 (93K+ moved in, +9% YoY). Sub-programs: Developmental Housing, "Joud" donation platform. Mission framing: raise homeownership to 70% by 2030; also functions partly as a private-listing marketplace layer for eligible buyers, not purely a subsidy portal.

**Major campaign — "بيت العمر" ("Home of a Lifetime")**, launched by the Real Estate Development Fund + NHC + Saudi National Bank (Oct 2025): down-payment support to SAR 150,000, installments from **SAR 1,499/month**, ~2.99% margin. **Pattern: emotional/legacy headline paired with one hyper-concrete monthly riyal number** — worth replicating in Tuba's own campaign copy logic. Standard funnel messaging: register → verify eligibility → choose unit → sign contract (a simple 4-step trust funnel repeated across gov-housing comms). Visual identity not verifiable via search.

## REGA (rega.gov.sa) — the compliance layer every platform must design around

Central regulator: property registration, non-government real estate activity regulation, sector development. ~2.36M registered digital-service users, ~4.8M annual site visits.

**FAL license system** — umbrella brand for licenses covering brokerage, marketing/advertising, property management, facilities management, auctions, consulting/analysis. 148,000+ FAL licenses issued. Public **FAL License Inquiry** e-service lets anyone verify a broker/entity's license — the direct analog of "checking a listing is real," a category-wide consumer trust ritual.

**Real Estate Advertisement License (ترخيص إعلان عقاري)** — separate, mandatory for *advertising* specifically (distinct from brokerage FAL), required across "social media, audio, visual or written media, exhibitions, and public events." New **"اللائحة التنظيمية للتسويق والإعلانات العقارية"** bylaw (12 articles: issuance conditions, ad content requirements, prohibitions — no misleading data, no disparaging competitors, no fake/duplicate listings, licensee obligations).

**Critical, directly actionable detail:** the regulation explicitly permits an ad's visible compliance content to be reduced to just **the license number + a QR code**, specifically so the license disclosure doesn't "disfigure" (تشويه) the ad's design. This is regulator-sanctioned minimal-badge design — Tuba can build an elegant, small, brand-consistent compliance badge rather than a bulky legal disclaimer block. REGA also runs a public ad-license verification lookup.

**Naming disambiguation (do not confuse):** عقاري/"Aqari" (REGA's own official e-services app for contracts/licenses — government infrastructure, not a marketplace) vs. عقار/"Aqar" (aqar.fm, the commercial marketplace researched above — advertisers verified via **Nafath**, Saudi's national ID SSO) vs. a third, separate private property-management tool also sometimes called "Aqari Platform."

## Other notable landscape players

- **Property Finder KSA** — UAE-origin regional marketplace, standard-format app, no distinctive campaign/visual-identity material surfaced; functional/parity competitor, not a differentiated voice.
- **Msaken/Masaken** — name fragmented across ≥3 unrelated entities (marketplace, developer, gov program); low confidence beyond name-level differentiation.
- **NHC (National Housing Company, nhc.sa)** — not a marketplace but the Kingdom's dominant master-plan developer; **highly relevant as a 2024 rebrand case study** (agency: Lippincott). New tagline **"Elevating Life"** (platform: "Life, Enhanced"). Rebrand rationale: closed a perception gap between "outdated social housing" and NHC's actual shift into large-scale, amenity-rich communities. Logo: central symbol surrounded by green botanical shapes referencing flagship projects. **Color rationale (citable precedent): green = national pride/growth; "warm sunset-inspired tones" = evening community gathering.** Imagery shift: deliberately moved from clinical bird's-eye masterplan views to ground-level, human, relatable community perspective; relocated sales centers from construction sites to aspirational city-center locations. Stated values: ambition, inclusiveness, reliability. Goals: 300K units by 2025, 600K by 2030, 70% national homeownership.
- **Sotheby's International Realty – Saudi Arabia** (opened March 2023, Al Faisaliah Tower Riyadh) — pure luxury positioning leveraging the global Sotheby's auction-house halo; targets branded residences, luxury hospitality-linked residential, Red Sea coastal developments. Signals a "prestige/international-license" pattern in the luxury segment (also seen with Christie's x Wasalt) — global brands licensing into local partners rather than homegrown luxury brands.
- **OpenSooq / Haraj** — generalist classifieds with strong-if-unspecialized real estate sections; a reminder Tuba also competes against generic marketplaces, not only dedicated PropTech apps.

## Saudi digital-marketing landscape, 2024–2026

- Social identities: 38.6M (111% of population — heavy multi-account usage); ~99% internet penetration
- YouTube 27.2M users (~79% of population); TikTok ad-reach equivalent to 138%+ of adults, penetration 90%+ (esp. 16–24); **Snapchat ~24.7M ad reach, consistently a top-3 global Snapchat market**, culturally resonant for its private/discreet nature, heavy daily usage; Instagram ~16.9M ad reach, strongest for lifestyle/aspirational content, Reels >> static; LinkedIn ~12M members (B2B/broker/developer relevance)
- **Content patterns that work for real estate:** short-form video dominance (TikTok/Snapchat/Reels) over static listing posts; behind-the-scenes/transparency content (office culture, site-visit/construction-progress) reportedly drives high engagement (5–8% cited); educational content (financing explainers, eligibility walk-throughs mirroring Sakani's step funnel); Arabic/Gulf-dialect content outperforms English on TikTok, with English subtitles layered in for expat reach; influencer marketing is major (TikTok/Snapchat/YouTube; ~70% of young Saudis report a social-influenced purchase) — paid promotion legally requires a **"Mawthooq"** license from the General Authority for Media Regulation, a parallel trust/compliance layer to REGA's FAL; 360°/VR virtual tours are an industry-wide aspiration, not confirmed as universal execution among the platforms studied

**Seasonal conventions:**
- **National Day (Sept 23):** a new official design identity/slogan is issued centrally each year (2025: **"فخرنا طبعنا"** — "Our Glory is Our Nature/Instinct") and brands are expected to adapt creative to that year's official theme rather than freelance a new concept; Snapchat runs dedicated National Day brand-campaign programs (e.g., past AR-lens "The Kingdom in Augmented Reality," one cited example reaching 4.9M users, ~70% organic share); Ministry of Commerce issues special discount licenses for the period, formalizing it as a promotional season, not just a branding moment
- **Ramadan:** real estate companies lean on **financing-term incentives (fee waivers, deferred/waived down payments, interest-free installments, deferred first installment until handover) rather than direct price cuts**, explicitly to protect a project's perceived value — plus intensified digital cadence, live site-visit broadcasts, and high-intent SEO/PPC targeting
- **Founding Day (Feb 22):** a second major national-pride occasion, runs alongside broad retail discounting (up to 80–90% cited in consumer categories); no real-estate-specific campaign specifics were verifiable, but worth accounting for as a third annual "national pride + commercial promotion" moment distinct from National Day

## Cross-competitor patterns extracted for the Tuba system

1. **Compliance-as-design-element, not disclaimer** — REGA explicitly sanctions a minimal license-number-plus-QR badge specifically to avoid "disfiguring" ad creative → Tuba should design one elegant, consistent FAL/license badge, not legal fine print
2. **Name-as-wordplay tagline convention** (Wasalt) — worth testing for Tuba's own naming/tagline system
3. **Government-adjacent trust language sets the category bar** (Sakani/NHC/REGA process-transparency framing: numbered steps, eligibility verification, license lookups) — commercial platforms that echo this borrow credibility from the government layer
4. **Financing-term incentives over price discounts** during Ramadan — protects brand/price perception long-term
5. **Reverse-marketplace positioning (Deal app)** shows room for structurally differentiated mechanics, not just palette differentiation
6. **Citable color-strategy precedent** (NHC: green=national pride/growth, warm sunset tones=community gathering) — a model for articulating *why* Tuba's purple/coral pairing means something, not just "we picked colors"
7. **Snapchat + TikTok are primary channels** for Saudi real estate, not secondary/experimental — Instagram Reels for aspirational/lifestyle, LinkedIn for B2B/broker/developer
8. **Trust-as-productized-badge-system** (Bayut's "Tru-" family) — a namable, visual, ownable trust mechanic outperforms a generic "verified" claim, but must be backed by real enforcement or it becomes a credibility liability (per Bayut's own review-sentiment gap)
9. **Numbers-first authority narratives (Aqar) leave emotional/warmth territory completely open** — validated white space for Tuba's "guidance and relief" positioning
10. **Imported vs. indigenous brand voice (Bayut)** — a real opening for Tuba to feel unmistakably, originally Saudi rather than a localized foreign template
