# Campaign Scorecard

> **Part of:** [AI_KNOWLEDGE_PLATFORM.md](../AI_KNOWLEDGE_PLATFORM.md)
> **Purpose:** evaluates a full campaign concept or completed campaign at the strategic level — distinct from [copy-scorecard.md](copy-scorecard.md) and [design-scorecard.md](design-scorecard.md), which score individual assets. Use at Brief approval (before production) and at campaign completion (for the [../brand-memory/campaign-history.md](../brand-memory/campaign-history.md) record).
> **Owner:** Marketing Manager
> **Review frequency:** semi-annually

---

## Scoring Dimensions (1-10 each)

### 1. Business Alignment
Does this campaign serve a real, stated business goal (project-analysis.md §9-10)? Is the objective one sentence, not a wish list?

### 2. Audience Fit
Is the audience a named persona (not "everyone")? Does the message match that persona's actual pain points/objections (messaging-framework.md, the relevant persona file)?

### 3. Creative Strength
Would this campaign fail the "could a competitor run this unchanged" test (advertising-dna.md §1)? A strong score means clearly, distinctly Tuba.

### 4. Offer
If applicable: is the offer real, specific, and — for Ramadan-adjacent timing — framed as financing-term rather than price-cut (campaign-playbook.md §1.3)?

### 5. Message
One clear core message, traceable to the Brand Promise (advertising-dna.md §2). Not multiple competing ideas.

### 6. Channel Fit
Are the chosen channels the right ones for this audience/goal (social-media-playbook.md's evidenced platform priority)? Is content genuinely native to each channel, not identically cross-posted?

### 7. KPIs
Are success metrics specific and numeric (advertising-playbook.md §5's KPI reference), matched to the campaign's actual funnel role?

### 8. Expected Impact
Is the projected outcome realistic given category benchmarks and any relevant [../brand-memory/](../brand-memory/) evidence, or is it aspirational without grounding?

## Scoring Sheet

```markdown
Campaign: _______________
Reviewer: _______________
Date: _______________
Stage: [ ] Brief review (pre-production)  [ ] Post-completion review

1. Business Alignment: __/10
2. Audience Fit: __/10
3. Creative Strength: __/10
4. Offer: __/10 or N/A
5. Message: __/10
6. Channel Fit: __/10
7. KPIs: __/10
8. Expected Impact: __/10

AVERAGE (excluding N/A): __/10
VERDICT: per quality-thresholds.md
```

## Two Uses of This Scorecard

| Stage | What's scored |
|---|---|
| **Brief review** (before production) | The plan itself — is this worth building? |
| **Post-completion review** | The realized campaign against its own brief — did it deliver what §1-8 promised? Feed the result into [../brand-memory/winning-campaigns.md](../brand-memory/winning-campaigns.md) or [../brand-memory/failed-campaigns.md](../brand-memory/failed-campaigns.md) |

---

## Best Practices
- Score at Brief stage even for small campaigns — a low Business Alignment or Audience Fit score here saves far more effort than catching the same issue after full production
- Compare Brief-stage and Post-completion scores over time — a consistent gap between planned and realized Creative Strength is a signal worth escalating to [../brand-memory/lessons-learned.md](../brand-memory/lessons-learned.md)

## Common Mistakes
- Only scoring at completion, missing the chance to catch a weak brief before resources are spent
- Scoring "Expected Impact" as pure optimism rather than checking it against real category benchmarks or brand-memory evidence
