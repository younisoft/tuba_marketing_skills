## 2. Visual Identity Comparison

| Player | Primary color (verified where possible) | Logo/type character | Photography control |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Aqar** | `#008236` deep green — **verified directly from production CSS/SVG** | Solid, geometric, functional wordmark; IBM Plex Sans Arabic | None — 100% user/agent-submitted photos |
| **Bayut** | Green, reframed as "Saudi Green" for KSA campaigns — exact hex **not verifiable** (no public brand book, site blocked automated fetch) | Rounded/friendly curves (secondhand description, unverified) | Coached via "Bayut Academy" photography guidance, not fully brand-controlled |
| **Wasalt** | **Not verifiable** — geometric/stylized mark visible on social, no confirmed hex | Unknown | Unknown |
| **NHC** (reference case) | Green (national pride/growth) + warm sunset tones (community/gathering) — **explicit, citable rationale** | Central symbol + botanical motif | Deliberately shifted from clinical aerial views to ground-level human perspective |
| **Tuba** | `#2A0C72` deep purple + `#F95A60` coral — **documented in official guidelines** | Rebus monogram (globe + letter + hut), circle-built bilingual wordmark | Undocumented — open gap (see visual-system.md §6) |

### The single most important finding in this section

**Every verified or credibly-reported competitor color in this category sits somewhere on the green spectrum** (Aqar's confirmed forest green, Bayut's "Saudi Green," NHC's national-pride green). Tuba's purple + coral is not just *different* — it is the **only non-green identity among the researched category leaders.** This is not a minor stylistic footnote; in a market where the dominant portals have converged on the same color family, purple/coral is Tuba's single most efficient, hardest-to-copy recognition asset. Protecting and repeating this color pairing consistently (per visual-system.md §1.7's 60/30/10 hierarchy) will do more for brand recognition than almost any other single design decision available to Tuba.

**Why the category converged on green:** green carries pre-existing associations of growth, trust, "go/proceed," and — per NHC's own stated rationale — national pride (echoing the Saudi flag). It is a safe, explicable choice. Tuba's purple signals something different and arguably more accurate to its actual brand brief: authority, depth, and warmth (via the coral pairing) rather than "growth and go." **Do not drift toward green for legibility-of-category reasons** — the temptation to "look like real estate" by adopting green is exactly the homogenization Tuba's palette currently escapes.

## 4. App Store / Google Play Patterns

| Player | App Store subtitle/positioning | Rating |
|---|---|---|
| Aqar | "Saudi Real Estate" / "#1 Real Estate App in Saudi Arabia" | 4.6/5 (~239K ratings) |
| Bayut | "Buy, Rent, or Sell Simplified" | 4.8/5 (379 ratings) |

**Pattern:** both leaders use plain, functional subtitles — no emotional language survives into the App Store listing itself, even when the brand has emotional territory elsewhere (Bayut's "Journey to your Dream Home" campaign line never appears in its own App Store copy). **Implication for Tuba:** there's a real opportunity to be the first major Saudi property app whose store listing *itself* carries brand voice — even a single warm phrase in the subtitle — since the category treats the App Store purely as a feature-spec sheet.

**Review sentiment pattern:** Bayut's own users report fake/outdated listings and unresponsive agents despite an elaborate trust-badge system — a direct warning for Tuba: **a trust *badge* is only as credible as the trust *enforcement* behind it.** Any FAL/verification badge Tuba adopts (see design-components.md) must be backed by real, current moderation or it will erode trust faster than having no badge at all.

## 5. Social Media Presence — Platform-by-Platform

| Platform | Category pattern | Source evidence |
|---|---|---|
| **Snapchat** | The *largest* organic channel for the category leader (Aqar ~262K, its biggest platform) and the site of Bayut's best-documented, highest-performing paid campaign (AR Lens: 13–53% lower CPM, 18% higher CTR, 6.5x earned reach) | Aqar, Bayut research |
| **TikTok** | Strong secondary channel (~60-96K range for both Aqar and Bayut), casual dialect, discovery-hashtag culture (#اكسبلور) | Aqar, Bayut research |
| **Instagram** | Arabic-language accounts dramatically outperform English ones in the same brand (Bayut: ~61K Arabic vs. ~648 English) | Bayut research |
| **LinkedIn** | Used for B2B/corporate register — milestones, partnerships, culturally-timed greetings, recruitment — not consumer-facing storytelling | Aqar, Bayut research |
| **X/Twitter** | Announcement/customer-service register, brand bio as elevator pitch (both Aqar and Bayut use nearly identical "the Kingdom's leading platform" phrasing) | Aqar, Bayut research |
| **YouTube** | Consistently the *weakest*/least-invested channel across all researched competitors — no subscriber data was even surfaceable for either leader | Aqar, Bayut research |

**Implication for Tuba:** Snapchat and TikTok should be treated as **primary**, not experimental, channels (see [social-media-playbook.md](../advertising-system/social-media-playbook.md)) — this isn't a generic "youth platform" assumption, it's the specific, evidenced behavior of the two category leaders' own audiences. YouTube is the clearest **white-space opportunity**: near-zero competitor investment means even modest, consistent long-form/educational video content (property tours, market explainers, buyer-guidance series — directly serving Tuba's "guidance" brand promise) could win disproportionate share of a channel no one else is seriously contesting.

## 6. Advertising Campaigns — What's Been Tried, What Worked, What's Missing

### Notable campaigns found
| Campaign | Brand | Format | Result / signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| National Day airplane flyover | Aqar | Physical/earned-media stunt | First-mover claim, trade press pickup, qualitative "thousands of shares" — no hard reach numbers disclosed |
| "Journey to your Dream Home" | Bayut | Digital brand campaign | UX/ease-of-use focused, not emotional |
| 2025 "Mega Brand Campaign" ("Buy, Sell, Rent, Properties") | Bayut | OOH + digital, multi-platform | Largest KSA effort to date, but rides macro Vision-2030 narrative rather than human storytelling |
| Snapchat AR Lens ("envision your dream home") | Bayut | Platform-native interactive ad | Best-quantified result in the entire research set — strong evidence platform-native creative beats repurposed banner ads |
| TruBroker billboard campaigns (UAE template, likely portable) | Bayut | OOH, agent-as-hero | ~12x lead generation for featured agents — makes the *trust mechanism* the creative hero |
| "بيت العمر" (Home of a Lifetime) | Sakani/REDF/NHC/SNB | Integrated campaign | Emotional/legacy headline + one hyper-concrete monthly riyal figure (SAR 1,499/month) — a copy pattern, not a competitor campaign, but directly reusable logic |

### What's conspicuously missing across the entire category
- **No competitor researched has a confirmed, well-documented Ramadan brand campaign** — only general financing-incentive conventions and light-touch social greetings were found. This is a genuine seasonal white space (see campaign-playbook.md).
- **No competitor demonstrates sustained emotional/lifestyle storytelling** — Aqar leads with numbers, Bayut leads with trust-badges and macroeconomic narrative, both largely bypass "what does home actually mean to you" territory.
- **No competitor's visual system extends into a distinctive illustration, iconography, or motion language** — every identity researched lives primarily in UI chrome and campaign-specific OOH, not a repeatable, ownable creative *system*. This is precisely the gap [visual-system.md](../advertising-system/visual-system.md) and [iconography.md](../advertising-system/iconography.md) close for Tuba.

## 7. Copywriting & Slogan Patterns

| Pattern | Example | Read |
|---|---|---|
| **Scale-as-authority** | Aqar's near-verbatim repetition of "12M downloads / 400M monthly searches" across every channel | Effective for credibility, but emotionally flat — a numbers arms race Tuba shouldn't try to win head-on with a newer platform |
| **Productized trust vocabulary** | Bayut's "Tru-" prefix family (TruCheck, TruBroker) | A genuinely strong pattern: name your trust mechanism, don't just claim it. Tuba's FAL/license badge (see design-components.md) should get a memorable, ownable name, not a generic "Verified" label |
| **Name-as-wordplay tagline** | Wasalt: "في وصَلت... تأكد إنك وصلت" | A distinctly Saudi copywriting convention — literal wordplay on the brand name's meaning. Worth exploring whether "Tuba/طوبة" (with its "goodness/blessing" etymology, see project-analysis.md §1) supports a similar device — see copywriting.md |
| **Emotional headline + hyper-concrete number** | Sakani's "بيت العمر" + "SAR 1,499/month" | Pattern: lead with legacy/emotional resonance, close with one specific, ungimmicky number. Directly usable in Tuba's campaign copy (campaign-playbook.md) |
| **Register code-switching by channel** | Aqar: formal/patriotic for PR, casual dialect for TikTok, data-authoritative for blog | Confirms channel-specific tone variation (not a single flat voice everywhere) is category-standard practice — see social-media-playbook.md |
| **On-platform copy stays transactional even when off-platform copy is more expressive** | Both Aqar and Bayut | Confirms the exact gap identified in project-analysis.md §6 for Tuba itself — this is a category-wide failure mode, and fixing it *inside the product*, not just in campaigns, is a real differentiation opportunity |

## 8. Trust-Building Comparison

| Mechanism | Aqar | Bayut | Sakani/REGA | Tuba (current state) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Government licensing integration | REGA + Nafath + Ejar | REGA + FAL (structural, pre-publish gate) | Native (is the government) | FAL/Ad-license workflow exists in product (project-analysis.md §1) — not yet a *marketing* asset |
| Named/branded trust mechanism | Institutional only (no branded badge name) | "Tru-" family (TruCheck, TruBroker) | N/A | Not yet developed — clear opportunity |
| Individual listing/agent social proof | None found | Badges (Super Lister, Quality Lister) but review-sentiment gap on enforcement | N/A | Not yet developed |
| Recurring authority content (market reports) | "زبدة العقار" report series | "Saudi Market Insights" blog vertical | N/A | Not yet developed — see content-system.md |

**Synthesis:** trust in this category is currently won through **institutional integration** (everyone has REGA in some form) plus, at best, **one layer of branded badge vocabulary** (Bayut only). No competitor has built trust into an emotionally warm, human-guided *experience* — every trust signal researched is procedural/legalistic in tone, even when visually badge-ified. This directly validates Tuba's founding brand brief (project-analysis.md §2): **"relief" and "guidance"** are trust-adjacent emotional territories no competitor has claimed, and they are differentiated from "verified/licensed/checked" language without contradicting it — Tuba can be both compliant *and* warm, where competitors have so far only managed compliant *or* (occasionally, imperfectly) trustworthy-sounding.

## 10. Seasonal Campaign Conventions (category-wide, not brand-specific)

- **National Day (Sept 23):** a new official government design identity/slogan is issued each year (2025: "فخرنا طبعنا") — brand creative is expected to adapt to that year's official theme, not freelance a wholly separate concept. Snapchat runs dedicated National Day brand programs; the Ministry of Commerce issues formal discount licenses for the period.
- **Ramadan:** category convention favors **financing-term incentives (fee waivers, deferred/interest-free installments) over direct price discounts**, explicitly to protect long-term brand/price perception — plus intensified digital cadence and live site-visit content.
- **Founding Day (Feb 22):** a second, distinct national-pride occasion running alongside broad retail discounting — currently under-served by named real-estate campaigns in this research, a possible secondary seasonal opportunity.

Full campaign mechanics for each occasion are built out in [campaign-playbook.md](../advertising-system/campaign-playbook.md).

## 11. What NOT to Copy (explicit anti-patterns)

- **Do not adopt green.** The entire visible category has converged there — see §2.
- **Do not lead with download/scale numbers as the primary brand promise.** Tuba is not yet the incumbent by volume, and competing on Aqar's own numbers game is a losing frame; compete on the emotional and craft territory numbers can't cover.
- **Do not let trust badges outrun trust enforcement.** Bayut's own user reviews show what happens when badge marketing exceeds moderation reality — any Tuba trust badge must be backed by real, current verification.
- **Do not import a foreign playbook and relabel it "Saudi."** Bayut's "Saudi Green" reframing of a UAE-origin brand system is a specific, avoidable mistake — Tuba's advantage is that its brand foundation (project-analysis.md, current-brand-analysis.md) was never imported from elsewhere.
- **Do not treat on-platform product copy as exempt from brand voice.** Every competitor researched keeps its most interesting language in campaigns/PR and reverts to generic transactional copy ("Search," "Add") inside the actual product — this is the single most consistent category-wide weakness, and the easiest one for Tuba to simply not repeat.

## Executive Summary

Saudi Arabia's real estate marketplace category has consolidated around a small number of converging patterns: **green as the near-universal brand color**, **institutional trust signaling (REGA/FAL/Nafath) as table stakes**, **numbers-first authority claims** (Aqar), and **at most one layer of productized trust branding** (Bayut's "Tru-" family) as the extent of anyone's advertising *system* sophistication. No researched competitor has built a repeatable visual language beyond UI chrome and one-off campaigns, and none has claimed genuinely warm, human, "guidance and relief" emotional territory — every brand voice found defaults to functional/transactional the moment it touches the actual product experience, regardless of how expressive its campaign-only language gets.

This leaves at least four clear, evidenced openings for Tuba: **(1)** a non-green, already-distinctive color identity that only needs consistent application to become instantly recognizable in-category; **(2)** the unclaimed emotional register of warmth and guidance, which is *compatible with*, not opposed to, the compliance-and-verification signals every competitor already leans on; **(3)** a near-empty YouTube channel and a thin Ramadan-campaign field, both immediately actionable; and **(4)** an existing Tuba product mechanic (request-first property matching) that competitors are actively building and marketing as a headline differentiator, while Tuba already has it and simply hasn't told anyone yet. None of this requires out-innovating the market on features — it requires operationalizing brand and voice as consistently as the market leaders operationalize trust badges and scale claims, which is exactly what the remaining phases of this system build.
