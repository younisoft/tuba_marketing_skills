# Swipe File — Pattern Extraction, Not Imitation

> **Layer:** Brand Intelligence (static, additive — new real-world patterns can be added as observed)
> **Purpose:** extract *reusable structural patterns* from real advertising (mostly in-category, drawn from [competitor-analysis.md](../knowledge-base/competitor-analysis.md) and [research/competitor-research-notes.md](../research/competitor-research-notes.md)) — never to copy, but to understand *why* a pattern works so Tuba can build its own, differently-executed version.
> **Owner:** Creative Director / Design lead
> **Review frequency:** quarterly — add new observed patterns as they appear
> **Related documents:** [competitor-analysis.md](../knowledge-base/competitor-analysis.md), [prompt-patterns.md](../advertising-system/prompt-patterns.md), [creative-inspirations.md](creative-inspirations.md)

> **The rule that governs this entire document:** every entry below extracts a *mechanism*, not a look. If a pattern's "why it works" explanation could not survive being executed in completely different colors, words, and imagery, it isn't a real pattern — it's just a description of someone else's ad. Never recommend imitation; only recommend the underlying mechanism, reapplied in Tuba's own system.

## 1. Headline Patterns

**Pattern: name-as-wordplay** (Wasalt: "في وصَلت... تأكد إنك وصلت" — competitor-analysis.md §7)
**Why it works:** a brand name that's also a real word creates a headline that feels discovered, not written — the reader completes the meaning themselves, which is more persuasive than being told.
**Tuba application:** explore whether "طوبة" (goodness/blessing etymology — project-analysis.md §1) supports a similar self-referential wordplay, built independently in Tuba's own voice (headline-library.md §6, copywriting.md §3).

**Pattern: emotional headline + one hyper-concrete number** (Sakani "بيت العمر" + "SAR 1,499/month" — competitor-analysis.md §7)
**Why it works:** the emotional phrase earns attention; the specific number earns belief — neither alone does both jobs.
**Tuba application:** already formalized as "The Concrete Number" formula, headline-library.md §3, copywriting.md §3.

## 2. CTA Patterns

**Pattern: trust-mechanism-as-CTA-subject** (Bayut's "Tru-" badge family used as the actual campaign hero — competitor-analysis.md §6)
**Why it works:** makes the *proof* the star of the ad instead of a supporting footnote — the CTA becomes "see the proof," which is a lower-resistance ask than "buy now."
**Tuba application:** "Verify it yourself" (cta-library.md §15) applies this exact mechanism to the FAL badge, built as Tuba's own named trust asset rather than borrowing Bayut's naming convention (design-components.md §2.1).

## 3. Photography/Composition Patterns

**Pattern: agent-as-hero billboard** (Bayut's TruBroker campaigns — ~12x lead generation for featured agents, competitor-analysis.md §6)
**Why it works:** individual, named, real people outperform generic platform imagery for the specific audience (other brokers, and buyers) who are evaluating credibility, not browsing inventory.
**Tuba application:** broker-persona content (broker-personas.md) should feature real agents by name and face, not stock "professional handshake" imagery — see visual-system.md §6.1's real-people-not-staged-clichés rule.

**Pattern: ground-level, human-scale imagery over clinical aerial masterplan views** (NHC's 2024 rebrand shift — competitor-analysis.md §2)
**Why it works:** aerial/masterplan views communicate scale to investors but communicate coldness to consumers — the rebrand specifically found this was hurting perceived approachability.
**Tuba application:** developer-partnership content should always pair any aerial/masterplan shot with at least one ground-level, human moment (campaign-playbook.md §2.3).

## 4. Typography Patterns

**Pattern: bold, unapologetic numeral display for price/data** (IKEA's pricing philosophy — "no apology about price," referenced in copywriting.md's methodology credit)
**Why it works:** hedging or shrinking a number visually signals the brand itself doubts the value — confident numeral display signals the opposite.
**Tuba application:** visual-system.md §2.3's Display-weight numeral treatment for stat blocks and pricing (design-components.md §3.1, §8) directly reapplies this mechanism.

## 5. Motion Patterns

**Pattern: platform-native interactive format over repurposed banner ads** (Bayut's Snapchat AR Lens — 13-53% lower CPM, 18% higher CTR, 6.5x earned reach uplift, competitor-analysis.md §6)
**Why it works:** an ad built *for* the platform's native interaction model (AR, in Snapchat's case) doesn't compete with organic content for attention the way a repurposed static ad does — it *is* the kind of content the platform rewards.
**Tuba application:** social-media-playbook.md §4 already mandates at least one custom AR Lens per major seasonal campaign, built on this exact mechanism — with Tuba's own concept (e.g., a "visualize your street" mechanic tied to the Home+Place icon pairing, iconography.md §4), not a copy of Bayut's execution.

## 6. Offer Patterns

**Pattern: financing-term incentive over price discount** (category-wide Ramadan convention — competitor-analysis.md §10)
**Why it works:** a price cut permanently recalibrates perceived value downward; a financing-term change (deferred payment, waived fee) reduces friction without touching the anchor price — the same psychological mechanism as behavioral economics' "present bias" (market-psychology.md §11).
**Tuba application:** campaign-playbook.md §1.3 codifies this as a hard rule, not just an observed pattern.

## 7. Structure Patterns

**Pattern: bold, earned-media-generating physical activation** (Aqar's National Day airplane flyover — first-mover claim, trade press pickup, competitor-analysis.md §6)
**Why it works:** a genuinely novel, real-world action generates press coverage and organic sharing that paid media cannot buy at the same credibility level — it works because it's real and rare, not because it's expensive.
**Tuba application:** advertising-playbook.md §3 flags Awareness/Brand campaigns as the one campaign type warranting dedicated PR/earned-media planning — the mechanism (genuine novelty, not just budget) is what should be replicated, in a concept that's authentically Tuba's own (e.g., tied to the "a place in the world" brand promise rather than a generic patriotic stunt).

## 8. Trust Patterns

**Pattern: minimal, regulator-sanctioned compliance display** (REGA's license-number-plus-QR allowance — competitor-analysis.md §11)
**Why it works:** removes the false choice between "legally compliant" and "well-designed" — proves compliance can be elegant.
**Tuba application:** the entire design-components.md §2.1 FAL badge spec is built directly on this mechanism.

**Pattern: government-adjacent process-transparency language** (Sakani's "register → verify → choose → sign" funnel — competitor-analysis.md §6)
**Why it works:** borrowing the *structure* of government trust communication (numbered, plain, procedural) lends commercial platforms some of that trust-by-association without needing to claim government status.
**Tuba application:** content-system.md §2.2's process infographics and educational content structure should mirror this numbered-step clarity.

## 9. Luxury Patterns

**Pattern: global-prestige licensing entry (Sotheby's, Christie's x Wasalt — competitor-analysis.md §1, §6)**
**Why it works:** borrowed international prestige provides instant credibility in a segment where local brand history may be thin.
**Why Tuba should NOT copy this mechanism directly:** Tuba's luxury credibility should come from property specificity and platform craft (advertising-dna.md §10, luxury-framework.md), not borrowed international branding — this is a pattern to understand and deliberately *not* replicate, since it would contradict the "homegrown, indigenous voice" differentiation identified in competitor-analysis.md §11.

## 10. Urgency Patterns (extracted with the strongest guardrail in this document)

**Pattern observed generally in the category:** discount-license-gated retail urgency (Ministry of Commerce formal discount licensing periods — competitor-analysis.md §10)
**Why it works when done honestly:** a *real*, externally-verifiable constraint (a licensed promotional period, an actual construction handover date) gives urgency copy real teeth without manufacturing pressure.
**Tuba application:** psychological-triggers.md §4's hard rule — only ever use urgency tied to a real, checkable deadline; this pattern is extracted specifically to reinforce why the guardrail exists, not to license more aggressive urgency tactics.

## Best Practices
- Every new pattern added to this file must include a "why it works" mechanism explanation and a distinct "Tuba application" — an entry with only a description of the original ad is incomplete
- Cross-check every extracted pattern against [advertising-dna.md §5](../advertising-system/advertising-dna.md)'s forbidden territory before adding a "Tuba application" — some patterns (like the Urgency one above) are worth understanding specifically *because* they show where the line is

## Common Mistakes
- Treating a "swipe" as license to reuse specific words, colors, or visual compositions from the original source — only the underlying mechanism transfers, per this document's governing rule
- Adding an entry without a clearly differentiated Tuba-specific execution — see the Luxury Patterns entry (§9) for how to handle a pattern that shouldn't be directly replicated at all

## Future Expansion Notes
- New patterns should be sourced from ongoing competitor-watch observations ([brand-memory/competitor-watch.md](../brand-memory/competitor-watch.md)) — promote a pattern from that living log into this static file once its mechanism is clearly understood and a genuine Tuba application has been identified
