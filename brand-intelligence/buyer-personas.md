# Buyer Personas — Tuba

> **Layer:** Brand Intelligence (static)
> **Purpose:** deep qualitative profiles of Tuba's buyer segments — goes beyond the quick-reference rows in [messaging-framework.md](../advertising-system/messaging-framework.md) (which covers Pain points/Desired outcome/Core message/Proof/CTA/Emotional angle for campaign briefing) to add Questions, Objections, Buying journey, and Content/Visual preferences needed for deeper creative and product decisions.
> **Owner:** Marketing Strategy lead, updated with Product/Research input
> **Review frequency:** annually, or when real user research becomes available to replace/validate these working profiles
> **Related documents:** [project-analysis.md §8](../knowledge-base/project-analysis.md), [messaging-framework.md](../advertising-system/messaging-framework.md), [market-psychology.md](market-psychology.md), [customer-journey.md](customer-journey.md)

**Note on origin:** these profiles are working hypotheses built from platform structure, market context, and category research (project-analysis.md §8) — not yet validated by formal user interviews. Treat them as the current best model, and update this document (not just brand-memory) once real research data exists to confirm or revise them.

## 1. First-Time Buyer

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | Own instead of rent; build equity; establish permanence for a growing or planned family |
| **Fears** | Making an irreversible mistake; being taken advantage of due to inexperience; missing a legal/financial requirement |
| **Pain points** | Doesn't know what questions to ask; overwhelmed by financing options; can't tell a good deal from a bad one |
| **Questions** | "How much do I actually need saved?" "What if something's wrong with the property that I can't see?" "How long does this whole process take?" |
| **Objections** | "I don't think I'm ready yet" / "This feels too big to trust to an app" |
| **Emotional triggers** | Relief, Confidence — see emotional-keywords.md §1, §3 |
| **Buying journey** | Long consideration phase, heavy research/comparison, family consultation before commitment (market-psychology.md §6) |
| **Content preferences** | Step-by-step educational content, myth-vs-fact, plain-language process explainers |
| **Visual preferences** | Warm, real (not staged) photography; simple, uncluttered layouts; visible trust badges |
| **Best CTA** | "Understand the process" → "Request your property" |

## 2. Luxury Buyer

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | A property that reflects personal taste and achievement; privacy; a legacy asset |
| **Fears** | Being marketed to like a mass-market customer; privacy exposure; a property that isn't genuinely distinctive |
| **Pain points** | Generic "luxury" marketing that all looks the same; feeling rushed or sold to |
| **Questions** | "What makes this property actually different?" "How discreet can this process be?" |
| **Objections** | "I'd rather go through someone I already know" |
| **Emotional triggers** | Quiet pride, restrained specificity (luxury-framework.md) |
| **Buying journey** | Relationship-first, longer and more private; often initiated through a trusted referral or agent relationship rather than broad discovery |
| **Content preferences** | Curated, low-volume, high-craft content; editorial photography; specific property storytelling (storytelling-framework.md) |
| **Visual preferences** | Restraint, negative space, premium (not flashy) photographic treatment (visual-decision-tree.md §1) |
| **Best CTA** | "Talk to a guide" (never a broad "Request now") |

## 3. Investor

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | Reliable yield/appreciation; portfolio diversification; a defensible, data-backed decision |
| **Fears** | A bad bet dressed up in marketing language; missing real market risk |
| **Pain points** | Distrusts adjective-heavy claims; needs comparable data, not just one listing's story |
| **Questions** | "What's the actual yield?" "How does this district compare to others?" "What's the exit liquidity like?" |
| **Objections** | "The numbers don't add up the way you're presenting them" |
| **Emotional triggers** | Confidence, via data (copywriting.md §5.7) |
| **Buying journey** | Research-heavy, comparison-driven, often less emotionally reactive to photography than to numbers |
| **Content preferences** | Market reports, district comparisons, "دليل طوبة" recurring content (content-system.md §5) |
| **Visual preferences** | Data visualization, stat blocks (design-components.md §3.1), minimal decorative photography |
| **Best CTA** | Link to full market report → "Understand the process" |

## 4. Family Buyer

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | A home that fits current and near-future family needs (space, schools, safety) |
| **Fears** | Choosing a location/property that doesn't work for the family long-term |
| **Pain points** | Balancing multiple people's priorities in one decision; time pressure around school calendars |
| **Questions** | "What schools are nearby?" "Is this a family-friendly neighborhood?" "Is there room to grow?" |
| **Objections** | "We need to agree as a family before moving forward" |
| **Emotional triggers** | Belonging, Warmth |
| **Buying journey** | Multi-decision-maker, benefits from shareable/comparison-friendly content (market-psychology.md §6) |
| **Content preferences** | Neighborhood/lifestyle content, school-proximity information, family-oriented storytelling |
| **Visual preferences** | Real family moments (not staged), Place & Lifestyle imagery (iconography.md §4) |
| **Best CTA** | "Request your property" |

## 5. Young Professional

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | Independence, a property near work, a first real step into stability or investment |
| **Fears** | Overcommitting financially too early; making a decision alone without family guidance |
| **Pain points** | Limited experience with the process; time-constrained (working full-time) |
| **Questions** | "Is this a smart financial move at my stage?" "How fast can I actually move in?" |
| **Objections** | "I'm not sure I want to be tied down to one place yet" |
| **Emotional triggers** | Confidence, Relief (speed) |
| **Buying journey** | Digital-first, comfortable with app-based process, values speed and clarity |
| **Content preferences** | Short-form educational content, TikTok/Instagram-native (social-media-playbook.md §2-3) |
| **Visual preferences** | Modern, clean, fast-paced video content |
| **Best CTA** | "Request your property" / "Understand the process" |

## 6. Retiree

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | Downsizing, comfort, proximity to family or healthcare, a low-maintenance property |
| **Fears** | Technology/process complexity; being rushed by unfamiliar digital tools |
| **Pain points** | Prefers human contact over pure self-serve; may need more explanation of digital steps |
| **Questions** | "Can I just talk to someone directly?" "Is this going to be complicated to manage?" |
| **Objections** | "I'd rather use an agent I already trust" |
| **Emotional triggers** | Relief, Warmth, human reassurance |
| **Buying journey** | Prefers phone/human contact at key decision points, slower and more deliberate |
| **Content preferences** | Clear, larger-format, low-jargon content; less reliance on short-form social |
| **Visual preferences** | Calm, uncluttered, high-legibility layouts |
| **Best CTA** | "Talk to a guide" |

## 7. International Buyer

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | Investment or relocation entry into the Saudi market, often tied to Vision 2030-era opportunity |
| **Fears** | Regulatory unfamiliarity (foreign ownership rules), language/process barriers |
| **Pain points** | Needs English-language clarity without losing local credibility; unfamiliar with Saudi-specific regulatory terms |
| **Questions** | "Can I legally own this as a foreigner?" "How does financing work for someone outside the country?" |
| **Objections** | "I don't know if I can trust a platform I can't verify from abroad" |
| **Emotional triggers** | Confidence, Trust (heavy Reassure-stage need) |
| **Buying journey** | Research-heavy, often starts months before serious engagement, values regulatory clarity above emotional appeal |
| **Content preferences** | English-language investment/regulatory explainers (a genuine, evidenced white-space opportunity — competitor-analysis.md §2's English-investment-underinvestment finding) |
| **Visual preferences** | Professional, data-forward, international-standard presentation |
| **Best CTA** | "Understand the process" → direct agent contact |

## Best Practices
- Always name the specific persona a piece of content is for (creative-brief-template.md) — "buyers" alone is not specific enough
- Cross-check any new buyer-facing campaign against this file's Objections column — every campaign's Reassure stage should answer at least one real listed objection

## Common Mistakes
- Writing generic content that tries to speak to all seven personas at once — dilutes relevance for everyone
- Assuming International Buyer needs are served by simply translating consumer Arabic content into English (see prompt-patterns.md §4 — same rule applies in reverse)
