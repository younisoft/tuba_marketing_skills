# Storytelling Framework — Tuba

> **Layer:** Brand Intelligence (static)
> **Purpose:** nine story models for the recurring narrative situations Tuba content needs to tell, all built on the same underlying structure — the customer as hero, Tuba as guide (advertising-dna.md Principle 1), and the Guidance Framework (copywriting.md §1) as the narrative spine.
> **Owner:** Content lead
> **Review frequency:** semi-annually
> **Related documents:** [copywriting.md §1](../advertising-system/copywriting.md), [content-system.md](../advertising-system/content-system.md), [visual-metaphors.md](visual-metaphors.md)

**The universal story shape underneath all nine models:** a real person with a real want (Hook) faces a real obstacle (Recognize) → receives guidance (Guide) → takes a clear action (Solve) → has their remaining doubt addressed (Reassure) → reaches an outcome (Decide). Every model below is this shape, specialized by subject.

---

## 1. Property Storytelling

**Shape:** the property as the setting for a life, not the subject of the story.
**Structure:** open on the life-context (who lives here, what happens here) → reveal specs as supporting detail, not the headline → close on the feeling of arrival.
**Full formula:** copywriting.md §5.8.
**Example beat:** "Built for slow mornings" → 3BD/2BA/180m² → "the kind of quiet where you can hear the AC hum from the next room" (prompt-patterns.md §5).

## 2. Family Storytelling

**Shape:** the household as a small ensemble cast, the home as the place their individual stories intersect.
**Structure:** show more than one family member's specific need being met by the same property (a child's school proximity + a parent's home-office corner) — reinforces messaging-framework.md §8's multi-decision-maker reality.
**Best format:** carousel or Reel — one slide/beat per family member's need.

## 3. Investment Storytelling

**Shape:** data as the plot, feeling as the resolution (inverting the usual order — copywriting.md §5.7).
**Structure:** open on a real, sourced number → walk through what produced it (district growth factors) → close on the reader's specific decision-making confidence, not a lifestyle image.
**Best format:** market-insight content, "دليل طوبة" (content-system.md §5).

## 4. Success Stories

**Shape:** a real customer's actual before/after, told largely in their own words.
**Structure:** brief context → the moment of relief or decision → the outcome — copywriting.md §4.5's structure, expanded.
**Non-negotiable rule:** must be real, attributed, and accurate — never composite or fabricated (advertising-dna.md §5, psychological-triggers.md §2).

## 5. Transformation Stories

**Shape:** a visible before/after — a property renovated, a family's living situation genuinely changed, a first-time buyer's confidence shift.
**Structure:** establish the "before" state honestly (not exaggerated for contrast) → show the specific intervention/guidance → reveal the "after" state.
**Best format:** before/after visual content (content-system.md §2.1).
**Guardrail:** the "before" state must be real and not artificially worsened for dramatic effect — exaggerating a starting problem to inflate the transformation is a subtle form of the fabrication this system prohibits.

## 6. Customer Journey Stories

**Shape:** a linear walkthrough of one real customer's path through the six stages in [customer-journey.md](customer-journey.md).
**Structure:** stage-by-stage narrative — what triggered their search (decision-triggers.md), what almost stopped them (decision-triggers.md §4), what resolved it, what the outcome felt like.
**Best format:** long-form (YouTube, blog) — this model needs more space than a single social caption to land.

## 7. Neighborhood Stories

**Shape:** the place itself as the protagonist — a district's character, rhythm, and specific appeal, independent of any single listing.
**Structure:** sensory, specific detail (a street, a landmark, a local rhythm) rather than generic "great location" claims — pairs directly with the Place & Lifestyle pillar and desert/lifestyle iconography (iconography.md §4, visual-metaphors.md).
**Best format:** the "حي الأسبوع" (Neighborhood of the Week) recurring series (content-system.md §2.4, social-media-playbook.md §8).

## 8. Developer Stories

**Shape:** the project's specific reason for being — what problem or opportunity it was designed around — told in professional register (copywriting.md §5.2).
**Structure:** the developer's intent → the specific design/planning decisions that follow from it → the buyer outcome those decisions produce.
**Best format:** partnership-launch content (campaign-playbook.md §2.3), avoiding purely clinical masterplan-render storytelling per the NHC reference case (competitor-analysis.md §2).

## 9. Educational Stories

**Shape:** a common misconception or fear, narratively resolved rather than just listed as a fact.
**Structure:** state the misconception as a real, relatable worry ("I thought I needed 30% down") → walk through the actual reality → close with the reader's new, correct understanding.
**Best format:** Myth vs. Fact (content-system.md §2.1), carousel or short video.

## Cross-Model Rules

1. **The customer (or a real, specific stand-in) is always the protagonist.** Tuba is the mentor/guide character, structurally — never the hero of its own story (advertising-dna.md Principle 1, and the IKEA-methodology "customer as hero" pattern it was adapted from — copywriting.md's methodology credit).
2. **Every story must be traceable to something real** — a real property, a real data point, a real (attributed) customer, or an honestly-representative composite *explicitly labeled as illustrative* if no real example exists yet. Never present an invented story as a real one.
3. **Specificity is the difference between a story and a claim** — "a family found their street in three weeks" is a claim; "the Al-Otaibi family found their street in Al Yasmin within three weeks of their first request" (real, attributed) is a story.

## Best Practices
- Match story model to content pillar (content-system.md §1) before writing — a Property Showcase asset should use Model 1, not Model 4
- Keep composite/illustrative examples (when no real story exists yet) explicitly labeled as such — never blur the line with real customer stories

## Common Mistakes
- Writing a "success story" that's actually a generic feature list wearing a narrative structure
- Over-dramatizing a Transformation story's "before" state (see §5 guardrail)
