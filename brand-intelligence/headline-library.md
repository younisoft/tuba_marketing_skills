# Headline Library — Tuba

> **Layer:** Brand Intelligence (static)
> **Purpose:** the permanent, categorized headline **formula** library — reusable structures with the psychology behind them, not one-off finished headlines to copy-paste. Builds on the three core formulas in [copywriting.md §3](../advertising-system/copywriting.md); this document expands formula coverage by content category and format context.
> **Owner:** Copy lead
> **Review frequency:** semi-annually, validated against real performance data once available
> **Related documents:** [copywriting.md](../advertising-system/copywriting.md), [content-hooks.md](content-hooks.md), [emotional-keywords.md](emotional-keywords.md), [brand-memory/headline-performance.md](../brand-memory/headline-performance.md)

**How to use this library:** every formula below is a fill-in-the-blank structure with one worked Arabic and English example. Never ship the worked example verbatim on a live campaign — generate a fresh, situation-specific instance of the formula. Run every output through [prompt-patterns.md §1](../advertising-system/prompt-patterns.md) before finalizing.

## 1. Educational

**Formula: "What [audience] should know about [topic]"**
Why it works: signals value before commitment, matches the Guide archetype (advertising-dna.md §6) — informs rather than sells.
> EN: "What every first-time buyer should know about FAL licensing" · AR: "ما يجب أن يعرفه كل مشترٍ لأول مرة عن رخصة فال"

**Formula: "The [number]-step guide to [outcome]"**
Why it works: reduces perceived complexity to a countable, finishable task — directly serves the Relief emotion (emotional-keywords.md §1).
> EN: "The 4-step guide to your first property request" · AR: "٤ خطوات لطلب عقارك الأول"

## 2. Luxury

**Formula: one specific, unrepeatable detail, stated plainly**
Why it works: luxury copy earns its register through restraint and specificity, never adjectives (copywriting.md §5.3, luxury-framework.md).
> EN: "A view of the Red Sea that doesn't repeat anywhere else in the city." · AR: "إطلالة على البحر الأحمر لا تتكرر في أي مكان آخر بالمدينة."

## 3. Investment

**Formula: "[Concrete number]. [What it means for the reader]."**
Why it works: data-first register this audience trusts (copywriting.md §5.7); the second sentence translates the number into a felt outcome without abandoning rigor.
> EN: "14% average 3-year appreciation. The kind of number that lets you sleep at night." · AR: "١٤٪ متوسط ارتفاع القيمة خلال ٣ سنوات. رقم يريح باله."

## 4. Urgency (real deadlines only — psychological-triggers.md §4)

**Formula: "[Real external deadline] — here's what that actually means for you"**
Why it works: urgency tied to a verifiable, external fact reads as helpful information, not manufactured pressure.
> EN: "School starts in 6 weeks — here's a realistic rental timeline" · AR: "الدراسة تبدأ خلال ٦ أسابيع — إليك جدولاً واقعياً للإيجار"

## 5. Authority

**Formula: "[Specific credential/mechanism], not just our word for it"**
Why it works: names the actual authority source rather than asserting trustworthiness as an adjective (prompt-patterns.md §3).
> EN: "REGA-licensed, verifiable in seconds — not just our word for it" · AR: "مرخّص من الهيئة العامة للعقار، وتقدر تتحقق بنفسك"

## 6. Emotional

**Formula: "Ever imagined [specific, sensory future moment]?"** (The Open Dream — copywriting.md §3)
Why it works: activates a real, specific image rather than an abstract feeling-word (prompt-patterns.md §5).
> EN: "Ever imagined the street your kids grow up on?" · AR: "هل تخيلت يوماً الشارع الذي سيكبر فيه أطفالك؟"

## 7. Property (listing-specific)

**Formula: "[Life-context opener], [one distinguishing spec]"** (copywriting.md §5.8)
Why it works: opens with the life the property enables, not the spec sheet, then earns the spec with one real detail.
> EN: "Built for slow mornings — north-facing balcony that catches the evening light" · AR: "صُمّمت لصباحات هادئة — شرفة شمالية تحتضن ضوء المساء"

## 8. Community

**Formula: "[Real outcome], told by the person who lived it"**
Why it works: real social proof, framed honestly (psychological-triggers.md §2) — never a fabricated quote.
> EN: "How one family found their street in three weeks" (paired with a real, attributed story) · AR: "كيف وجدت عائلة شارعها خلال ثلاثة أسابيع"

## 9. Trust

**Formula: "[Specific mechanism], verify it yourself"**
Why it works: invites active verification instead of asking for passive belief — the strongest trust pattern identified in prompt-patterns.md §3.
> EN: "Every listing carries a FAL number — verify it yourself" · AR: "كل إعلان يحمل رقم رخصة فال — تحقق بنفسك"

## 10. Reels / Short-form Video

**Formula: text-overlay fragment, ≤5 words, paired with spoken hook (social-media-playbook.md §3)**
Why it works: silent-scroll viewers need the text alone to work; sound-on viewers get reinforcement, not redundancy.
> EN: "3 mistakes first-time buyers make" · AR: "٣ أخطاء يقع فيها المشتري الأول"

## 11. Ads (Performance)

**Formula: [offer/spec] + [location] + [one differentiator]** (copywriting.md §6 — offer-forward, compressed)
Why it works: performance contexts reward immediate clarity over scene-setting.
> EN: "3BR villa, Al Nakheel — FAL-verified, ready now" · AR: "فيلا ٣ غرف، النخيل — موثقة برخصة فال، جاهزة الآن"

## 12. Landing Pages

**Formula: brand-promise-adjacent headline + supporting clarity line** (visual-system.md §9's hero-section logic)
Why it works: landing pages get one shot at framing before the visitor scans past — lead with the promise, clarify immediately.
> EN: "You will never have to figure this out alone. Request your property, free, in two minutes." · AR: "لن تحتاج لمواجهة الأمر وحدك. اطلب عقارك، مجاناً، خلال دقيقتين."

## 13. Email

**Formula: Hook-formula subject line, ≤8 words (copywriting.md §5.11)**
Why it works: subject lines compete for a half-second decision — a full sentence loses to a sharp fragment.
> EN: "3 new matches in Al Nakheel" · AR: "٣ عقارات جديدة تطابق طلبك في النخيل"

## 14. Push Notifications

**Formula: specific, personalized, single-fact (copywriting.md §5.12)**
Why it works: generic notifications get muted; specific ones read as genuinely useful.
> EN: "Your saved search just matched a new listing" · AR: "بحثك المحفوظ طابق إعلاناً جديداً الآن"

## Formula Selection Guide

| If the goal is... | Use category |
|---|---|
| Building long-term trust and recall | Educational, Authority, Trust |
| Driving an immediate action | Ads, Landing Pages, Push Notifications |
| Emotional connection/brand-building | Emotional, Community |
| Investor/B2B credibility | Investment |
| Luxury-segment specificity | Luxury |

## Best Practices
- Never use more than one formula category per single asset (copywriting.md §3's "no more than 2 formulas layered" rule)
- Always regenerate a fresh instance of a formula for the actual campaign context — the worked examples above exist to teach the pattern, not to be reused verbatim

## Common Mistakes
- Treating "Urgency" as licence to fabricate deadlines — see the hard guardrail in psychological-triggers.md §4
- Using a Luxury-category headline outside an explicit luxury campaign brief (campaign-playbook.md §2.6)
