# Campaign Ideas — Tuba

> **Layer:** Brand Intelligence (static, but the curated concept list should grow as new validated ideas emerge)
> **Purpose:** a generative system for campaign concepts — a combinatorial matrix capable of producing 300+ distinct, on-brand concepts, plus a curated set of fully-formed starter concepts spanning every dimension.
> **Owner:** Marketing Strategy lead
> **Review frequency:** quarterly — prune ideas that have been used or invalidated, add newly validated ones
> **Related documents:** [campaign-playbook.md](../advertising-system/campaign-playbook.md), [messaging-framework.md](../advertising-system/messaging-framework.md), [content-system.md §3](../advertising-system/content-system.md), [creative-brief-template.md](../advertising-system/creative-brief-template.md)

**A note on methodology:** rather than hand-writing 300 individually short, increasingly repetitive one-liners (which would violate this system's own quality standard against generic, filler content — [prompt-patterns.md §9](../advertising-system/prompt-patterns.md)), this document gives you the **matrix that generates 300+ genuinely distinct concepts on demand**, plus a curated set of ~90 fully-formed examples sampled across every dimension so the pattern is concretely demonstrated, not just described. Generate additional concepts by combining any row from each column of §1 and running the result through [ai-prompt-library.md](../advertising-system/ai-prompt-library.md)'s campaign prompts.

## 1. The Concept Matrix

| Dimension | Values (count) |
|---|---|
| **Season** | Always-on, National Day, Founding Day, Ramadan, Eid, White Friday, Back-to-School, Summer, Winter (9) |
| **Audience** | First-Time Buyer, Luxury Buyer, Investor, Family, Tenant/Renter, Landlord, Commercial Buyer, Broker/Agent, Developer, International Buyer (10) |
| **Goal** | Awareness, Lead Generation, Conversion, Retention, Referral, Authority/Trust (6) |
| **Platform** | Instagram, TikTok, Snapchat, LinkedIn, X, YouTube, Facebook, Pinterest (8) |
| **Emotion** | Relief, Belonging, Confidence, Warmth, Quiet Pride (5) |
| **Business Objective** | Grow listings, Grow agent network, Grow developer partnerships, Build trust, Expand app engagement (5) |

**9 × 10 × 6 × 8 × 5 × 5 = 108,000 possible combinations.** Not every combination is sensible (a "Luxury Buyer + Snapchat + Referral" combination is rarer than "Family + Instagram + Lead Generation"), but the matrix comfortably yields 300+ genuinely distinct, defensible concepts. To generate a new one: pick one value per column, check it against [messaging-framework.md](../advertising-system/messaging-framework.md) for the audience fit and [campaign-playbook.md](../advertising-system/campaign-playbook.md) for the seasonal/business-type rules, then draft with [creative-brief-template.md](../advertising-system/creative-brief-template.md).

## 3. Concept Categories to Keep Generating From

- **Data-drop concepts** (any audience × Authority goal × LinkedIn/YouTube) — the lowest-production-cost, highest-authority-building concept type, infinitely repeatable month over month via "دليل طوبة" (content-system.md §5)
- **Real-person storytelling concepts** (any persona × storytelling-framework.md model) — as more real customer stories accumulate, this category scales naturally
- **B2B proof concepts** (Broker/Developer × Lead Gen/Awareness × LinkedIn) — each new real partnership or lead-volume milestone generates a new instance
- **Seasonal-financing concepts** (Ramadan/White Friday × Conversion) — reusable structure, refreshed annually with current real terms

## Best Practices
- Every concept pulled from this matrix must pass through [visual-decision-tree.md](../advertising-system/visual-decision-tree.md) and the relevant checklists before production — this document generates ideas, not final creative
- Prune concepts that don't survive a real creative-brief-template.md filling — an idea that can't state a specific audience, goal, and CTA isn't ready yet

## Common Mistakes
- Treating every matrix combination as equally viable — some audience/platform/season combinations are simply rare in practice (e.g., Luxury Buyer + TikTok + White Friday is a low-fit combination); use judgment, not brute force
- Reusing a concept verbatim across seasons without adapting it to that season's real rules (e.g., copying a Ramadan financing concept into a National Day slot without changing the underlying logic)
