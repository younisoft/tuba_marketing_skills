## 1. Luxury Psychology (Saudi-specific)

Per market-psychology.md §8-9: Saudi luxury real estate perception is increasingly defined by **privacy, land/space, and personalization** rather than brand-name opulence alone. Buyers in this segment do not want to be *told* a property signals status — they want the property's specificity to make that case implicitly. This is the foundational reason Tuba's luxury register is built on restraint, not display.

## 2. When Luxury Register Applies (and when it doesn't)

| Context | Register |
|---|---|
| A property explicitly marketed/priced in the luxury segment | Full luxury register |
| A boutique/luxury broker partnership (broker-personas.md §3) | Full luxury register |
| Standard property showcase, even a high-value one not positioned as "luxury" | Standard accessible-premium register (advertising-dna.md §10) |
| Mass-market awareness/brand campaigns | Never luxury register — this is Tuba's default-accessible positioning, not luxury-first |

**Rule:** luxury register is a deliberate, briefed choice (creative-brief-template.md), never a default upgrade applied to "make something feel nicer."

## 3. The Core Luxury Principle: Show, Don't Label

Forbidden words: "luxurious," "exclusive," "opulent," "premium" (as a label), "the finest" (copywriting.md §8.3). Replace every luxury adjective with one concrete, unrepeatable fact about the specific property (prompt-patterns.md §2).

| Instead of... | Do this |
|---|---|
| "A luxurious villa with stunning views" | "Four bedrooms. One view of the Red Sea that doesn't repeat anywhere else in the city." |
| "Exclusive, high-end finishes" | Name the actual specific material/craft detail |

## 4. Luxury Audience Specifics

See [buyer-personas.md §2](buyer-personas.md) for the full profile. Key points relevant specifically to framework application:
- Relationship-first buying journey — luxury content should rarely use a broad "Request now" CTA; use "Talk to a guide" (cta-library.md §14)
- Discretion is a real, stated value, not a marketing nicety — curated/limited distribution over broad targeting (campaign-playbook.md §2.6)
- Referral and existing-relationship discovery is common — this segment rewards a strong broker-partnership program (broker-personas.md §3) as much as or more than direct consumer advertising

## 5. Luxury Visual Rules (summary — full spec: visual-decision-tree.md §1)

- More negative space, editorial-quality photography (the one context where higher production polish is warranted — visual-system.md §6.1)
- Standard color hierarchy still applies — luxury does not get a separate palette (advertising-dna.md §9's consistency rule)
- Secondary/outline CTA button style preferred over a bold filled CTA — quieter confidence

## 6. Luxury Proof Points (what replaces "trust me, it's luxury")

- Specific, real property details (§3)
- Curated distribution/access mechanics (psychological-triggers.md §5 — exclusivity shown through actual mechanism, not just claimed)
- Relationship quality and discretion, demonstrated through process, not asserted through adjectives

## 7. Luxury Pricing Psychology

Per market-psychology.md's general pricing principles applied to this segment: luxury pricing should be presented with the same "no apology, no hedging" confidence as standard pricing (swipe-file.md §4's IKEA-derived pricing pattern) — luxury buyers are not more price-sensitive to confident presentation, they're *more* sensitive to any sign of oversell or uncertainty.

## 8. What Luxury Register Should NEVER Do

- Never borrow international prestige branding (Sotheby's/Christie's-style licensing) as Tuba's luxury credibility source — Tuba's luxury credibility comes from property specificity and platform craft, a deliberate differentiation choice (competitor-differentiation.md §4)
- Never stack luxury adjectives for emphasis ("truly luxurious," "utterly exclusive") — see prompt-patterns.md §9's hedge-stacking rule
- Never apply status-signaling framing outside this explicitly briefed segment (advertising-dna.md §5, §10)

## Best Practices
- Every luxury asset should pass a specific test: could this exact sentence/image describe a different, generic luxury property? If yes, it's not specific enough yet (§3)
- Route every luxury campaign through visual-decision-tree.md §1 before production

## Common Mistakes
- Applying luxury adjectives to a standard-segment property "to make it sound nicer" — dilutes the luxury register's meaning for when it's actually needed
- Using broad-reach performance-ad tactics (visual-system.md §9.6) for luxury-segment content — mismatched to the relationship-first buying journey (§4)
