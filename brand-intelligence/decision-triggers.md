# Decision Triggers — What Actually Moves a Saudi Real Estate Decision Forward

> **Layer:** Brand Intelligence (static)
> **Purpose:** catalog the specific moments and inputs that push a prospect from one stage to the next — the practical, campaign-usable companion to [market-psychology.md](market-psychology.md)'s broader psychology.
> **Owner:** Marketing Strategy lead
> **Review frequency:** annually
> **Related documents:** [customer-journey.md](customer-journey.md), [psychological-triggers.md](../advertising-system/psychological-triggers.md), [copywriting.md §1](../advertising-system/copywriting.md)

## 1. Trigger Categories

| Category | Definition | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **External life-event triggers** | Something happening outside the platform that starts the search | New job/relocation, marriage, child arriving, lease ending |
| **Financial-readiness triggers** | A change in financial capacity or awareness | Saved enough for a down payment, financing pre-approval, Sakani eligibility confirmed |
| **Social triggers** | Influence from other people | A friend/family member just bought, a family conversation about moving |
| **Content/discovery triggers** | Something seen that starts or accelerates the process | A property that "feels right" on social, a market-insight stat that reframes timing |
| **Platform-experience triggers** | Something the platform itself does that moves the decision | A saved-search match notification, a fast, clear agent response |

## 2. Triggers That Start a Search

- Lease renewal deadline approaching (Tenant/Renter — messaging-framework.md §5)
- School year approaching (Family — campaign-playbook.md §1.6)
- A "why are we still paying rent" household conversation (First-time buyer)
- A market-insight stat suggesting "now" is a good time (Investor)
- Job relocation confirmed (Tenant, Family)

## 3. Triggers That Accelerate Consideration

- A specific property that matches multiple stated criteria at once ("this is basically what we described")
- A district-level data point that reframes a location as smarter than assumed (Investor, Commercial)
- A friend/family member's positive experience specifically with *this* platform (referral trust transfer — market-psychology.md §7)
- Financing terms that make monthly cost concrete and comfortable (see the Sakani "بيت العمر" pattern — competitor-analysis.md §7)

## 4. Triggers That Cause Hesitation / Stall the Decision

| Hesitation trigger | Root cause | Reassure-stage response |
|---|---|---|
| "What if the listing isn't real?" | Fear of fraud | FAL badge, verification messaging (design-components.md §2.1) |
| "What if I'm missing something in the paperwork?" | Fear of an uninformed mistake | Plain-language process content (content-system.md pillar 1) |
| "What if the price isn't fair?" | Fear of overpaying | Market data, price-per-meter context (real-estate-terminology.md §4) |
| "I need to talk to my family first" | Genuine multi-decision-maker process (market-psychology.md §6) | Shareable, comparison-friendly content — not pressure to decide alone |
| "I'm not sure this is the right time" | Macro uncertainty or personal timing doubt | Educational/timing-context content, never manufactured urgency (psychological-triggers.md §4) |

## 5. Triggers That Convert Consideration Into Action

- A direct, human response from a licensed agent that answers the *specific* hesitation above, not a generic reply
- A clear, singular next step (one CTA, per copywriting.md §4) at the exact moment hesitation is resolved
- Confirmation that the practical process (financing, paperwork) is simpler than assumed

## 6. Triggers Specific to B2B Audiences

| Audience | Trigger |
|---|---|
| Broker | A visible, credible lead-quality signal (not just lead volume) from the platform |
| Developer | Evidence of qualified buyer intent data for their specific project type/location |

## 7. Mapping Triggers to Campaign Timing

Use this table when planning campaign-workflows.md §1 (Campaign Planning) — trigger category should inform *when* and *how* a campaign reaches its audience:

| Trigger type | Best campaign timing |
|---|---|
| External life-event (relocation, school year) | Always-on + seasonal peak (back-to-school — campaign-playbook.md §1.6) |
| Financial-readiness | Tied to financing-offer campaigns (Ramadan — campaign-playbook.md §1.3) |
| Social | Referral campaign structure (campaign-playbook.md §3.2) |
| Content/discovery | Always-on content system (content-system.md) — this is the slowest-burn, highest-compounding trigger category |
| Platform-experience | Product/retention-focused, not campaign-focused — a UX/notification quality issue, not a copy issue |

## Best Practices
- Identify which trigger category a campaign is targeting *before* writing copy — the Hook stage should name the actual trigger, not a generic one (e.g., "lease renewal coming up?" beats a generic "thinking about moving?")
- Hesitation triggers (§4) should map directly to the Reassure stage of any related campaign (copywriting.md §1)

## Common Mistakes
- Writing generic "ready to find your home?" hooks that don't name a real trigger — see prompt-patterns.md §1 on specificity
- Treating all hesitation as objection to overcome with pressure rather than information to provide (psychological-triggers.md §11 guardrail)
