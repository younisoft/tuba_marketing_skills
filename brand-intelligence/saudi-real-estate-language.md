# Saudi Real Estate Language — How Customers Actually Speak

> **Layer:** Brand Intelligence (static)
> **Purpose:** document the *conversational*, real-world language Saudi real estate customers use — as distinct from formal market/industry language ([market-language.md](market-language.md)) or technical definitions ([real-estate-terminology.md](real-estate-terminology.md)). This is the vocabulary Tuba's copy should sound like it's *listening to*, not lecturing at.
> **Owner:** Copy/Content lead
> **Review frequency:** annually — colloquial language shifts faster than formal terminology; check against real customer-service transcripts and social comments if available
> **Related documents:** [copywriting.md](../advertising-system/copywriting.md), [emotional-keywords.md](emotional-keywords.md), [market-psychology.md](market-psychology.md)

## 1. Common Everyday Expressions

| Expression (AR) | Meaning/context | Use in Tuba copy? |
|---|---|---|
| بيت العمر | "The home of a lifetime" — a legacy/milestone home | Yes, sparingly — powerful but overused across the category (competitor-analysis.md §7 notes it as a Sakani campaign anchor) |
| استقر | "Settle down/in" — the emotional endpoint of a search | Yes — pairs naturally with the Decide stage |
| قسط مريح | "Comfortable installment" — financing framed by monthly ease, not total price | Yes — matches the Ramadan financing-not-discount convention (campaign-playbook.md §1.3) |
| بيت العائلة | "The family home" | Yes, for family-persona content |
| أطمن على | "To feel reassured about" | Yes — direct match to the Reassure stage and the "relief" brand emotion |
| على قد الحال | "Within one's means/reasonable" | Use carefully — can undercut the "confident, non-apologetic" pricing tone if overused |

## 2. Preferred Terminology (customer-facing)

| Preferred | Avoid | Why |
|---|---|---|
| وسيط مرخّص (licensed agent/broker) | "وكيل" alone without licensing context | Names the trust mechanism, not just the role |
| اطلب عقارك (request your property) | ابحث لساعات (search for hours) | Matches Tuba's actual request-first mechanic (competitor-analysis.md §3) |
| تحقق بنفسك (verify it yourself) | ثق بنا فقط (just trust us) | Active verification language outperforms passive trust assertion — prompt-patterns.md §3 |
| دفعة أولى / تقسيط (down payment / installment) | "تمويل معقد" (complex financing) framing | Never make financing sound harder than it is |

## 3. Words to Avoid (colloquial register)

- Bureaucratic/legalistic phrasing dropped into casual contexts ("وفقاً للأنظمة المعمول بها") — fine in formal disclosures, wrong in a social caption
- Sales-pressure colloquialisms ("لا تفوت الفرصة", "العرض لفترة محدودة" without a real deadline) — forbidden per copywriting.md §8.3
- Overly Levantine/Egyptian dialect markers that read as imported media Arabic rather than Saudi/Gulf voice — see market-psychology.md §13-14 on cultural specificity

## 4. Professional Language (B2B/agent-facing)

More formal MSA, industry-aware vocabulary: صفقة (deal), عمولة (commission), قائمة حصرية (exclusive listing), شبكة عملاء (client network). Full register guidance: copywriting.md §5.2, §7.1.

## 5. Luxury Language

Restrained, specific, sensory rather than superlative — see [luxury-framework.md](luxury-framework.md) for the full model. Preferred colloquial luxury phrases: خصوصية تامة (complete privacy), إطلالة لا تتكرر (a view that doesn't repeat), تفاصيل مدروسة (considered details). Avoid: فاخر جداً, أرقى, الأفخم — label-words forbidden per copywriting.md §8.3.

## 6. Friendly / Warm Language

معاً (together), نساعدك (we help you), بيتك (your home), خطوة بخطوة (step by step) — the full preferred-vocabulary list lives in copywriting.md §8.1-8.2; this section adds the conversational register Saudis actually use in speech, not just brand copy: خلك مطمن (stay reassured, colloquial), يهمنا وضعك (your situation matters to us).

## 7. Trust Language (as customers say it, not as brands claim it)

Customers ask, in their own words: "هل هذا مرخّص؟" (Is this licensed?), "كيف أتأكد إن الإعلان حقيقي؟" (How do I make sure the ad is real?), "مين الوسيط ورا هذا؟" (Who's the agent behind this?). Tuba's copy should pre-answer these exact colloquial questions, not just state "we're verified" — this is the practical application of copywriting.md §5.6's specificity rule.

## 8. Broker Vocabulary

فال (FAL — the license, referenced by name in professional conversation, not spelled out formally every time), عرض حصري (exclusive listing), عميل جاد (a serious/qualified lead — the single most valued word in an agent's vocabulary; "جاد" signals lead-quality, directly relevant to broker-facing CTA language, cta-library.md).

## 9. Investment Vocabulary

عائد (return/yield), سعر المتر (price per square meter — the default Saudi real estate value unit, more commonly used in conversation than total price alone), نمو السوق (market growth), فرصة استثمارية حقيقية (a genuine, not hyped, investment opportunity — customers explicitly distinguish "حقيقية" from marketing hype in casual conversation, a strong signal for copywriting.md §5.7's data-first register).

## 10. Property Vocabulary (colloquial, not just technical)

صالة (living/sitting area, used more colloquially than "غرفة معيشة"), ملحق (an attached/separate unit, e.g., for extended family or staff — a genuinely common search criterion, see market-psychology.md §13), مدخل خاص (private entrance), حوش (a yard/courtyard, especially in traditional-style homes) — see real-estate-terminology.md for full technical definitions.

## 11. Regional Differences

| Region | Notable language pattern |
|---|---|
| Riyadh/Najd | More formal Najdi-inflected register even in casual contexts; "بيت" used broadly across house types |
| Hejaz (Jeddah/Makkah/Madinah) | Slightly more relaxed register, coastal/lifestyle vocabulary more natural ("كورنيش," "بحر" proximity as a stated criterion) |
| Eastern Province (Dammam/Khobar) | More bilingual code-switching in professional/investment contexts due to expat-heavy business environment |

**Application rule:** default brand voice stays in a neutral, broadly-understood Saudi register (copywriting.md §7.1); regional flavor is acceptable in hyper-local social content (a Jeddah-specific coastal campaign) but never required system-wide.

## 12. Modern Expressions

App-native vocabulary now in common use: إشعار (notification), تطابق (a match, in the request-first sense), حفظ البحث (saved search) — these are now understood as plainly as any older real estate term and should be used confidently, not over-explained.

## 13. Traditional Expressions

بيت العز (a home of dignity/pride), دار (an older, warmer synonym for "house," carries more heritage connotation than "منزل") — useful for heritage/National Day-adjacent content (campaign-playbook.md §1.1), used sparingly elsewhere to avoid sounding dated in everyday product copy.

## Best Practices
- When in doubt between a formal and colloquial term, default toward the colloquial-but-correct option — it's what customers actually search and say (market-psychology.md §15)
- Test new vocabulary choices by asking: "would this appear naturally in a WhatsApp message between two Saudi friends discussing a property?" (copywriting.md §5.13's register)

## Common Mistakes
- Treating "formal MSA" as automatically "more professional" — professionalism in this market comes from clarity and specificity, not formality (see market-language.md for where formal register *is* correct)
- Using regional dialect markers inconsistently within a single asset
