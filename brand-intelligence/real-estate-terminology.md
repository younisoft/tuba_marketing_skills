## 1. Regulatory & Compliance Terms

| Term (AR/EN) | Definition | Plain-language version for consumer copy |
|---|---|---|
| **REGA** (الهيئة العامة للعقار) | Saudi Arabia's Real Estate General Authority — the sector regulator | "The government body that regulates real estate in Saudi Arabia" |
| **FAL license** (رخصة فال) | The umbrella license system covering brokerage, marketing/advertising, property management, and related real estate activities | "The license every legitimate agent or ad must carry" |
| **Real Estate Advertisement License** (ترخيص إعلان عقاري) | A specific license required for advertising a property, distinct from the brokerage FAL license | "The license that makes a property ad legal to run" |
| **Ejar** (إيجار) | The government digital rental-contract registration platform | "The official system that registers rental contracts" |
| **Nafath** (نفاذ) | Saudi Arabia's national digital identity/SSO system, used to verify individuals online | "The national ID verification system" |

## 2. Property Type Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Villa (فيلا) | A standalone or semi-detached house, typically with a private yard/majlis |
| Apartment (شقة) | A unit within a multi-unit residential building |
| Duplex (دوبلكس) | A two-floor unit within a building |
| Off-plan (على الخارطة) | A property sold before or during construction, based on plans |
| Ready-built (جاهز) | A completed, move-in-ready property |
| Land/plot (أرض) | Undeveloped land sold for future construction or investment |
| Commercial (تجاري) | Non-residential property — retail, office, or mixed-use |

## 3. Transaction Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Down payment (الدفعة الأولى) | The upfront portion of a property's price paid before financing covers the rest |
| Installment (قسط) | A scheduled partial payment, typically monthly |
| Deed (صك) | The official ownership document for a property |
| Escrow (حساب ضمان) | A protected account holding funds until transaction conditions are met (especially relevant to off-plan purchases) |
| Handover (التسليم) | The point at which a completed property is transferred to the buyer |

## 4. Financial/Investment Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Yield (العائد) | The income return on a property relative to its value, usually expressed annually |
| Appreciation (ارتفاع القيمة) | The increase in a property's value over time |
| Price per square meter (سعر المتر) | The default Saudi real estate value benchmark — see saudi-real-estate-language.md §9 |
| Loan-to-value / financing ratio | The proportion of a property's price covered by financing vs. buyer's own funds |

## 5. Platform/Product Terms (Tuba-specific)

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Property request (طلب عقار) | Tuba's demand-first flow — a buyer/renter states criteria, agents respond directly |
| Market property request (تسويق طلب عقار) | The supply-side counterpart — matching a seller/agent to open requests |
| Verified listing | A listing that has passed Tuba's FAL/REGA compliance and identity checks |
| Saved search | A stored search that triggers notifications on new matches |

## 6. Spec/Layout Terms

بيت (house, general), صالة (living/sitting area), مجلس (majlis — a formal reception/sitting room, a distinct and culturally significant space from the general living area), ملحق (an attached separate unit), حوش (yard/courtyard), مدخل خاص (private entrance) — see saudi-real-estate-language.md §10 for colloquial usage notes on these same terms.

## Best Practices
- Every regulatory term used in consumer-facing content must be plain-language-defined on first use, per copywriting.md §5.4 — this glossary is the source of truth for what that plain-language version should say
- Keep this glossary as the single reference — don't let slightly different definitions drift into different documents across the system

## Common Mistakes
- Using "FAL" and "REGA" interchangeably — they are related but distinct (§1); conflating them in consumer copy causes confusion, not clarity
- Assuming a term is self-explanatory because the team uses it daily — always check against the "would a first-time buyer understand this cold" standard
