# Developer Experience

> **Part of:** [`../BRAND_BEHAVIOR_SYSTEM.md`](../BRAND_BEHAVIOR_SYSTEM.md)
> **Persona grounding:** [`../brand-intelligence/developer-personas.md`](../brand-intelligence/developer-personas.md). A developer partner (a real estate development company listing projects, tracking demand, and managing off-plan sales through Tuba) operates at institutional scale and a longer time horizon than any other persona in this system — behavior here has to hold up across a multi-year project lifecycle, not a single transaction.

---

## The Governing Shift: From Guide to Strategic Partner

The relationship with a developer is closer to a B2B partnership than a marketplace transaction. Tuba behaves like a partner with a stake in the project's success — surfacing real market signal, not just processing listings — never like a passive directory the developer pays to appear in.

## Data Behavior Is the Core of the Relationship

A developer's primary need from the platform is honest, real signal: genuine interest levels, accurate demand data by unit type and price band, and buyer feedback that's unfiltered rather than smoothed to look more favorable. Per `BEHAVIOR_PRINCIPLES.md` §8, any dashboard metric shown to a developer must be real and currently true — a demand figure that's stale or inflated to keep the relationship comfortable is a worse failure with this audience than almost anywhere else in the system, because real capital allocation decisions are made on it.

## Project Pages Behave Like Product Pages, Not Brochures

A project's presentation on the platform matches the same trust standard applied to individual listings — clear pricing, clear timeline, clear licensing status, no aspirational rendering presented without a completion-status label. `advertising-dna.md §5`'s ban on unverifiable superlatives applies with particular force to off-plan project marketing, a category with a real history of overpromising.

## Long Time Horizon, Long Memory

A developer's relationship with Tuba spans a project's full lifecycle — pre-launch, sales phase, handover, post-handover. The platform's behavior toward buyers of that project (communication quality, complaint handling, handover support) reflects directly on the developer's own reputation, so `SERVICE_PHILOSOPHY.md` and `TRUST_BEHAVIOR.md` are applied with awareness that a service failure here has a second victim beyond the immediate buyer.

## Communication Cadence Matches Institutional Pacing

Developer-facing updates (sales performance, buyer feedback themes, market positioning) are delivered on a predictable, useful cadence — not ad hoc or only when something's gone wrong. A partner who only hears from Tuba during a problem will reasonably start to wonder what else isn't being surfaced.

## Handover Behavior Is Treated as a Shared Milestone

The moment buyers receive keys to a developer's project is a `SUCCESS_MOMENTS.md` Tier 5 event for the buyer — and it is also the moment the developer's own reputation is most on the line. The platform's behavior here (accurate handover timelines communicated to both sides, clear escalation if the developer misses a date) protects both relationships simultaneously rather than treating them as separate concerns.

## What Developer Experience Must Never Do

- Never present projected or aspirational figures as confirmed data on a developer-facing dashboard.
- Never let a buyer-facing delay go uncommunicated to the developer partner until it becomes a public complaint.
- Never bundle unrelated developers' projects in comparative reporting without the specific developer's own performance clearly isolated and honestly presented.
