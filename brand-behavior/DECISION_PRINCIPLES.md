# Decision Principles

> **Part of:** [`../BRAND_BEHAVIOR_SYSTEM.md`](../BRAND_BEHAVIOR_SYSTEM.md)
> **What this document is for:** whenever two good product ideas conflict — growth versus trust, speed versus depth, automation versus human touch — someone has to decide, often without time to convene a brand review. These are the immutable tie-breakers. They don't make every decision easy, but they make every decision *consistent*, which is what actually protects the brand over hundreds of small calls nobody remembers making.

---

## 1. When Trust and Growth Conflict, Trust Wins

If a feature would grow engagement or conversion but requires a manufactured urgency device, a hidden fee, or an unverifiable claim, it does not ship in that form — full stop, no exception for "just this once, it's a small one." `advertising-dna.md §5`'s forbidden territory is not a suggestion that yields under revenue pressure; it is the actual floor.

## 2. When Speed and Honesty Conflict, Honesty Wins — But Speed Is Then the Next Priority

A faster answer that's wrong is worse than a slightly slower one that's right (`BEHAVIOR_PRINCIPLES.md` §2). But once honesty is secured, speed is never abandoned as a secondary concern — the team should still be actively working to make the honest answer arrive faster, not treating slowness as an acceptable permanent trade-off.

## 3. When Automation and Human Touch Conflict on a High-Stakes Decision, Human Touch Wins

Anything touching legal standing, large sums of money, or a dispute defaults to human involvement, even if an automated path is technically capable of resolving it faster. `HUMAN_ASSISTANCE.md` defines exactly which categories this applies to; this principle is why that list exists at all.

## 4. When Personalization and Simplicity Conflict, Ask Which One the Specific User Needs More Right Now

Not a fixed ranking — a first-time buyer overwhelmed by options usually needs simplicity to win; a sophisticated investor comparing options usually needs personalization to win. The decision is made by reading the person in front of the product, per `EXPERIENCE_PRINCIPLES.md` §2, never by a company-wide default applied uniformly regardless of who's being served.

## 5. When a Feature Serves the Business More Visibly Than It Serves the Customer, It Doesn't Ship Without a Customer-Facing Reason Attached

Internal reporting needs, competitive feature parity, and investor-facing metrics are legitimate business concerns — but none of them are sufficient justification alone for a customer-facing change. Every shipped feature must be able to answer `PRODUCT_PHILOSOPHY.md`'s persona-benefit question honestly.

## 6. When Global Consistency and Local/Persona Relevance Conflict, Consistency of Values Wins, Consistency of Execution Loses

The five voice adjectives, the trust pillars, and the behavioral commitments never flex by audience or region. The specific words, pacing, and level of formality do — extensively, per `PERSONALIZATION_SYSTEM.md` and the audience-specific experience documents. Consistency lives one level higher than most teams instinctively enforce it.

## 7. When a Past Decision and New Evidence Conflict, New Evidence Wins — But the Reversal Is Explained, Not Silent

Behavior specifications in this folder are not permanent by default; they're the current best answer. When evidence (a scorecard trend, a support pattern, direct user feedback) shows a documented behavior is wrong, it's changed — and the change is logged with its reasoning, the same discipline `BRAND_PLATFORM_TRIBUNAL.md` itself modeled by re-examining a settled platform honestly rather than assuming it was permanently correct.

## 8. When in Doubt, Optimize for the Version of This Moment the Person Will Remember in Five Years

The tie-breaker of last resort. Most product decisions are genuinely close calls between two reasonable options. When every principle above still leaves ambiguity, ask which version of this interaction the person is more likely to still think about, positively, long after the session ends (`narrative-system/HUMAN_TRUTHS.md`'s entire Memory & Time family exists to make this question answerable, not rhetorical).
