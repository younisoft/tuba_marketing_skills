# Broker Experience

> **Part of:** [`../BRAND_BEHAVIOR_SYSTEM.md`](../BRAND_BEHAVIOR_SYSTEM.md)
> **Persona grounding:** [`../brand-intelligence/broker-personas.md`](../brand-intelligence/broker-personas.md). A broker is not a customer using Tuba occasionally — they're a professional whose livelihood runs through the platform daily. Behavior here is judged by a much higher bar for reliability and respect for expertise than a first-time consumer journey, because a broker will notice, immediately, if the platform is condescending or unreliable in ways a casual user might forgive.

---

## The Governing Shift: From Guide to Colleague

Everywhere else in the system, Tuba behaves like a knowledgeable friend guiding someone through unfamiliar territory (`advertising-dna.md §6`). With brokers, that posture inverts — the broker is often the expert, and Tuba's job is to behave like a capable colleague who makes their work easier, never like a platform explaining their own profession back to them.

## Onboarding Respects Existing Expertise

Broker onboarding never opens with basic real-estate education. It assumes fluency, verifies credentials efficiently (FAL/REGA licensing, per `TRUST_BEHAVIOR.md`'s verification standard), and gets to productive use as fast as the compliance requirements allow — per `EXPERIENCE_PRINCIPLES.md` §2's pacing principle applied to its most experienced audience.

## The Product Behaves Like It Understands Their Business Model

Lead quality is treated as more important than lead volume — a broker's time is a real cost, and the platform never floods them with unqualified leads to inflate an activity metric. Where a lead is routed, the context that produced it (what the buyer actually said they wanted, not just contact details) travels with it, so the broker's first conversation with a new lead doesn't start from zero.

## Transparency About Ranking and Visibility

A broker can see clearly why their listings rank where they do, and what would improve it — never a black-box algorithm they have to guess at. This is `TRUST_BEHAVIOR.md`'s fairness standard applied to the one audience most likely to notice and be harmed by an opaque or unfair system.

## Communication Register

Direct, efficient, and free of the softer explanatory tone used with first-time buyers — a broker-facing notification says what changed and what's needed, briefly, trusting the broker to know what to do with the information (`VOICE_GUIDELINES.md`'s register-adapts-values-don't rule).

## Support Response Standards Are Higher, Not Lower

Because a broker's income depends on the platform working, human assistance for broker-facing issues is held to a faster standard than the general consumer standard in `HUMAN_ASSISTANCE.md` — a broken lead-routing issue or a listing-sync failure is treated as urgent, not queued behind lower-stakes consumer tickets.

## Trust Is Earned Through Consistency, Not Charm

Brokers interact with the platform daily, which means small inconsistencies compound fast. The product's reliability — data accuracy, uptime, consistent behavior release over release — matters more to this audience than any single delightful moment ever could. `DELIGHT_MOMENTS.md`'s standard still applies, but it is a smaller lever here than in a consumer journey; competence is the primary currency.

## What Broker Experience Must Never Do

- Never treat a broker as a lead source to be optimized for Tuba's benefit without visible reciprocal value.
- Never change ranking, fee, or visibility rules without clear advance notice — brokers plan business around these mechanics.
- Never route a genuinely commercial dispute (a fee disagreement, a listing ownership conflict) to a generic consumer support queue.
