# Investor Experience

> **Part of:** [`../BRAND_BEHAVIOR_SYSTEM.md`](../BRAND_BEHAVIOR_SYSTEM.md)
> **Persona grounding:** [`../brand-intelligence/investor-personas.md`](../brand-intelligence/investor-personas.md). An investor is buying a return, not a home to live in — the emotional register of the rest of this system (belonging, comfort, family) is largely the wrong register here. This document exists because applying `FIRST_HOME_EXPERIENCE.md`'s warmth-forward behavior unchanged to an investor journey would read as naive, not caring.

---

## The Governing Shift: From Feeling to Rigor

Where a first-time buyer's journey is paced around emotional reassurance, an investor's journey is paced around analytical rigor — data depth, comparables, yield clarity, risk transparency. Warmth still governs (per `DECISION_PRINCIPLES.md` §6, values never flex by audience), but it shows up as respect for the investor's time and intelligence rather than emotional comfort — the same underlying commitment, expressed differently.

## Recommendations Are Led by Numbers, Framed by Judgment

Per `RECOMMENDATION_BEHAVIOR.md`'s signal hierarchy, investment-intent recommendations weight stated risk tolerance, timeline, and yield priorities heavily — but numbers alone don't tell the whole story, so the platform still surfaces judgment-based context (a neighborhood's realistic trajectory, a development's track record) rather than presenting a bare, unexplained yield percentage as if that were sufficient for a serious decision.

## Uncertainty Is Stated in the Investor's Own Vocabulary

`AI_BEHAVIOR.md`'s uncertainty-admission principle applies here with more technical precision than elsewhere — an investor is shown a confidence range or a stated set of assumptions behind a projection, not a single soft caveat ("this is just an estimate"). This audience has both earned and needs the fuller technical honesty.

## Onboarding Assumes Financial Fluency, Never Explains What Doesn't Need Explaining

Per `EXPERIENCE_PRINCIPLES.md` §2, an investor's onboarding skips basic real-estate education entirely and moves directly to what differentiates this platform's investment offering — verified data, licensed transactions, real comparables — trusting the investor to ask if something genuinely needs clarification.

## Risk Is Never Undersold to Close a Transaction Faster

The single hardest discipline in this document: an investment opportunity's real risk — market risk, liquidity risk, regulatory risk — is stated as plainly as its upside, even when a more optimistic framing would move the person toward a decision faster. This is `advertising-dna.md §5`'s forbidden-territory rule (no unverifiable superlatives, no pressure tactics) tested at its highest stakes, because the category with the most money at risk is exactly the category where the temptation to oversell is strongest.

## Portfolio-Level Behavior, Not Just Transaction-Level

A repeat investor's experience reflects their full portfolio and history with Tuba, not just the current search — surfacing patterns across their holdings (concentration in one area, expiring opportunities relevant to their stated strategy) the way a genuinely attentive advisor would, never resetting to a generic experience each visit (`EXPERIENCE_PRINCIPLES.md` §8).

## Human Assistance Escalates Faster for High-Value Decisions

Given the size of capital typically involved, `HUMAN_ASSISTANCE.md`'s standard response times are treated as a floor, not a target, for investor-facing inquiries above a meaningful transaction size — a serious investment question deserves a serious, prompt human response, not a queued ticket.

## What Investor Experience Must Never Do

- Never use urgency framing around investment opportunities ("limited units remaining for this yield") — the exact forbidden pattern `advertising-dna.md §5` bans, at its most consequential.
- Never present a return projection without stating its assumptions.
- Never treat an investor's larger transaction size as license for less transparency — if anything, the opposite.
