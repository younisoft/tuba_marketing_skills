## 1. Content Pillars

| Pillar | What it covers | Primary emotion (advertising-dna.md §4) | Competitor gap it exploits |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Guidance & Education** | How buying/renting/listing actually works, regulation explained simply, financing literacy | Confidence | Both Aqar and Bayut keep educational content in a separate "academy/blog" register, disconnected from warm brand voice — Tuba's is written in the same voice as everything else |
| **2. Market Insight & Investment** | Data-backed trend reporting, district-level analysis, ROI education | Confidence | Category-standard (competitor-analysis.md §9) — Tuba must match credibility here, differentiate on clarity and tone |
| **3. Place & Lifestyle** | The desert/lifestyle icon language brought to life — neighborhoods, city life, the *feeling* of Saudi places, not just floor plans | Belonging | The single most under-invested pillar across every researched competitor (competitor-analysis.md §9) |
| **4. Community & Customer Success** | Real buyer/renter/investor stories, testimonials, agent spotlights | Belonging, Relief | No competitor researched has a confirmed, sustained community/testimonial content program in KSA (competitor-analysis.md §9) |
| **5. Property Showcase** | Individual listings, project launches, featured homes | Relief, Confidence | Standard category content — differentiated via photography direction (visual-system.md §6) and warm property-description copy (copywriting.md §5.8) |
| **6. Broker & Developer (B2B)** | Lead-quality proof, platform tools, partnership stories | Confidence, quiet pride | Match Bayut's B2B sophistication (TruBroker-style credentialing), executed in Tuba's warmer voice |
| **7. Trust & Compliance** | FAL/REGA explainers, verification process, platform safety | Relief | Turns a legal requirement into reassurance content (advertising-dna.md §8) |

### 1.1 Pillar mix (recommended monthly split, all channels blended)

```
Guidance & Education      25%
Property Showcase         25%
Place & Lifestyle         20%
Market Insight            15%
Community/Customer        10%
Broker/Developer (B2B)     5%   (higher on LinkedIn specifically — see social-media-playbook.md)
```

Trust & Compliance content is not a separate percentage — it is woven into all six other pillars as the Reassure stage (copywriting.md §1), plus occasional dedicated explainer pieces.

## 2. Content Formats

### 2.1 Interactive content
- **Myth vs. Fact** — corrects common real-estate misconceptions (e.g., "Myth: you need 30% down. Fact: financing options exist from X%") — high-value Guidance pillar format, builds authority without selling
- **Before & After** — renovation/staging transformations, or "here's the neighborhood 5 years ago vs. today" for investment storytelling
- **Polls & Questions** — "Villa or apartment?" "What matters most: location or size?" — low-effort engagement that also functions as informal audience research
- **Quotes** — short, extractable lines from market reports or customer stories, designed for screenshot-sharing

### 2.2 Visual/data formats
- **Infographics** — market data, process step-by-steps (the "register → verify → choose → decide" funnel pattern borrowed structurally from Sakani's trust-building convention, competitor-analysis.md §6), FAL-verification explainers
- **Tips** — single-idea, high-utility short posts ("3 things to check before signing a rental contract")

### 2.3 Video formats
- **Short videos/Reels** — property tours, neighborhood spotlights, quick educational explainers (see social-media-playbook.md for platform-specific specs)
- **Carousels** — multi-step guidance content, property feature breakdowns (visual-system.md §9.2)
- **Live sessions** — Q&A with Tuba guidance staff or licensed agents on financing, regulation, market timing — directly embodies the "guide" archetype (advertising-dna.md §6) in real time
- **Stories** — day-in-the-life, behind-the-scenes, quick polls, countdown to project launches (never countdown-as-pressure-tactic — see advertising-dna.md §5)

### 2.4 Series formats
- **Weekly series** — e.g., "سؤال الأسبوع" (Question of the Week, financing/process Q&A), "حي الأسبوع" (Neighborhood of the Week) — builds appointment-viewing habits and reliable content cadence
- **Monthly campaigns** — themed around a single pillar or seasonal moment (see campaign-playbook.md)

## 3. Content by Funnel Role

| Funnel role | Definition | Example formats | Primary pillar |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Evergreen** | Useful regardless of when discovered, drives long-tail search/social traffic | Guidance explainers, Myth vs. Fact, process infographics | Guidance & Education |
| **Authority** | Establishes Tuba as a credible source, earns press/citations | Market insight reports, data infographics | Market Insight |
| **Trust** | Directly reduces perceived risk of transacting | FAL/verification explainers, testimonial content | Trust & Compliance, Community |
| **Lead generation** | Designed to capture a property request or listing signup | Property showcase + clear CTA, "request your property" prompts | Property Showcase |
| **Conversion** | Moves an engaged prospect toward a transaction | Agent-connect prompts, financing calculators, direct listing CTAs | Property Showcase, Guidance |
| **Retention** | Keeps past users engaged post-transaction | Market updates relevant to their area, referral prompts, community stories | Community, Market Insight |
| **Referral** | Explicitly asks satisfied users to bring others | Customer success stories with a share-prompt, referral-incentive content | Community |
| **Viral/shareable** | Optimized for reach beyond existing audience | High-emotion Place & Lifestyle content, surprising market-data stats, wordplay-driven campaign moments (copywriting.md §3 "Name Play" formula) | Place & Lifestyle, Market Insight |

**Rule:** every piece of planned content should be assignable to at least one funnel role before production — content with no funnel role is the first thing cut when planning a calendar.

## 4. Content Type Playbooks

### 4.1 Educational
Format: carousel, infographic, short video, live session. Tone: copywriting.md §5.4. Always plain-language-define any regulatory term on first use.

### 4.2 Investment
Format: data infographic, market report excerpt, quote card. Tone: copywriting.md §5.7 (data first, feeling second). Cite sources always — never present unsourced statistics.

### 4.3 Lifestyle
Format: photography-led feed post, Reels, Stories. Visual direction: visual-system.md §6 + iconography.md §4 (Home + Place icon pairing). This pillar is where the desert/lifestyle icon set should appear most — as texture, section dividers, and decorative overlay on lifestyle photography.

### 4.4 Community
Format: testimonial video/carousel, customer-success quote cards, agent spotlights. Always real — see copywriting.md §9 (no fabricated social proof, ever).

### 4.5 Customer success
Structure: brief context → the moment of relief/decision → the outcome, told in the customer's own words wherever possible (paraphrased for clarity, never fabricated).

### 4.6 Property showcases
Format: carousel or Reel. Copy: property-description formula, copywriting.md §5.8. Always includes FAL license reference (design-components.md badge).

### 4.7 Market insights
Format: data infographic, short-form "quick stat" video, LinkedIn-native long-form post. Recurring branded report series recommended (see §5 below) — mirrors the authority-content pattern both Aqar ("زبدة العقار") and Bayut ("Saudi Market Insights") already use successfully (competitor-analysis.md §6).

### 4.8 Broker content
Format: B2B carousel/LinkedIn post, case-study format ("how Agent X grew their lead pipeline"). Tone: copywriting.md §5.2.

### 4.9 Developer content
Format: project-launch campaign assets (campaign-playbook.md), structured project microsite/landing content, investor-facing data.

## 5. Recommended Recurring Content Property

**"دليل طوبة" (The Tuba Guide)** — a recurring monthly market-insight publication (data report + accompanying social/video content), directly filling the authority-content gap while reinforcing the "Guide" archetype (advertising-dna.md §6) at the level of a named, ownable content property rather than ad-hoc posts. Structure:
- One headline stat per month (concrete number, per copywriting.md §3 "Concrete Number" formula)
- District-level breakdown (2-3 areas)
- One myth-vs-fact segment
- Distributed as: LinkedIn long-form + Instagram/TikTok short summary video + downloadable PDF for agents/developers to share

This single content property can feed the Authority, Trust, Retention, and Lead Generation funnel roles simultaneously — the highest-leverage recurring content investment available given the pillar mix in §1.1.
