Per that document's own instruction, this system extracts the *underlying patterns*, not IKEA's language, furniture-category examples, or brand voice — every formula below is rebuilt in Tuba's own voice for real estate.

This is the official writing guide for every human or AI-generated piece of Tuba copy — Arabic or English, one word or one thousand.

## 1. The Guidance Framework — Tuba's Six-Stage Copy Structure

Every substantial piece of copy (an ad, a landing page section, a campaign, a long social caption) should move through some or all of these six stages, in order. Short-form copy (a single social caption, a push notification) may compress to just stages 1 and 6.

| Stage | Purpose | Tool |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Hook** | Name a real human moment — a dream or a worry — before anything about Tuba | A question or a short declarative image, never a feature |
| **2. Recognize** | Show you understand the *specific* version of that moment the reader is in | 2-3 branching scenarios ("or," not "and") |
| **3. Guide** | Position Tuba as the one walking alongside, not selling at, the reader | "We'll help you..." / "دعنا نساعدك..." register |
| **4. Solve** | Introduce the property/service/feature as the natural next step | Functional description translated into life benefit |
| **5. Reassure** | Remove the specific objection before the reader has to voice it | Financing, licensing/FAL, human support, guarantees |
| **6. Decide** | One clear next step, stated with quiet confidence | Single CTA — never two competing calls to action |

**Worked example (property campaign, English):**

> **Hook:** Ever imagined the exact street you'd walk your kids to school on?
> **Recognize:** Maybe it's a quiet cul-de-sac in Riyadh. Maybe it's five minutes from the Corniche in Jeddah.
> **Guide:** Tell us what that street looks like, and we'll start looking with you.
> **Solve:** Post a property request — our network of licensed agents responds directly to you, not the other way around.
> **Reassure:** Every agent on Tuba is FAL-licensed and REGA-verified, so you're never guessing who you're talking to.
> **Decide:** Request your property, free, in under two minutes.

**Worked example (Arabic, property campaign):**

> **Hook:** هل تخيلت يوماً الشارع الذي سيمشي فيه أطفالك إلى المدرسة؟
> **Recognize:** ربما زقاق هادئ في الرياض، أو على بُعد خمس دقائق من كورنيش جدة.
> **Guide:** أخبرنا كيف يبدو هذا الشارع، ولنبدأ البحث معاً.
> **Solve:** انشر طلب عقارك — شبكتنا من الوسطاء المرخّصين يتواصلون معك مباشرة.
> **Reassure:** كل وسيط على طوبة مرخّص من الهيئة العامة للعقار (فال) وموثّق — فلا حاجة للتخمين.
> **Decide:** اطلب عقارك الآن، مجاناً، في أقل من دقيقتين.

### 1.1 Applying the framework across real estate sub-categories

| Category | Hook | Solve |
|---|---|---|
| Buying (first-time) | The fear of getting it wrong | Guided, licensed process |
| Renting | Time pressure, family logistics | Fast, verified, request-first matching |
| Investment | Fear of a bad bet | Data, market insight, transparent returns |
| Agent/broker acquisition | Credibility and lead quality | Verified platform, professional shopfront |
| Developer/project | Brand-safe qualified demand | Structured project presentation, buyer intent data |

## 2. Voice & Tone

Derived directly from advertising-dna.md §11.

**The voice is:**
- Warm — like a knowledgeable friend, never a call-center script
- Clear — a smart teenager should understand any Tuba sentence on first read
- Grounded — states facts and lets them carry weight, no exclamation-point inflation
- Guiding — always gives the reader a next step, never leaves them stranded
- Respectful — never explains what the reader already knows, never pressures

**The voice is not:**
- Corporate/bureaucratic ("please be advised," "kindly note")
- Salesy/hyped ("unbeatable," "once in a lifetime," "don't miss out")
- Cold/technical (raw specs with no life context)
- Condescending (moralizing about "smart buyers" or "the right choice")

### 2.1 Tone by context (same voice, adjusted register)

| Context | Tone adjustment |
|---|---|
| Performance/paid ad | More direct, offer-forward — still warm, less scene-setting (see §6) |
| Brand/awareness content | Full six-stage framework, more room for feeling |
| Product/transactional (confirmation emails, app notifications) | Warm but efficient — the reassurance stage compresses to one line |
| B2B (agents, developers) | More professional register, less emotional scene-setting, still never cold |
| Support/customer service | Maximum warmth, zero defensiveness, always solution-first |

### 2.2 Sentence length rules

- Headlines: **5–8 words** maximum (Arabic or English)
- Supporting lines: **10–15 words**
- Body paragraphs: vary sentence length deliberately — short sentences for emphasis, one longer sentence for texture, never three long sentences in a row
- One idea per sentence — if a sentence has "and" joining two unrelated claims, split it

## 3. Headline Formulas

| Formula | Pattern | Example (EN) | Example (AR) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Open Dream** | "Ever imagined [dream]?" | "Ever imagined a garden your kids grow up in?" | هل تخيلت يوماً حديقة يكبر فيها أطفالك؟ |
| **The Quiet Reveal** | "[Simple truth] in [N words]" | "The secret is a licensed guide." | السر في مرافق موثوق. |
| **The Binary Choice** | "[A] or [B]?" | "Ready-built, or watch it rise?" | جاهز للسكن، أم تشاهده يُبنى؟ |
| **The Name Play** *(pattern observed in-market — see competitor-analysis.md §7)* | Wordplay on "Tuba/طوبة" meaning goodness/blessing | "Find your Tuba." | ابحث عن طوبتك. |
| **The Concrete Number** *(Sakani "بيت العمر" pattern)* | Emotional phrase + one specific number | "The home that changes everything — from SAR 2,900/month." | البيت الذي يغيّر كل شيء — من 2,900 ريال شهرياً. |
| **The Direct Address** | "You" placed first | "You're not just buying walls." | أنت لا تشتري جدراناً فقط. |

**Rule:** never combine more than two formulas in a single headline — layering techniques (a dream + a number + a question) collapses into noise.

## 4. CTA Formulas

| Intent | CTA (EN) | CTA (AR) |
|---|---|---|
| Start a search/request | "Request your property" | "اطلب عقارك" |
| Browse listings | "See what's out there" | "تصفّح المتاح" |
| List a property | "List with confidence" | "أضف عقارك بثقة" |
| Talk to a human | "Talk to a guide" | "تحدث مع مرشدك" |
| Learn/education content | "Understand the process" | "افهم الخطوات" |
| Agent signup | "Grow with Tuba" | "انمُ مع طوبة" |

**Rule:** exactly one CTA per asset (advertising-dna.md §10). If two actions genuinely compete (e.g., "Buy" vs. "Rent"), split into two assets rather than two buttons.

## 5. Copy Registers by Purpose

### 5.1 Emotional copy
Used at Hook/Recognize/Guide stages. Center a specific sensory or relational moment (a street, a family dinner, a first key-turn) — never an abstract adjective alone ("beautiful," "amazing").

### 5.2 Professional copy (agents/developers/B2B)
Lead with outcome, not warmth-first framing — e.g., *"Verified leads. Licensed platform. Your shopfront, done right."* Warmth still present in tone (never cold), but the Hook stage compresses since B2B readers want efficiency signaled quickly.

### 5.3 Luxury copy
Reserved for explicitly luxury-segment campaigns (advertising-dna.md §10). Shift register toward restraint and specificity over superlatives: *"Four bedrooms. One view of the Red Sea that doesn't repeat itself anywhere else in the city."* Avoid "luxurious," "exclusive," "opulent" as adjectives — show, don't label (see §8 forbidden list).

### 5.4 Educational copy
Explain like a knowledgeable friend, not a legal document. Use numbered steps, plain-language definitions before any regulatory term (e.g., "FAL — the license every legitimate agent must carry — verified in seconds"). Never assume the reader already knows an acronym.

### 5.5 Promotional/offer copy
State the offer plainly and once. Per campaign-playbook.md's Ramadan guidance (competitor-analysis.md §10), favor financing-term framing ("no down payment until handover") over blunt price-cut framing where possible — protects long-term value perception.

### 5.6 Trust copy
Always specific, never generic. Not *"we're trusted"* — instead *"every listing carries a FAL license number you can verify yourself"* (see design-components.md for the visual badge this copy accompanies).

### 5.7 Investment copy
Lead with data, close with feeling — the inverse order of consumer emotional copy, because this audience (competitor-analysis.md persona "Alanoud") explicitly distrusts adjective-heavy claims. *"Average 3-year appreciation in this district: 14%. This is the kind of number that lets you sleep at night."*

### 5.8 Property descriptions
Structure: **Life-context opener → factual specs → one distinguishing detail → gentle close.**
> *"A top-floor apartment built for slow mornings. 3 bedrooms, 2 baths, 180m², north-facing balcony that catches the evening light. FAL license #XXXXXX — verify anytime."*

### 5.9 Social captions
Short-form version of the Guidance Framework — Hook + Decide only, occasionally with one Reassure line. See social-media-playbook.md for platform-specific length/format rules.

### 5.10 Campaign headlines
See §3. Campaign headline must survive standalone on an OOH board with zero supporting copy (advertising-dna.md, visual-system.md §9.7) — test every campaign headline against that constraint.

### 5.11 Email writing
Subject line = a Hook-formula headline (≤8 words). One CTA. Reassurance stage compresses to a single trust line in the footer (FAL/license, support contact) — see visual-system.md §9.9.

### 5.12 Push notifications
Maximum 1 sentence, always specific, never generic re-engagement bait: not *"Check out what's new!"* — instead *"3 new listings match your request in Al Nakheel."*

### 5.13 WhatsApp messages
Most conversational register in the system — contractions and informal-but-correct Arabic/English acceptable here specifically because WhatsApp is a 1:1 channel. Still never uses forbidden vocabulary (§8) or false urgency.

### 5.14 Website microcopy
Buttons, form labels, empty states, error messages — every one of these gets Guidance Framework thinking even at 3-5 words. An empty search-results state should never just say "No results" — try *"Nothing yet — want us to notify you when a match appears?"*

## 6. Register Shift for Performance Marketing

Per competitor-analysis.md §7 (all researched competitors keep expressive language in campaigns and revert to plain transactional copy in-product) — Tuba's differentiation is refusing to make that split. However, performance ads (paid social/search) do compress the Guidance Framework: lead with Solve/offer specifics (price, location, spec) within the first line, keep Hook to a fragment, always end with a single strong CTA. Clarity-first is not the same as voice-abandoning — warmth shows in word choice even in a four-word headline.

## 7. Language-Specific Style

### 7.1 Arabic
- Saudi/Gulf-inflected Modern Standard Arabic for brand copy — formal enough for trust, warm enough to feel local (not textbook MSA, not heavy slang)
- Direct address using أنت/ك (you/your) as the dominant pronoun — mirrors the "customer is the hero" principle (advertising-dna.md §3)
- Avoid Eastern Arabic-Indic numerals in prices/specs — use Western numerals for scannability (visual-system.md §2.4)
- Never justify body text in narrow columns (visual-system.md §2.4)
- Casual dialect permitted only in short-form social (TikTok/Snapchat captions) per social-media-playbook.md — never in legal/trust copy

### 7.2 English
- Plain, warm, contraction-friendly in social/conversational contexts; contraction-free in formal/legal contexts
- Primarily serves investor, expat, and international-audience touchpoints (per competitor-analysis.md §2's finding that English channels are consistently under-invested by competitors — Tuba should invest here deliberately, not as an afterthought)
- Avoid Americanisms that don't travel (e.g., "realtor" — use "agent" or "broker")

### 7.3 Grammar consistency
- One editorial style per language, documented in a living style sheet (extend this section as real usage questions arise)
- Property specs always in consistent order: bedrooms → bathrooms → area (m²) → price
- Currency: always "SAR" or "ريال" spelled out at first mention per asset, symbol/abbreviation acceptable after

## 8. Vocabulary

### 8.1 Preferred words (English)
guide · together · clear · verified · your (place/street/pace) · relief · ready · straightforward · real · here for you · step by step

### 8.2 Preferred words (Arabic)
معاً (together) · نساعدك (we help you) · بثقة (with confidence) · واضح (clear) · موثّق (verified) · خطوة بخطوة (step by step) · مكانك (your place) · اطمئن (rest assured) · بيتك (your home)

### 8.3 Forbidden / banned expressions
| English | Arabic | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "Best in the market" / "#1" (unless independently citable) | "الأفضل في السوق" / "رقم واحد" | Unverifiable superlative — advertising-dna.md §5 |
| "Don't miss out!" / "Limited time!" (used as manufactured urgency) | "لا تفوّت الفرصة!" | Fear-based pressure — advertising-dna.md §5 |
| "Dear valued customers" | "عملاءنا الكرام" | Generic, distancing — contradicts direct-address principle (§7.1) |
| "Contact us today" (as a generic catch-all CTA) | "تواصل معنا اليوم" | Vague, not a real CTA (§4) |
| "Revolutionary," "game-changing" | "ثوري," "يغيّر قواعد اللعبة" | Overclaiming, breaks grounded voice tone |
| "Luxurious," "opulent" (outside luxury-segment campaigns) | "فاخر," "باذخ" (خارج حملات الفخامة) | Reserved register — §5.3 |

### 8.4 Power words (use deliberately, not as filler)
verified · together · guide/guided · clear(ly) · finally · exactly · yours · ready · real

### 8.5 Words to use with caution
"dream" (powerful but overused across the category — earn it with specificity, don't lead every headline with it), "family" (authentic when specific, generic when used as a catch-all demographic label)

## 9. Emotional Triggers & Psychological Persuasion (used responsibly — see advertising-dna.md §5 for hard limits)

| Technique | Application | Guardrail |
|---|---|---|
| **Specificity over abstraction** | "A top-floor apartment that catches the evening light" beats "a beautiful apartment" | Never invent specifics not true of the actual listing |
| **Objection pre-emption** | Address financing/trust concerns before the reader has to ask (Reassure stage) | Only state what's operationally true (advertising-dna.md §8) |
| **Concrete numbers over adjectives** | A real monthly payment figure beats "affordable" | Numbers must be accurate and current |
| **The customer as hero** | Frame Tuba as enabling the reader's decision, never as the impressive party | Advertising-dna.md Principle 1 |
| **Social proof, honestly presented** | Real testimonials, real transaction outcomes | Never fabricated reviews or invented statistics |
| **Choice architecture** | Present 2-3 clear options (Recognize stage), not overwhelming lists | Never more than 3 branching options in one piece of copy |

**What Tuba does not use:** scarcity fabrication, countdown-timer pressure, guilt framing, comparison-shaming ("everyone else has already found their home").
