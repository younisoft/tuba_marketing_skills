# Social Media Playbook — Tuba

> **Part of:** [Tuba Advertising Identity System](../ADVERTISING_IDENTITY_GUIDE.md)
> **Builds on:** [content-system.md](content-system.md) · [competitor-analysis.md](../knowledge-base/competitor-analysis.md) §5 (platform-by-platform competitive evidence)
> **Confirmed Tuba channels today:** Facebook, X, Instagram, Pinterest, LinkedIn, TikTok, Snapchat (project-analysis.md §14) — this playbook also recommends activating YouTube and Threads

Platform priority in this playbook is not generic best-practice guesswork — it is directly evidenced by category-leader behavior researched in competitor-analysis.md: Snapchat is the single largest organic channel for the market leader (Aqar, ~262K, its biggest platform) and the site of the best-documented paid-campaign performance in the entire research set (Bayut's AR Lens campaign). Treat the channel order below as a resourcing priority, not an arbitrary list.

---

## 1. Platform Priority Tier

| Tier | Platforms | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1 — Primary** | Snapchat, TikTok, Instagram | Where the Saudi real estate audience actually is and engages (competitor-analysis.md §5) |
| **Tier 2 — Strategic** | LinkedIn, YouTube | B2B credibility (LinkedIn) and a confirmed competitive white space (YouTube — competitor-analysis.md §5) |
| **Tier 3 — Maintenance** | X, Facebook, Pinterest, Threads | Announcement/customer-service/archival presence — maintain consistency, don't over-invest production budget |

---

## 2. Instagram

- **Visual identity:** Full visual-system.md palette and photography direction applied at highest production quality — Instagram is Tuba's most "brand-book-accurate" channel
- **Content style:** Reels-first (per category data, Reels significantly outperform static posts — research/competitor-research-notes.md §"Saudi digital-marketing landscape"); Place & Lifestyle and Property Showcase pillars lead here
- **Frequency:** 5-6 feed posts/Reels per week, daily Stories
- **Post structure:** Hook in first 3 seconds of video or first line of caption; caption follows Hook + Decide compressed structure (copywriting.md §5.9)
- **Thumbnail/cover style:** Consistent cover-frame treatment across Reels (mark watermark bottom corner, per visual-system.md §9.4) so the grid reads as one designed feed
- **Hook formulas:** Visual hook (arresting first frame) + headline formula from copywriting.md §3, usually "The Open Dream" or "The Binary Choice"
- **CTA:** Link-in-bio or "Request now" sticker on Stories; comments-driven engagement CTA on feed posts ("Which one's your favorite — 1 or 2?")
- **Hashtag strategy:** 3-5 targeted Arabic hashtags (city/district + property-type) + 1-2 brand hashtags (e.g., #طوبة #دليل_طوبة) — avoid generic hashtag stuffing
- **Content ratio:** 40% Property Showcase, 30% Place & Lifestyle, 20% Guidance & Education, 10% Community
- **Best practice:** run Arabic as the primary-language account (per competitor-analysis.md §5 finding that Arabic accounts vastly outperform English ones within the same brand) — English content can live as occasional Story highlights or a small secondary presence, not a parallel full account, until English demand is proven

## 3. TikTok

- **Visual identity:** More native/less polished than Instagram — raw-cut, vertical-native footage; brand color/logo present but not dominant (matches platform norms)
- **Content style:** Fast-paced property tours, "day in the life of an agent," quick myth-vs-fact, trend-adapted formats
- **Frequency:** 4-5 videos/week
- **Post structure:** Sub-2-second hook (text overlay + spoken hook simultaneously), 15-30 second core content, on-screen CTA text in final 2 seconds
- **Thumbnail style:** N/A (autoplay platform) — first-frame composition matters more than a designed thumbnail; ensure first frame alone is compelling
- **Hook formulas:** Question hooks and "Binary Choice" formula perform best in-feed; casual dialect register permitted here specifically (copywriting.md §7.1)
- **CTA:** Verbal + on-screen, always singular ("Comment your city and we'll find you three")
- **Hashtag strategy:** Mix of niche (#عقارات_الرياض) and broad discovery tags (#اكسبلور — confirmed effective discovery convention per competitor-analysis.md §5) — 3-4 total, not more
- **Content ratio:** 35% Property Showcase, 30% Guidance & Education (quick tips), 25% Place & Lifestyle, 10% Community
- **Best practice:** never simply re-post Instagram Reels unedited — TikTok's algorithm and audience reward native-feeling pacing and captions

## 4. Snapchat

- **Visual identity:** Vertical, immediate, less "produced" — leans into Stories-native, ephemeral feel
- **Content style:** Given this is the category's single strongest-performing channel (competitor-analysis.md §5-6), prioritize platform-native ad formats over static image ads specifically — AR/interactive formats (e.g., a "visualize your future home" lens, directly informed by Bayut's documented AR Lens success) are the highest-leverage investment on this platform
- **Frequency:** Daily Snap Stories; paid campaign cadence tied to campaign-playbook.md seasonal moments
- **Post structure:** Full-screen vertical, single message per Snap, swipe-up/tap CTA
- **Thumbnail style:** N/A — Snap ads are judged on first-second impact
- **Hook formulas:** Visual-first — the Hook stage should be an image/moment before any text
- **CTA:** Direct action (swipe up to request property / open lens)
- **Hashtag strategy:** N/A — Snapchat is not hashtag-driven; discovery is via Discover placement and paid targeting
- **Content ratio:** Weight toward Property Showcase and seasonal campaign content (National Day, Ramadan — campaign-playbook.md) given this channel's proven high-performance ceiling for interactive brand campaigns
- **Best practice:** budget for at least one custom AR Lens per major seasonal campaign rather than only running static/video ads here — this is the format category leaders have proven works disproportionately well in KSA

## 5. LinkedIn

- **Visual identity:** Most "corporate-appropriate" register of the palette — Purple-dominant, minimal Coral, professional photography or data-visualization graphics (visual-system.md §9.8 presentation-template logic applies well here)
- **Content style:** B2B-facing — broker/developer content pillar leads, plus market-insight authority content (the "دليل طوبة" recurring report, content-system.md §5, is a natural LinkedIn-native long-form format)
- **Frequency:** 2-3 posts/week
- **Post structure:** Longer-form allowed (150-300 words) — still open with a Hook line, not a company announcement lede
- **Thumbnail style:** Clean data-visualization or presentation-style graphics (visual-system.md §9.8)
- **Hook formulas:** "The Quiet Reveal" and "The Concrete Number" formulas perform well for a professional audience
- **CTA:** "Learn more," "Partner with us," "Grow with Tuba" — B2B-register CTAs from copywriting.md §4
- **Hashtag strategy:** 3-5 professional/industry tags (#السوق_العقاري_السعودي, #Vision2030, #PropTech)
- **Content ratio:** 40% Market Insight, 30% Broker/Developer, 20% Company culture/milestones, 10% Educational
- **Best practice:** avoid porting consumer-facing lifestyle content here unedited — register mismatch (copywriting.md §2.1)

## 6. X (Twitter)

- **Visual identity:** Lightweight — text-forward, quick graphics, brand colors present but secondary to message speed
- **Content style:** Announcements, customer service, real-time market commentary, National Day/cultural-moment engagement
- **Frequency:** Daily (mix of original posts and reactive/customer-service replies)
- **Post structure:** Short-form, single-idea, thread format for slightly longer educational content
- **Hook formulas:** "The Concrete Number" and direct-address formulas work well for quick-scroll text feeds
- **CTA:** Link to relevant landing page/app; "DM us" for support threads
- **Hashtag strategy:** Minimal — 1-2 tags maximum, X is not a hashtag-discovery-driven platform in practice
- **Content ratio:** 40% Customer service/support, 30% Announcements, 20% Market commentary, 10% Campaign amplification
- **Best practice:** this is Tuba's fastest-response-time channel — treat unanswered mentions/DMs here as a direct trust-pillar risk (advertising-dna.md §8)

## 7. Facebook

- **Visual identity:** Same as Instagram, slightly more text-permissive per post
- **Content style:** Skews toward an older, family-decision-maker demographic — Guidance & Education and Property Showcase content perform well; useful for longer-form community/group engagement (local buy/rent groups)
- **Frequency:** 3-4 posts/week (largely repurposed from Instagram with platform-appropriate captions, not 1:1 cross-posting)
- **Best practice:** monitor and respond in Facebook groups/Marketplace-adjacent community spaces where relevant — a listening channel as much as a broadcast one

## 8. YouTube — Priority Growth Channel

- **Why prioritize despite currently low Tuba activity:** competitor-analysis.md §5 confirms this is the *least*-invested channel by both category leaders — a rare, evidenced white-space opportunity
- **Content style:** Long-form guidance content ("how financing actually works," full neighborhood walkthroughs, live-session recordings from content-system.md §2.3), building the "Guide" archetype (advertising-dna.md §6) in the format best suited to depth
- **Frequency:** 1-2 videos/week to start, building toward a recognizable weekly series (e.g., "حي الأسبوع" neighborhood-of-the-week, content-system.md §2.4)
- **Thumbnail style:** visual-system.md §9.4 — bold 3-5 word overlay, consistent mark watermark, high-contrast scrim
- **Hook formulas:** First 5 seconds must state the viewer benefit directly ("By the end of this video, you'll know exactly how much down payment you actually need")
- **CTA:** Subscribe + link to relevant landing page in description
- **Best practice:** this channel compounds — unlike Stories/Snaps, YouTube content stays discoverable via search indefinitely, making it the highest long-term-value content investment in this playbook once resourced

## 9. Threads — Emerging/Optional

- Treat as a lightweight extension of X content until the platform's role in the Saudi market is clearer — do not build dedicated production resource here yet; monitor and maintain minimal presence

## 10. Pinterest

- **Content style:** Place & Lifestyle pillar content (interior inspiration, neighborhood aesthetic boards) — a natural home for the desert/lifestyle icon language (iconography.md §4) as graphic pin overlays
- **Frequency:** Low-effort, evergreen — batch-schedule monthly
- **Best practice:** treat as a long-tail search/discovery asset (Pinterest content has a long shelf life), not a real-time engagement channel

## 11. Cross-Platform Best Practices

1. **Never cross-post identically.** Every platform gets its content re-cut to its own pacing and aspect ratio (visual-system.md §9) — a TikTok video shrunk into an Instagram feed post with black bars is an instant off-brand signal
2. **Arabic-first, always.** Every platform's primary account is Arabic; English is a secondary layer, not a mirrored parallel operation (per the evidenced competitor pattern in competitor-analysis.md §5)
3. **One voice, many registers.** Tone shifts by platform (copywriting.md §2.1) but the five voice adjectives (advertising-dna.md §11) never change
4. **FAL/trust signaling is present but unobtrusive everywhere** — never absent (advertising-dna.md §8), never loud enough to dominate a post (visual-system.md color hierarchy, §1.7)
5. **Response-time discipline** — X and Instagram DMs/comments should be monitored on a same-day basis at minimum; unanswered public questions on a "guide" brand are a direct DNA violation
