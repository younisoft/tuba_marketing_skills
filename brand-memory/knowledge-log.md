# Knowledge Log — Tuba

> **Layer:** Brand Memory (living)
> **Purpose:** the memory journal — every major decision made about Tuba's brand, marketing strategy, or this documentation system itself, recorded with its reasoning. This is the file that lets someone six months or six years from now understand not just *what* Tuba decided, but *why*, what else was considered, and whether it's still the right call.
> **Owner:** Brand Owner
> **Update trigger:** whenever a real strategic decision is made — positioning shifts, major campaign-direction calls, system-document changes, hiring/process decisions with brand implications
> **Related documents:** every document in this system — this is the log of record for changes to any of them

**Status: no decisions logged yet beyond the system's own founding.** See [README.md §2](README.md). The founding of this documentation system itself is the first entry, below.

## Entry Template

```markdown
## [Decision title] — [date]

**Decision:** [what was decided, stated plainly]
**Reason:** [why — the actual reasoning, including any evidence from research/brand-memory files]
**Alternatives considered:** [what else was on the table, and why it wasn't chosen]
**Final outcome:** [what happened as a result — update this field over time as the decision plays out]
**Future review date:** [when this decision should be revisited, if applicable — not every decision needs one, but many do]
**Logged by:** [name/role] on [date]
```

## Founding Entry

```markdown
## Founding of the Tuba Advertising Identity, Operating, and Intelligence/Memory System — 2026-07-14

**Decision:** build a complete, layered documentation system (knowledge-base/ → advertising-system/ → brand-intelligence/ + brand-memory/) as the permanent single source of truth for Tuba's brand and advertising, rather than relying on ad hoc guidelines or individual institutional memory.
**Reason:** Tuba had a strong logo/color foundation (current-brand-analysis.md) and a genuinely differentiated founding brand brief (project-analysis.md §2), but no operational system translating that foundation into consistent day-to-day marketing execution (project-analysis.md §19's gap table) — the system was built to close exactly that gap.
**Alternatives considered:** a lighter single-document brand guide (rejected — insufficient for the scale of channels, personas, and campaign types this category requires); relying on institutional knowledge alone (rejected — doesn't scale across a growing team or AI-assisted workflows, and doesn't compound over time the way a written memory system does).
**Final outcome:** system built across four phases — knowledge-base/ + advertising-system/ (Phases 1-12), the operating layer (checklists, workflows, AI prompts — "Phase 13"), and this brand-intelligence/ + brand-memory/ layer ("Phase 14"). Ongoing — see [monthly-retrospectives.md](monthly-retrospectives.md) for how well it's working in practice.
**Future review date:** first annual review of brand-intelligence/ documents (12 months from founding), and continuous monthly review of brand-memory/ per README.md §6
**Logged by:** Brand strategy documentation project on 2026-07-14
```

## Best Practices
- Log a decision even when it seems obvious at the time — obviousness fades faster than most people expect, and the "alternatives considered" field is often the most valuable part in hindsight
- Update the "Final outcome" field over time rather than treating an entry as closed the day it's written — a decision's real outcome often isn't clear until months later

## Common Mistakes
- Only logging decisions that turned out well — a documented decision that didn't pan out is exactly the kind of institutional memory that prevents repeating the same mistake
- Vague reasoning ("felt like the right call") instead of citing the actual evidence or principle behind the decision
