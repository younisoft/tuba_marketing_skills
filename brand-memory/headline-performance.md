## How to Use This Ranking

1. When briefing new copy (creative-brief-template.md), check this ranking for the relevant category first
2. A formula with a strong, large-sample track record should be the default starting point; a formula with no data yet is fair to test
3. A formula that consistently underperforms across multiple campaigns and contexts should be flagged for review — possibly retired from active rotation, but never deleted from headline-library.md's record (keep the formula documented even if deprioritized, for future context)

---

## Best Practices
- Only rank formulas with a real, adequate sample size — a single campaign's result is a data point, not a ranking
- Segment rankings by persona/audience where possible — a formula that wins for Investors may lose for First-Time Buyers (buyer-personas.md, investor-personas.md)

## Common Mistakes
- Ranking by a metric that doesn't match the content's actual funnel role (e.g., ranking Authority-category headlines by conversion rate when their real job is trust-building, not immediate conversion)
- Declaring a formula "proven" based on too small a sample

## Future Expansion Notes
- A formula that dominates its category ranking across multiple quarters is a strong candidate to be promoted as the *default* recommendation in [headline-library.md](../brand-intelligence/headline-library.md) at its semi-annual review
