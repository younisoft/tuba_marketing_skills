## Entry Template

```markdown
## [Campaign Name] — [date]

**Link to full record:** [campaign-history.md entry]
**Why it succeeded:** [specific, causal explanation — not just "it performed well" but *what mechanism* drove the result]
**What should be repeated:** [the specific, reusable element — a headline formula, a visual approach, a targeting choice]
**Which principle(s) it validated:** [link to advertising-dna.md, creative-principles.md, or another brand-intelligence document whose claim this result supports]
**Should this be promoted into a static document?** [Yes/No — if yes, which document and what specific addition]
**Logged by:** [name/role] on [date]
```

## Best Practices
- Every winning campaign entry must explain the *mechanism* of success, not just cite the metric — a headline number without a causal explanation isn't reusable knowledge
- Cross-check every "what should be repeated" against creative-principles.md before generalizing — a single win might be context-specific, not universally repeatable

## Common Mistakes
- Declaring a campaign a "win" based on vanity metrics (reach, likes) rather than the KPIs actually targeted in the brief (advertising-playbook.md §5)
- Over-generalizing from one success — wait for a pattern to repeat before promoting it into a static Brand Intelligence document (see README.md §1's promotion logic)

## Future Expansion Notes
- Once several winning campaigns share a common mechanism, that's the strongest signal for promotion into brand-intelligence/creative-principles.md or the relevant format-specific document
