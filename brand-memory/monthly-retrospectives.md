## Retrospective Template

```markdown
## [Month, Year]

**Campaigns run this month:** [list, linked to campaign-history.md]
**What worked:** [summary, linked to winning-campaigns.md entries]
**What didn't:** [summary, linked to failed-campaigns.md entries]
**Tests concluded:** [linked to ab-tests.md]
**Recurring customer feedback themes:** [linked to customer-feedback.md]
**New insights:** [linked to marketing-insights.md / market-observations.md]
**Competitor moves worth noting:** [linked to competitor-watch.md]
**Durable lessons to carry forward:** [linked to lessons-learned.md — only the ones confirmed this month, not a re-list of old ones]
**Open questions for next month:** [anything unresolved]
**Attendees/contributors:** [names/roles]
**Logged by:** [name/role] on [date]
```

## Best Practices
- Hold this as a genuine fixed-cadence meeting/review, not just a document that gets filled in retroactively — the discipline of the cadence is as valuable as the content
- Every retrospective should reference, not duplicate, the detailed entries in the source files (campaign-history.md etc.) — this is a synthesis layer, not a re-transcription

## Common Mistakes
- Skipping a month "because nothing much happened" — even a quiet month is worth a short entry for continuity
- Letting "durable lessons to carry forward" become a repetitive re-statement of the same few lessons every month instead of tracking genuinely new ones (check against [lessons-learned.md](lessons-learned.md) before re-logging)

## Future Expansion Notes
- Quarterly, review the last three months' retrospectives together for larger patterns that a single month wouldn't reveal — feed durable, cross-month patterns into [lessons-learned.md](lessons-learned.md) and the annual Brand Intelligence review cycle
