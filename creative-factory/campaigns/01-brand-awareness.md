# Campaign 01 — Brand Awareness

> **Sources retrieved:** see [`../ROUTING_LOG.md`](../ROUTING_LOG.md) — shared grounding only; no persona file needed (this is the one campaign type where the audience is deliberately broad, per `campaign-playbook.md §3.3`, not a named persona).
> **Campaign type:** Awareness × Brand (`campaign-playbook.md §3.3` + `§3.4`, combined — this is Tuba's first-ever brand hero moment, so it carries both the awareness objective and the brand-tier production investment)
> **Not a re-litigation of the Big Idea:** this campaign is Phase 1 of the platform's own documented rollout (`BIG_IDEA_PLATFORM.md` Part Eight, "Phase 1 (launch): introduce the idea") — it introduces **Everyone's Tuba Is Different** to the market for the first time.

---

## Strategic Objective

Build unaided brand recognition around Tuba's purple/coral color identity and introduce the platform's central idea — *everyone's Tuba is different* — as a distinct emotional territory no competitor occupies (`campaign-playbook.md §3.3`; the white space confirmed in `current-brand-analysis.md` §13, referenced via `advertising-dna.md`).

## Business Goal

Tuba has functioned in-market without a claimed emotional territory (a documented, honest gap — `BIG_IDEA_PLATFORM.md` Part Seven). This campaign closes that gap in one moment rather than accumulating it gradually across unrelated content.

## Human Insight

Every category competitor's awareness advertising shows one dream and asks everyone to want it. But no two people are searching for the same thing — a retiree downsizing and a newlywed couple are both "home buyers" and have almost nothing else in common. The category has never said this out loud. Tuba is the first brand whose own name lets it say it (`BIG_IDEA_PLATFORM.md` Part Two).

## Emotional Territory

**Belonging**, made personal rather than generic — supported by **Confidence** (`narrative-system/EMOTIONAL_SYSTEM.md`). Exactly two emotions at real weight, per that document's governing rule; nothing else competes for the frame.

## Creative Platform

**Everyone's Tuba Is Different** (`BIG_IDEA_PLATFORM.md`), dramatized through Narrative Pillar 2 — **Your Particular Reasons**, the hub pillar (`narrative-system/NARRATIVE_PILLARS.md`) — told as an anthology of four short, specific vignettes rather than one hero story, per `CINEMATIC_LANGUAGE.md`'s "anthology over epic" rule:

| Vignette | Archetype | The unrepeatable detail |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | The First Home Seeker | A door with a keychain still bearing the shop's paper tag |
| 2 | The Returning Son | A specific, unchanged corner-shop smell on the walk back |
| 3 | The Family Protector | A pencil height-chart already started on a hallway wall before the family has even moved in |
| 4 | The Quiet Achiever | A plain, unremarkable front door chosen over the ornamental one next to it |

This is not four separate ideas — it is one claim, proven four different ways, matching `BIG_IDEA_PLATFORM.md`'s explicit discipline: *"never say it without immediately proving it with one particular, unrepeatable detail."*

## Art Direction

Composition follows `narrative-system/VISUAL_LANGUAGE.md`: one subject, given room; eye-level, never a drone/hero-angle shot; architecture in service of the person, never the reverse. Each vignette opens on the unrepeatable detail in tight, textured close-up before revealing the person — the detail *is* the hook, not an establishing wide shot. Purple carries the connective tissue between vignettes (transition cards, the closing line); Coral appears exactly once per vignette, on the one detail that makes it unrepeatable, per `BIG_IDEA_PLATFORM.md` Part Nine's "Coral's deepened job."

## Typography Direction

Display type per `visual-system.md §2.2` — Plus Jakarta Sans ExtraBold (Latin) / Almarai Bold (Arabic) for the campaign line, set large with deliberate negative space around the possessive word ("**Your** particular reasons") — the platform's one explicit typographic emphasis technique (`BIG_IDEA_PLATFORM.md` Part Nine, Typography Direction). No new typeface, no new weight introduced.

## Photography Direction

Warm, golden-hour-biased, real Saudi homes and people (`visual-system.md §6.1`), extended with the platform-specific rule: every frame contains one detail that could not be swapped into a different vignette without becoming false (`BIG_IDEA_PLATFORM.md` Part Nine). Regional range held deliberately across the four vignettes — Najd, Hejaz, Eastern Province, and a mixed-urban setting — per `narrative-system/CULTURAL_TRUTHS.md`'s rule against monolithic depiction.

## Motion Direction

Growth-based motion for transitions between vignettes — a soft cross-dissolve on a physical match-cut (a hand on one door becoming a hand on the next), never a hard graphic wipe (`narrative-system/MOTION_LANGUAGE.md`, `visual-system.md §8`). The film's final beat holds a static frame two full seconds longer than any prior shot — the Reflection beat (`STORY_ARCHITECTURE.md`) the whole piece has been earning. No countdown, no speed-ramp, no logo spin.

## Copy Direction

Full six-stage Guidance Framework (`copywriting.md §1`) — this is a brand-tier asset, entitled to the complete arc, not a compressed one.

> **Hook:** Everyone says they want to help you find your dream home.
> **Recognize:** But whose dream, exactly?
> **Guide:** Not one dream. Yours — whatever it actually is.
> **Solve:** Tuba doesn't show you what everyone wants. We help you find what you do.
> **Reassure:** Every home on Tuba is FAL-licensed and verified — the guidance is personal, the trust isn't optional.
> **Decide:** See how it works.

**Arabic (same arc, natively written, not translated):**
> **الفكرة:** الكل يقول إنه سيساعدك تجد بيت أحلامك.
> **لكن:** حلم مين بالضبط؟
> **التوجيه:** مو حلم واحد. حلمك أنت — أياً كان.
> **الحل:** طوبة ما تعرض عليك اللي يبيه الكل. تساعدك تلقى اللي تبيه أنت.
> **الطمأنة:** كل عقار على طوبة موثّق ومرخّص من فال — التوجيه شخصي، والثقة مو اختيارية.
> **القرار:** شوف كيف تسير الأمور.

Campaign line: **"Everyone's Tuba is different." / "لكل واحد طوبته."** — used once, at the film's close, immediately following the fourth vignette's proof point, never opening the piece (per `BIG_IDEA_PLATFORM.md`'s "never explain the idea, demonstrate it" rule).

## Social Execution

Tier 1 platforms carry this campaign (`social-media-playbook.md §1`): **Snapchat** (the category's proven highest-performing seasonal/brand format — a custom AR Lens letting a user "step through" one of four doors), **Instagram** (full film + 4-part carousel breaking out each vignette + Story teasers), **TikTok** (a re-cut, faster-paced 15s edit of vignette 1 only, native pacing per `social-media-playbook.md §3`). **YouTube** carries the full 60–90s film as the anchor asset (earned-media candidate per `campaign-playbook.md §2.1`'s PR note). LinkedIn is deliberately not a primary channel for this consumer-facing brand moment.

## Design Mockup Description

The hero asset is a 60-second vertical/horizontal dual-cut film. It opens in near-silence on a close, textured shot of a paper price-tag still looped through a brass keychain — no music, no logo, no context. A hand enters frame, removes the tag. Cut (soft dissolve) to a corner-shop awning, sun-bleached, the same hand now years older, touching the same peeling paint. Cut to a pencil mark at exactly a child's height on a bare hallway wall, morning light crossing it. Cut to a plain wooden door being chosen, quietly, over an ornate one just visible in the same frame. Each cut is a door-shaped match cut. Only in the final three seconds does Purple fill the frame, the mark fades in — never spinning, never distorting — and the line resolves in white type: *"Everyone's Tuba is different."* No CTA appears on screen; the CTA lives only in the platform-specific cutdowns.

## Success Metrics

Per `campaign-playbook.md §3.3` and `advertising-playbook.md §5`'s KPI set:
- Aided and unaided brand recall (pre/post brand-lift study)
- Share of voice within category seasonal/brand-moment conversation
- Video completion rate ≥40% on the full-length YouTube/Instagram cut (brand-film benchmark, not a performance-ad benchmark)
- Earned media pickup (Saudi trade press coverage, per the Aqar National Day precedent noted in `competitor-analysis.md §6`)
- **Not measured here:** CPL/CPA — this is explicitly not a lead-generation asset; a request-property CTA appearing anywhere in the hero film would violate `advertising-dna.md` Principle 4 (one idea per asset) at the exact moment this campaign is trying to establish an emotional claim, not extract an action

## Self-Critique

- **Would this win an international creative award?** Plausibly, on craft and on the name-lock insight — the closest real precedent is Apple's "Think Different," which won on identical grounds (a values claim only that brand could make).
- **Would someone remember it next week?** The four-detail structure is designed to be recounted ("did you see the one with the height chart?") rather than summarized as a slogan — a stronger memory mechanism than most category advertising.
- **Would it still feel relevant in two years?** Yes — nothing in it is trend-dependent; the anthology structure is explicitly built to keep adding vignettes without becoming stale (`BIG_IDEA_PLATFORM.md` Part Eight, Phase 2).
- **Would this exist without the Tuba brand?** No — the entire campaign line is grammatically locked to the brand's own name (`BIG_IDEA_PLATFORM.md` Part Six's ownability scoring). No competitor's name supports an equivalent device.
