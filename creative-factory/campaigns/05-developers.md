# Campaign 05 — Developers

> **Sources retrieved:** shared grounding + [`../../brand-intelligence/developer-personas.md`](../../brand-intelligence/developer-personas.md) §1 (Large Master-Plan Developer, primary) and §2 (Boutique Developer, secondary contrast) — read to avoid the exact mistake the persona file itself flags: using identical partnership messaging across developer scale/type.
> **Campaign type:** Developer partnership / off-plan project launch (`campaign-playbook.md §2.3`)

---

## Strategic Objective

Win qualified buyer-demand partnerships with developers by presenting projects at the scale and brand-safety level they require — never a generic "list with us" pitch.

## Business Goal

Developer partnerships are Tuba's structural gateway to inventory quality and volume simultaneously (`developer-personas.md §1`'s stated fear: "being presented alongside lower-quality listings in a way that dilutes project prestige") — this campaign's real job is proving Tuba won't do that.

## Human Insight

A Large Master-Plan Developer already has their own marketing infrastructure (`developer-personas.md §1` Objection). They don't need another platform offering to market for them — they need one that won't make their project look smaller or less serious than it is next to everything else on the platform.

## Emotional Territory

**Confidence**, in the professional-partnership register (`copywriting.md §5.2`) — the same register `investor-personas.md`'s Institutional/Fund Investor requires, since large-scale developer B2B communication shares that formality (`developer-personas.md §4`).

## Creative Platform

Narrative Pillar 6 — **Building What Outlasts You** — through the archetype of **The Dream Builder** (`CHARACTER_ARCHETYPES.md` §1: "someone building toward a specific future they can already see clearly, even though it doesn't exist yet"). The campaign's proof mechanic is deliberately Tuba's own version of the platform's central claim, one level up: *a developer's own specific reasons for a project's design* — a preserved hill, a protected tree, a community-first choice against advisor recommendation — told the same way an individual buyer's particular reasons would be (`narrative-system/CAMPAIGN_UNIVERSE.md`'s Developers entry, cited directly, not reinvented).

## Art Direction

Structured, professional, ground-level human imagery balanced with masterplan context (`campaign-playbook.md §2.3`'s NHC-informed guidance) — never a purely clinical aerial masterplan render standing alone. The property/project is the *setting*, never positioned as the protagonist (`WORLD_BUILDING.md`'s hard rule, applied here at B2B scale exactly as it applies to consumer content).

## Typography Direction

Formal presentation hierarchy (`visual-system.md §9.8`'s deck-structure logic) — this campaign's primary deliverable is a partnership deck, not a social-first asset, so typography treatment follows presentation conventions (Purple/gradient cover, Canvas content slides, Coral reserved for callout statistics only).

## Photography Direction

Phased and honest — announcement-stage imagery differs from construction-update imagery differs from milestone imagery (`campaign-playbook.md §2.3`'s phased timing structure), each stage using real, current project material, never a single evergreen render reused past its accuracy window.

## Motion Direction

Where video exists (a partnership-announcement film), it favors the same restrained, unhurried pacing as Campaign 01's brand film but in a lower emotional register — no match-cut poetry here, a clear, structured walk from context to vision to partnership ask, per `CINEMATIC_LANGUAGE.md`'s "would this choice make sense in a quiet, honest short film... or only in an advertisement?" test, answered here in the direction of clarity over craft.

## Copy Direction

Professional register throughout, Solve-stage leading (`copywriting.md §1.1` table: developer/project Hook = "brand-safe qualified demand," Solve = "structured project presentation, buyer intent data"):

> **Hook:** Your project deserves buyers who are actually ready — not just traffic.
> **Recognize:** Every developer partner has a different reason a project matters — scale, legacy, a specific design choice worth protecting.
> **Guide:** Your project, presented the way it deserves, in front of buyers who are ready to act.
> **Solve:** Structured, phase-aware presentation. Real buyer-intent data, not vanity impressions.
> **Reassure:** Full brand-safety controls — your project is never presented alongside listings that don't match its standard.
> **Decide:** Discuss your project.

## Social Execution

LinkedIn primary for developer/B2B visibility, Instagram secondary for consumer demand-generation once a partnership is live, dedicated landing/microsite content for each active partnership (`campaign-playbook.md §2.3`). This campaign's own promotional content (the acquisition pitch itself) stays on LinkedIn; once a specific project partnership launches, that project gets its own consumer-facing campaign built from `campaign-playbook.md §2.2`/§2.3 templates — kept separate from this acquisition brief.

## Design Mockup Description

A partnership deck cover (`visual-system.md §9.8`): Purple→Coral gradient background, full white logo lockup, the line "Your project, presented the way it deserves." Section-divider slides throughout follow the same large-number + title pattern as the source guideline PDF's own established convention. A content slide midway through shows a real developer example structure (anonymized as "Project X" until a real named partnership is confirmed) — ground-level human photography of the finished-community vision paired with one clear buyer-intent data visualization, Coral used only on the single most important number on that slide.

## Success Metrics

- Qualified developer partnership inquiries
- Time from first contact to signed partnership (funnel efficiency, a real B2B cycle metric per `developer-personas.md §1`'s "longer B2B sales cycle" note)
- Post-partnership: buyer-intent data volume delivered vs. developer's own baseline
- Developer partner satisfaction/renewal rate (retention metric, tracked in `brand-memory/marketing-insights.md` once real partnerships exist)

## Self-Critique

- **Would this win an international creative award?** No — B2B partnership decks are not award-circuit work, and this campaign is honestly scoped as a functional acquisition tool, not a creative showcase.
- **Would someone remember it next week?** The specific promise (brand-safety controls, phase-aware presentation) is more memorable to a developer than generic "grow with us" language, but this is a low-emotional-recall category by nature.
- **Would it still feel relevant in two years?** Yes — the structural pitch (phased, brand-safe, data-backed) is durable regardless of which specific projects are live.
- **Would this exist without the Tuba brand?** Largely yes — this is the most genuinely competitor-replicable campaign in this set, honestly. What's Tuba-specific is the explicit refusal to treat "Building What Outlasts You" as generic B2B language — the brief insists a developer's specific design choices get the same individualized respect an individual buyer's reasons get elsewhere in this system, which is a real, if subtle, throughline back to the big idea.
