# Campaign 04 — Real Estate Brokers

> **Sources retrieved:** shared grounding + [`../../brand-intelligence/broker-personas.md`](../../brand-intelligence/broker-personas.md) §1 (Independent/Solo Agent, primary) — read for its exact Objection field, which this campaign's Reassure stage is built to answer directly rather than with generic partnership language.
> **Campaign type:** Broker acquisition, B2B (`campaign-playbook.md §2.4`)

---

## Strategic Objective

Grow the verified agent network by directly answering the independent agent's real objection — *"I've been burned by platforms that promised leads and delivered impressions"* — with proof, not a partnership pitch.

## Business Goal

Network growth among independent/solo agents specifically, who are the most price/ROI-sensitive and proof-dependent broker sub-type (`broker-personas.md §1`) — meaning this campaign lives or dies on whether it can show real numbers, not on how warm it sounds.

## Human Insight

Every platform tells agents "join us, grow your business." Independent agents have heard that sentence enough times to be immune to it. What they haven't heard from a real estate platform, specifically, is an admission that lead-quality anxiety is real and reasonable — and a specific answer to it.

## Emotional Territory

**Confidence**, led, with **Gratitude** (from broker-personas.md's "quiet pride" note) as undertone — never the primary register, since pride that's announced rather than earned would violate `EMOTIONAL_SYSTEM.md`'s rule that Pride is "private... never displayed for status."

## Creative Platform

Narrative Pillar 7 — **The People Who Carried You There** (`NARRATIVE_PILLARS.md`) — the one pillar where **The Guide** is briefly allowed to be the protagonist rather than the supporting presence, because this campaign is specifically about *why an agent chooses this work* (`CHARACTER_ARCHETYPES.md` §8's "what they fear: being seen as a salesperson instead of what they actually are"). Every agent's specific way of helping is different — patient, fast, quietly thorough — which is this campaign's own instance of Everyone's Tuba Is Different, applied to the people Tuba works *with*, not just the people it serves.

## Art Direction

Data-forward, balanced with warm partnership language (`campaign-playbook.md §2.4`), never a stock "real estate agent handshake" photography cliché (`visual-system.md §6.1`). Real agent photography where used — circular headshot crop per `design-components.md §1.2`, never a posed stock model.

## Typography Direction

Professional register — standard hierarchy (`visual-system.md §2.3`), no oversized display-tier indulgence; this is Solve/Reassure-stage content for a busy, skeptical professional audience, not a brand-emotion film.

## Photography Direction

Ground-level, specific, credibility-focused (`broker-personas.md §1` visual preference) — a real agent's real workspace or a real, anonymized (with consent) lead-volume dashboard screenshot, never a generic "success" stock image (handshake, upward arrow chart, suited silhouette).

## Motion Direction

Understated — this is professional/B2B register (`copywriting.md §5.2`), so motion serves clarity (a number counting up honestly, a simple UI walkthrough) rather than emotional pacing. No cinematic dissolve treatment here; this campaign earns its warmth through honesty of content, not visual poetry.

## Copy Direction

Professional register, Hook stage compressed since B2B readers want efficiency signaled quickly (`copywriting.md §2.1`):

> **Hook:** You've heard "join our platform, grow your business" before. We know.
> **Recognize:** And you've probably been burned by a platform that promised leads and delivered impressions.
> **Guide:** شريكك، لا مجرد منصة — your partner, not just a platform.
> **Solve:** Real, qualified leads, matched to what you actually list — not a shared inbox everyone's fighting over.
> **Reassure:** See our real numbers before you commit to anything.
> **Decide:** Grow with Tuba.

## Social Execution

LinkedIn primary (`social-media-playbook.md §5`, B2B-facing content pillar leads), supported by targeted performance campaigns to broker-adjacent audiences (`campaign-playbook.md §2.4`). Timed around industry events per the same section's Restatex-style-exhibition note — this campaign is always-on with a scheduled push around the next confirmed regional real estate exhibition.

## Design Mockup Description

A LinkedIn carousel titled *"عملاء حقيقيين، مو مشاهدات / Real leads, not impressions."* Slide 1: the headline alone, Purple background. Slide 2: a simple, honest before/after — not a fabricated case study, a structural explanation of how request-first matching differs from a shared-inbox lead model, illustrated as a clean two-column diagram (crowded inbox icon vs. a single matched request icon, per `iconography.md`'s Purple-only rule). Slide 3: an invitation to see real, current network numbers via direct contact — deliberately not a fabricated statistic, since no real performance data exists yet for this still-growing network (a genuine, honestly-flagged constraint, not a copy weakness). Slide 4: logo lockup, "Grow with Tuba," single CTA.

## Success Metrics

- Qualified agent sign-ups (verified/licensed, not raw form-fills)
- Cost per verified agent acquisition
- LinkedIn engagement rate among broker-tagged audience segments
- Sign-up-to-first-lead time (a direct proof point for the "leads, not impressions" claim — should be tracked and eventually *fed back into future creative* per `brand-memory/cta-performance.md`)

## Self-Critique

- **Would this win an international creative award?** No — B2B lead-quality proof content is not typically award-circuit work, and pretending otherwise would be dishonest.
- **Would someone remember it next week?** The specific objection-naming opener ("we know you've been burned before") is more memorable than a standard partnership pitch, because it's the first thing the reader was already thinking.
- **Would it still feel relevant in two years?** The mechanic (request-first vs. shared-inbox) will stay relevant as long as the product does; the specific copy would need refreshing once real lead-volume data exists to cite.
- **Would this exist without the Tuba brand?** The underlying claim (request-first matching beats a shared lead pool) is genuinely product-specific to Tuba, not borrowed — but the *tone* of this campaign is closer to generic B2B SaaS marketing than any other campaign in this set, and that's worth naming honestly as this campaign's real creative ceiling.
