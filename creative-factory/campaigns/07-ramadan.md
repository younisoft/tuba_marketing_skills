# Campaign 07 — Ramadan

> **Sources retrieved:** shared grounding only — `campaign-playbook.md §1.3` is self-contained (see [`../ROUTING_LOG.md`](../ROUTING_LOG.md)).
> **Campaign type:** Seasonal & Cultural — Ramadan (`campaign-playbook.md §1.3`)

---

## Strategic Objective

Sustain engagement through the category's traditionally slower transaction period using financing-term incentives — never price cuts, a confirmed category convention (`campaign-playbook.md §1.3`, `competitor-analysis.md §10`).

## Business Goal

Ramadan is a real transaction slowdown, not a marketing-created one — this campaign's job is to protect long-term value perception while staying warmly, usefully present, not to force volume against the season's natural rhythm.

## Human Insight

Ramadan is the one month where the "unphotographed rooms" of a home — the prayer corner, the iftar table, the specific chair someone breaks their fast in — matter more than any listing photo ever does. A campaign that tries to sell during Ramadan the way it sells in October misreads the entire month.

## Emotional Territory

**Peace**, led (`EMOTIONAL_SYSTEM.md`: "leads 'The Unphotographed Rooms' almost exclusively"), with **Warmth** supporting via the **Coffee** symbol's hospitality register (`SYMBOL_LIBRARY.md`).

## Creative Platform

Narrative Pillar 5 — **The Unphotographed Rooms** (`narrative-system/CAMPAIGN_UNIVERSE.md`'s Ramadan entry, cited directly), through **The Family Protector** and **The Neighbor** archetypes. Everyone's Ramadan looks different — a different iftar table, a different specific gathering, a different private prayer corner — the season is a natural, built-in proof of the platform's core claim, requiring no invented device the way Campaign 01 needed one; Ramadan already *is* the proof.

## Art Direction

Warm, gathering-oriented Place & Lifestyle imagery (`campaign-playbook.md §1.3`) — never the generic crescent-moon-and-lantern stock cliché the category defaults to; the existing moon icon in the brand's own rounded-outline style replaces it (`iconography.md`, direct instruction from `campaign-playbook.md §1.3`).

## Typography Direction

Standard hierarchy, no seasonal typographic gimmick (ornamental "Ramadan fonts" are explicitly the kind of imported convention `competitor-analysis.md §11` flags as a category mistake) — Almarai/Plus Jakarta Sans, unchanged.

## Photography Direction

Interior-led, evening/lamp-lit warm tones distinct from the golden-hour daytime default (`visual-system.md §6.1` — light should match the emotional hour of the story, per `narrative-system/VISUAL_LANGUAGE.md`'s Light entry), always including a human presence or human trace — the pour of coffee, a set table — per the Coffee symbol's usage rule.

## Motion Direction

The most restrained pacing in this entire deliverable — Ramadan content should feel like it's not trying to compete for attention, per `CINEMATIC_LANGUAGE.md`'s silence principle: "a story is allowed to hold a quiet moment without music or dialogue filling it." No countdown motion graphics under any circumstance (`campaign-playbook.md §1.3`'s explicit "no artificial countdown pressure" rule, doubly relevant during a month about patience and reflection).

## Copy Direction

Full arc, but Reassure-stage carries the financing specificity that makes this campaign real rather than purely atmospheric:

> **Hook:** The room that matters most this month is never the one in the listing photos.
> **Recognize:** The prayer corner. The table that's about to fill up again. The chair by the window where you'll break your fast.
> **Guide:** However you're spending this Ramadan, we're here if a move is part of it.
> **Solve:** No down payment until handover — settle the details after, not before.
> **Reassure:** Every financing term is stated plainly, upfront, in the same place as the property.
> **Decide:** Understand the process.

**Arabic (native, not translated — and this campaign's primary-language version, per `copywriting.md §7.1`):**
> **الفكرة:** الغرفة اللي تهمك أكثر هالشهر أبداً ما تكون اللي في صور الإعلان.
> **الإدراك:** ركن الصلاة. الطاولة اللي بترجع تمتلئ. الكرسي عند الشباك اللي بتفطر جنبه.
> **التوجيه:** أياً كان شكل رمضانك هالسنة، إحنا موجودين لو كان فيه انتقال ضمنه.
> **الحل:** لا دفعة أولى حتى الاستلام — رتّب التفاصيل بعدين، مو قبل.
> **الطمأنة:** كل شرط تمويل مذكور بوضوح، من البداية، بنفس مكان العقار.
> **القرار:** افهم الخطوات.

**Content arc across the month** (per `campaign-playbook.md §1.3`'s explicit staging instruction, not a single static post): early Ramadan = reflection/planning content; mid-Ramadan = financing-term content (the copy above); last 10 days/Eid transition = urgency-free "settle in before Eid" framing, handed off cleanly to Campaign 07's Eid-adjacent successor rather than this brief overreaching into Eid's own register.

## Social Execution

All platforms, increased cadence (`campaign-playbook.md §1.3`'s "intensified digital campaign cadence" note) — Instagram and Snapchat lead for the warm, visual gathering content; the financing-term Reassure content specifically performs better as saved/shared carousel content (`content-system.md` format logic) than as video.

## Design Mockup Description

An Instagram carousel, evening-lit, warm lamp-light color grading distinct from the brand's usual golden-hour daytime default. Slide 1: a single, specific detail — a hand pouring coffee into a small cup, out of focus in the background a table being set — headline only, no body copy. Slides 2–3: the financing message, stated plainly in large type against a simple Purple-8 background, no imagery competing with the number. Slide 4: FAL badge, logo lockup, "افهم الخطوات / Understand the process," single CTA. The moon icon (brand's own rounded outline style, never a generic crescent-and-lantern graphic) appears once, small, as a section-divider mark between slides 1 and 2.

## Success Metrics

- Engagement rate relative to the category's typical Ramadan slowdown baseline (the honest benchmark — this campaign's win condition is *sustaining*, not spiking)
- Financing-term content saves/shares specifically
- Property requests tagged with Ramadan-campaign attribution
- Sentiment/qualitative read on whether the campaign felt "warm and present" vs. "salesy during a sacred month" (a real brand-risk this campaign must actively guard against, named honestly)

## Self-Critique

- **Would this win an international creative award?** Plausible for the financing-message restraint alone (refusing price-cut framing during a religiously significant month is a real point of view, not just compliance) — less plausible for the visual execution, which is warm and correct but not structurally novel the way Campaign 01's device is.
- **Would someone remember it next week?** The specific financing line ("no down payment until handover") is concrete and rememberable; the atmospheric imagery is warm but generically warm unless the specific detail (the coffee pour, the exact chair) is held to consistently in final production.
- **Would it still feel relevant in two years?** Yes — Ramadan recurs annually and this brief's structure (staged arc, financing-not-discount framing) is designed to be reused and refreshed with new specific details each year, not reinvented.
- **Would this exist without the Tuba brand?** The financing-term convention is category-standard, not Tuba-exclusive (honestly noted). What's specifically Tuba is routing the whole campaign through "The Unphotographed Rooms" pillar rather than a generic "Ramadan Mubarak, here's an offer" post — a real, if quiet, differentiation.
