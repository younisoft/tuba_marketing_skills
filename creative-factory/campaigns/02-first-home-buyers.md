# Campaign 02 — First Home Buyers

> **Sources retrieved:** shared grounding ([`../ROUTING_LOG.md`](../ROUTING_LOG.md)) + [`../../brand-intelligence/buyer-personas.md`](../../brand-intelligence/buyer-personas.md) §1 (First-Time Buyer) — read specifically for its Fears/Questions/Objections fields, which this brief's Reassure stage answers directly rather than generically.
> **Campaign type:** Lead Generation, persona-specific (`campaign-playbook.md §3.1` mechanic, `messaging-framework.md` row 2 audience)

---

## Strategic Objective

Convert First-Time Buyer anxiety into qualified property requests by answering their real, named fears before they have to ask — not by discounting or pressuring.

## Business Goal

First-time buyers are the segment most likely to abandon the funnel from information overload, not disinterest (`buyer-personas.md §1` — "doesn't know what questions to ask"). This campaign's job is funnel completion, not top-of-funnel awareness.

## Human Insight

The fear isn't the property. It's the possibility of missing something an experienced person would have caught — a silent, specific dread that generic "we make it easy!" messaging never actually addresses because it never names the real fear out loud (`buyer-personas.md §1` Objection: *"This feels too big to trust to an app"*).

## Emotional Territory

**Relief**, led, with **Confidence** supporting — the exact combination `messaging-framework.md` row 2 specifies for this persona, and the pairing `narrative-system/EMOTIONAL_SYSTEM.md` names as this persona's natural home under Narrative Pillar 1.

## Creative Platform

Narrative Pillar 1 — **First Times** (`narrative-system/NARRATIVE_PILLARS.md`) — through the archetype of **The First Home Seeker**, whose representative beat is named exactly in `narrative-system/CHARACTER_ARCHETYPES.md`: *"the moment fear turns into readiness — not a dramatic reveal, just a quiet internal shift from 'I don't think I can' to 'I think I can.'"* This campaign's entire creative territory is that shift, staged as a real, specific process rather than an inspirational montage. This is still an expression of **Everyone's Tuba Is Different** — this buyer's *particular* fears (not a generic "first-time buyer" checklist) are what the content answers.

## Art Direction

Documentary-plain, not aspirational (`narrative-system/PHOTOGRAPHY_LANGUAGE.md`) — the visual world of someone mid-process, not someone already moved in. Recurring symbol: **Keys** (`SYMBOL_LIBRARY.md`) — used once, at the very end of the campaign arc, never as decorative texture earlier. Trust badge visibly, elegantly integrated per `design-components.md §2.1` on every property-facing asset — Layer 1 (Regulatory) and Layer 4 (Person) trust matter first for this audience (`trust-framework.md §4`).

## Typography Direction

Standard hierarchy (`visual-system.md §2.3`) — no display-tier indulgence here; this is Guide/Solve/Reassure-stage content, which reads better set smaller and denser (educational carousels, step lists) than the single oversized brand-line treatment Campaign 01 uses.

## Photography Direction

Warm, real, uncluttered (`buyer-personas.md §1`'s stated visual preference), simple layouts, trust badges visible without hunting. No staged real-estate-agent-handshake photography (`visual-system.md §6.1`).

## Motion Direction

Calm, procedural pacing — Reels/carousels that move through the process step by step, never rushing the Reassure content (`narrative-system/MOTION_LANGUAGE.md`'s "every asset should have somewhere calm to land" rule applies literally here: the process explainer content should *end* on the calmest beat, not the most exciting one).

## Copy Direction

Compressed six-stage arc, weighted toward Reassure per `messaging-framework.md`'s Cross-Audience Rule 2 ("First-Time Buyers get the fullest arc" — full stages retained, but Reassure gets disproportionate length):

> **Hook:** The scariest part of buying your first home isn't the price. It's not knowing what you don't know.
> **Recognize:** Maybe it's financing. Maybe it's the paperwork. Maybe it's just not knowing who to ask.
> **Guide:** Every question you're afraid to ask is one we're used to answering.
> **Solve:** Tell us what you're looking for — post one request, and licensed agents come to you.
> **Reassure:** Every listing is FAL-licensed. Every agent is REGA-verified. And a real person is always one message away if you get stuck.
> **Decide:** Understand the process.

## Social Execution

Instagram and TikTok lead (Tier 1, `social-media-playbook.md §1`) with **educational carousel and short-video formats specifically** — `content-system.md §1.1`'s 25% Guidance & Education allocation is this campaign's home pillar. YouTube carries a longer-form "everything a first-time buyer needs to know" explainer (`social-media-playbook.md §8`'s stated white-space opportunity). Facebook included deliberately (`buyer-personas.md`'s family-consultation buying journey note matches Facebook's older, family-decision-maker skew — `social-media-playbook.md §7`).

## Design Mockup Description

A 5-slide Instagram carousel titled *"٤ أسئلة يخاف كل مشتري لأول مرة يسألها" / "4 questions every first-time buyer is afraid to ask."* Slide 1: headline only, Purple background, no supporting copy (`content-hooks.md §4`). Slides 2–4: one real question per slide in large type ("How much do I actually need saved?" / "What if something's wrong with the property I can't see?" / "How long does this actually take?"), answered underneath in two short, plain-language sentences — each slide carries the same header treatment and position so the swipe reads as one designed object. Slide 5: FAL badge, full logo lockup, and the single CTA "Understand the process."

## Success Metrics

- Cost per qualified property request (primary KPI, per `campaign-playbook.md §3.1`)
- Request-to-agent-match rate specifically among first-time-buyer-tagged requests
- Carousel completion rate (swipe-through to slide 5) as a proxy for Reassure-stage effectiveness
- Educational content saves/shares (a First-Time-Buyer-specific engagement signal — this audience saves "how-to" content at a higher rate than it likes it)

## Self-Critique

- **Would this win an international creative award?** Unlikely as a craft award — this is disciplined, warm execution of a category-standard format, not a novel creative device. That's the right call for this specific job.
- **Would someone remember it next week?** The specific named fears ("what if something's wrong that I can't see") are more memorable than generic reassurance copy, but the format itself (educational carousel) is not distinctive.
- **Would it still feel relevant in two years?** Yes — first-time-buyer fear is not trend-dependent.
- **Would this exist without the Tuba brand?** Partially — the mechanic (educational Reassure content) could exist for any competitor. What makes it distinctly Tuba is the voice (warm, specific, never condescending — `broker-personas.md`'s "respectful voice adjective" note applies equally here) and the fact that it's written in the *same* voice as the brand's emotional content, not siloed into a separate "academy" register the way both researched competitors do (`content-system.md §1` pillar 1's stated competitor gap). Honestly, this is a *voice* differentiator more than a *concept* differentiator — worth naming plainly rather than overclaiming novelty.
