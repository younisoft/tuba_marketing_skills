# Campaign 06 — Saudi National Day

> **Sources retrieved:** shared grounding only — `campaign-playbook.md §1.1` is self-contained for this task (see [`../ROUTING_LOG.md`](../ROUTING_LOG.md) for what was deliberately not read).
> **Campaign type:** Seasonal & Cultural — Saudi National Day, September 23 (`campaign-playbook.md §1.1`)

---

## Strategic Objective

Associate Tuba with belonging and national pride through many specific, individual stories — never a single generic "we're proud too" post, which `campaign-playbook.md §1.1` and `BIG_IDEA_PLATFORM.md` Part One (rejected cliché #8) both independently flag as the category's defining National Day failure mode.

## Business Goal

National Day is the single most contested seasonal moment in the category (`competitor-analysis.md §10, §11` — every competitor runs near-identical patriotic content). Winning here means refusing the shared format entirely, not out-producing it.

## Human Insight

Every brand's National Day post shows one flag-and-family image and asks the whole country to see themselves in it. But no two Saudis' pride looks the same — a Najdi grandfather's pride is not a Jeddah twenty-something's pride is not a returning expatriate's pride. The category has never let National Day be specific.

## Emotional Territory

**Quiet pride** (`advertising-dna.md §4.1` — explicitly named as core, "especially... National Day"), paired with **Belonging**. Never loud, performed pride — `EMOTIONAL_SYSTEM.md`'s rule that Pride is private applies with extra force during a moment every competitor is trying to perform loudly.

## Creative Platform

Narrative Pillar 6 — **Building What Outlasts You** — combined with Pillar 2, **Your Particular Reasons** (`narrative-system/CAMPAIGN_UNIVERSE.md`'s National Day entry, cited directly: "rather than a single patriotic image everyone is asked to share, many different, specific 'this is my particular Saudi Arabia' stories"). Executed as a real anthology — not four vignettes like Campaign 01, but a genuinely open, ongoing call-and-response format (detailed in Social Execution below) so the "many specific stories" claim is actually demonstrated, not just described.

## Art Direction

Purple-dominant with restrained Coral accent (`campaign-playbook.md §1.1`), Saudi-house icon paired with desert/palm iconography (`iconography.md §4`'s Home + Place pairing, specifically the "Saudi house + palm/desert = heritage, National Day" combination named in that document). Explicitly avoids generic green/white national-color clichés already owned by every other brand's National Day creative (`campaign-playbook.md §1.1`'s direct instruction).

## Typography Direction

Standard display hierarchy — no new typographic device introduced; National Day earns visual distinction through subject matter and specificity, not through breaking the type system (`advertising-dna.md §9`'s consistency rule: campaigns get seasonal content, never a seasonal palette or type swap).

## Photography Direction

Deliberately wide regional range — Najd, Hejaz, Eastern Province, Asir, coastal, urban, rural (`CULTURAL_TRUTHS.md`'s explicit rule against a monolithic "Saudi" depiction) — each submitted or featured story tied to one real, specific place, never an abstracted "desert" backdrop standing in for the whole country.

## Motion Direction

Same restrained, unhurried pacing discipline as every other brand-tier asset in this system (`MOTION_LANGUAGE.md`) — critically, **no countdown timer or urgency motion graphic**, an explicit hard rule this document repeats because National Day content is exactly where competitors are most tempted to add manufactured excitement.

## Copy Direction

Compressed arc — this is a seasonal brand moment, not a lead-gen ask:

> **Hook:** Everyone's Saudi Arabia looks a little different.
> **Recognize:** Maybe it's a specific street in Diriyah. Maybe it's the exact shade of the sky over the Corniche at sunset.
> **Guide/Solve:** This National Day, we're not showing you one Saudi Arabia. We're asking what yours looks like.
> **Reassure:** *(compressed out — no trust claim needed for this asset type)*
> **Decide:** Share yours.

**Arabic (native, not translated):**
> **الفكرة:** وطن كل واحد فينا يبان له شكل مختلف شوي.
> **الإدراك:** يمكن شارع معيّن بالدرعية. يمكن لون السماء وقت الغروب على الكورنيش.
> **التوجيه/الحل:** هالعيد الوطني، ما نبي نعرض عليك وطن واحد. نبي نعرف شكل وطنك أنت.
> **القرار:** شاركنا وطنك.

**Key message line (per `campaign-playbook.md §1.1`'s worked example, retained as the campaign's own anchor, not replaced):** *"العز في أرضنا، والبيت في قلبها"* — pride lives in our land, home lives in its heart.

**Production dependency, stated honestly, not fabricated around:** per `campaign-playbook.md §1.1`, creative must adapt to that year's official government-issued National Day theme before finalizing — this brief is built to be theme-compatible (it names no invented theme of its own) and must be checked against the confirmed official theme before any asset in this campaign goes to production.

## Social Execution

Snapchat leads with a custom AR Lens (`campaign-playbook.md §1.1`'s explicit channel note, and `social-media-playbook.md §4`'s proven-format rule), Instagram and YouTube carry the anthology format: a rolling, real-time content series inviting and re-sharing (with consent) real "this is my Saudi Arabia" submissions across the month leading into September 23 — not a single posted asset, a genuine open call structurally incapable of being reduced to one generic image.

## Design Mockup Description

A Snapchat AR Lens that overlays a simple, elegant Saudi-house silhouette (per `iconography.md`'s existing asset) onto the user's own real environment, with a palm-frond shade pattern animating in softly behind it — the user's own specific place becomes the backdrop, not a stock desert scene. Paired Instagram content: a continuously updating carousel/grid where each tile is one real, submitted "this is my Saudi Arabia" story (a specific street, a specific dish, a specific view), captioned in the submitter's own words, Purple frame consistent across every tile so the growing grid reads as one designed collection even as it expands.

## Success Metrics

- Real user-generated submissions collected and featured (the actual proof the "many specific stories" claim is honest, not just described)
- Snapchat AR Lens engagement/completion rate vs. the Bayut AR Lens benchmark referenced in `competitor-analysis.md`
- Share of voice within National Day category conversation specifically for *distinctiveness* (measured qualitatively — press/social commentary noting Tuba's approach differs from the category pattern), not just volume
- Trade press pickup (per the Aqar National Day precedent, `competitor-analysis.md §6`)

## Self-Critique

- **Would this win an international creative award?** Genuinely plausible — a real, growing user-generated anthology that structurally refuses the category's default single-image approach is a legitimate award-circuit device (comparable in spirit to campaigns built on real crowd-sourced content rather than a single produced hero asset).
- **Would someone remember it next week?** The mechanic (share yours) is more memorable than a static post specifically because it invites participation rather than just observation.
- **Would it still feel relevant in two years?** Yes, structurally — it's designed to run every year, growing richer with each year's accumulated real submissions rather than needing reinvention.
- **Would this exist without the Tuba brand?** The underlying insight (National Day pride is not monolithic) is not exclusive to Tuba, but the mechanism for proving it — real user submissions collected and honored, not a single art-directed hero image — is a direct, deliberate expression of Everyone's Tuba Is Different applied to national identity rather than to home-buying specifically, which is a genuine, non-obvious extension of the platform.
