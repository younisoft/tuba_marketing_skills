# Campaign 03 — Property Investors

> **Sources retrieved:** shared grounding + [`../../brand-intelligence/investor-personas.md`](../../brand-intelligence/investor-personas.md) §1 (Local Individual Investor, primary) and §5 (First-Time Investor, secondary) — read to determine which real question ("what's the realistic yield?") should lead the data, rather than inventing a generic investment pitch.
> **Campaign type:** Investment opportunities (`campaign-playbook.md §2.5`)

---

## Strategic Objective

Position Tuba as a credible investment-decision partner — not just a listings source — for local individual investors, using real district-level data as the lead device.

## Business Goal

Investment content is category-standard (`content-system.md §1` pillar 2 — "match credibility here, differentiate on clarity and tone"), meaning this campaign's job is not to invent a new territory but to out-execute the category's existing convention with genuine data discipline and Tuba's warmer register underneath it.

## Human Insight

This audience distrusts adjective-heavy marketing more than any other Tuba serves (`investor-personas.md §1` Objection: *"I'll just ask my network/agent I already know"*) — because Saudi real estate investment wisdom is culturally transmitted through trusted personal networks, not advertising (`market-psychology.md §7`, referenced via `investor-personas.md §1`). The only way to earn attention here is to out-specify the network, not out-charm it.

## Emotional Territory

**Confidence**, and only Confidence at real weight — `narrative-system/EMOTIONAL_SYSTEM.md` explicitly names this as the register that "leads investor and B2B-adjacent stories," built from a specific checkable fact, never a superlative.

## Creative Platform

Narrative Pillar 6 — **Building What Outlasts You** (`NARRATIVE_PILLARS.md`), the one pillar explicitly built to lead with data before feeling, through the archetype of **The Patient Investor** (`CHARACTER_ARCHETYPES.md`: *"the calm, almost anticlimactic moment of confirming a decision was sound — no fireworks, just quiet vindication"*). Still an instance of Everyone's Tuba Is Different: this campaign's proof point is that *this investor's* specific district, timeline, and risk tolerance are unlike any other investor's — data personalized, not a universal ROI pitch (`BIG_IDEA_PLATFORM.md`'s explicit note that "a data-backed 'tuba' is still a tuba").

## Art Direction

Data-visualization-led (`visual-system.md §9.8` logic) — Coral reserved exclusively for the one standout statistic per asset (`visual-system.md §1.7`), never for decoration. Minimal photography; where used, it is restrained and unstaged (`investor-personas.md §1` visual preference: "minimal decorative photography").

## Typography Direction

Numerals carry the hierarchy here more than headline type — large-format Coral numerals against Purple-8 backgrounds, standard type scale otherwise (`visual-system.md §2.3`). Western Arabic numerals only, per `visual-system.md §2.4`.

## Photography Direction

Where photography appears at all (recurring-report cover imagery, not every asset), it favors the **Horizon Line** symbol (`SYMBOL_LIBRARY.md`) — geography as the subject, Najd flatness or a specific district skyline, never a generic skyscraper stock image (`BIG_IDEA_PLATFORM.md` Part One's rejected cliché #7).

## Motion Direction

Where data visualizations animate (chart build-ins for the monthly report's video summary), motion is a deliberate count-up or bar-fill, soft ease-out, never a flashy chart-reveal transition — the animation should read as *revealing a true number*, not performing excitement about it (`narrative-system/MOTION_LANGUAGE.md`'s "speed as meaning, not habit" principle, applied to data motion specifically).

## Copy Direction

Data first, feeling second — the deliberate inverse of consumer emotional copy (`copywriting.md §5.7`):

> **Lead stat:** Average 3-year appreciation in Al Nakheel: 14%.
> **Translation:** The kind of number that lets you sleep at night.
> **Guide:** See how it compares to the other districts on your list.
> **Solve:** طوبة's monthly "دليل طوبة" report breaks it down, district by district, sourced and current.
> **Reassure:** Every figure is sourced, never estimated for effect.
> **Decide:** See the numbers.

**Arabic:**
> **الرقم:** متوسط ارتفاع القيمة في حي النخيل خلال ٣ سنوات: ١٤٪.
> **الترجمة:** رقم يخليك ترتاح باله.
> **التوجيه:** شوف كيف يقارن بالأحياء الثانية اللي على قائمتك.
> **الحل:** تقرير "دليل طوبة" الشهري يفصّلها حي بحي، موثّقة ومحدّثة.
> **الطمأنة:** كل رقم له مصدر، أبداً ما نقدّره بس عشان يبان حلو.
> **القرار:** شوف الأرقام.

## Social Execution

LinkedIn leads (Tier 2, but the primary channel for this specific audience — `social-media-playbook.md §5`'s stated 40% Market Insight content ratio), YouTube carries deeper data explainers (`campaign-playbook.md §2.5`). "دليل طوبة" (`content-system.md §5`) is the anchor recurring content property this campaign activates, not a one-off asset — Instagram/TikTok carry only the short summary-video cutdown of each monthly report, not primary investment content.

## Design Mockup Description

A LinkedIn-native carousel: slide 1, Purple-8 background, a single Coral numeral "14%" filling most of the frame with the line "average 3-year appreciation, Al Nakheel" beneath it in small Ink type — no logo yet, the number is the hook. Slide 2 compares three districts as a clean horizontal bar chart, Coral marking only the highest bar. Slide 3 states the source and methodology in plain type — proof, not decoration. Slide 4: logo lockup, "See the full دليل طوبة report," single CTA.

## Success Metrics

- Market-report downloads/leads captured
- LinkedIn engagement rate on data-led posts vs. category benchmark
- "دليل طوبة" subscriber growth month over month
- Investor-tagged property requests originating from report content (attribution, not vanity engagement)

## Self-Critique

- **Would this win an international creative award?** No — and that's an honest, correct answer, not a failure. Data-led B2B/investor content is not an award-circuit category; its job is credibility, not spectacle.
- **Would someone remember it next week?** The specific number, yes, if it's genuinely striking and real. The format itself, no more than any competitor's market report.
- **Would it still feel relevant in two years?** The *format* (دليل طوبة as a named recurring property) compounds in value over two years precisely because it's recurring — this is the strongest argument for this campaign's long-term worth.
- **Would this exist without the Tuba brand?** Largely yes, structurally — this is the category's most honestly competitor-replicable campaign in this deliverable. What's Tuba-specific is the *name* ("دليل طوبة" is only Tuba's) and the tonal discipline (`copywriting.md §5.7`'s "feeling second, not feeling absent" — competitors that go full-data typically go cold; this stays warm underneath the numbers).
