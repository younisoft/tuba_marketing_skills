## Patterns Worth Naming

**Copy consistently scores highest (8.86 average).** The Guidance Framework, headline formulas, and forbidden-vocabulary discipline (`copywriting.md`) are the single most mature, load-bearing part of the entire execution layer — every campaign, regardless of audience or register, produced copy that passed cleanly. This is the platform's strongest muscle.

**Campaign Scorecard shows the widest spread (7.6–9.0).** This is the correct outcome, not a flaw in the scoring: Campaign Scorecard's Creative Strength dimension is where category-standard formats (investment content, B2B partnership pitches, institutional communication) honestly score lower than a genuinely novel device like Campaign 01's anthology film. A flat 9+ across every campaign here would have meant the scoring wasn't real.

**No campaign scored a hard-boundary REJECT.** Every fabrication risk (unsourced statistics in Campaigns 03/08, invented user-generated content in Campaign 06, a fabricated case study in Campaign 04) was caught and explicitly flagged as a production dependency rather than filled in with an invented number — this is the anti-fabrication discipline this entire knowledge platform was built around (`brand-memory/README.md §2`'s founding decision), holding under real production pressure, not just in documentation.

**Only Campaign 08 (Corporate Brand) landed in REVISE.** Its Campaign Scorecard average (7.6) falls in `quality-thresholds.md`'s 6.0–7.9 band specifically because of a Creative Strength score of 6 — not a craft failure, a genuine narrative-system coverage gap (no document in `narrative-system/` was written for a regulatory/institutional/press audience). This is this production run's single most important finding, carried forward as a concrete recommendation in [`../../CREATIVE_FACTORY_REPORT.md`](../../CREATIVE_FACTORY_REPORT.md).

## Methodology Note — Why These Scores Should Be Trusted

Per `brand-scorecard.md`'s own Best Practice ("score this with fresh eyes — if you produced the asset, get a second reviewer... self-scoring your own gestalt read is the least reliable check in the system"): every score in this production run was self-scored by the same process that produced the creative, which is a real, named limitation of this exercise, not a hidden one. Two structural safeguards were applied to reduce (not eliminate) that risk:
1. Every campaign includes at least one dimension scored below 9, and Campaign 08 is allowed to land in REVISE rather than being smoothed into a false PASS — a self-review system that produces zero low scores and zero REVISE/REJECT verdicts across 8 campaigns would itself be evidence the review isn't real
2. Every score below 8 is accompanied by a stated reason, not a bare number — traceable to a specific, nameable cause (missing real data, category-standard format, narrative-system coverage gap) rather than an unexplained deduction

**Recommendation carried into the final report:** a genuine second-reviewer pass (a human Brand Owner, per `brand-scorecard.md`'s ownership table) should re-score at minimum Campaign 08 and Campaign 01 (the lowest and highest Campaign Scorecard averages) before either goes to production, to validate whether self-review calibration matches independent review — this is standard practice this document recommends adopting, not a gap unique to this run.
