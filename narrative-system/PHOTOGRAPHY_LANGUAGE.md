# Photography Language

> **Part of:** [`../NARRATIVE_SYSTEM.md`](../NARRATIVE_SYSTEM.md)
> **Extends** [`../advertising-system/visual-system.md`](../advertising-system/visual-system.md) §6 (photography style, property photography rules) with the underlying philosophy — why the rules there exist, not just what they specify.

---

## Human First

Every photograph in this world answers one question before any other: whose story is this? A person, or clear evidence of a person's presence, comes before architecture, before styling, before the "hero shot" of a space. Where visual-system.md §6.2 requires at least one human trace per interior set, this document explains why: a space photographed with no trace of anyone in it is a listing. A space with a trace of someone in it is a home mid-story.

## Architecture — In Service, Not the Subject

Architecture appears constantly in this world, and is the subject of almost nothing. It is filmed the way a supporting character is filmed — present, well-lit, clearly cared about, but never centered at the expense of the person the story is actually about (`WORLD_BUILDING.md`'s hard rule).

## Objects

Objects are used the way a novelist uses a well-chosen detail — one specific, telling object says more than a wide shot of an entire room. A single worn pair of shoes by a door, one particular coffee cup, a height chart in pencil — these carry more narrative weight in this world than a beautifully styled flat-lay ever could.

## Lifestyle vs. Documentary

Documentary over lifestyle, always. "Lifestyle photography," as the category default understands it, means staged scenarios performed for the camera — people laughing at nothing, families posed at unnatural distances. This world's photography looks instead like it caught something that was already happening before the camera arrived, and will keep happening after it leaves.

## Natural Moments

The best photograph in this world is one where the subject seems to have forgotten the camera exists. This is a directing discipline, not a luck-based accident — it comes from giving people something real to do (pour the coffee, read to a child, glance out the actual window) rather than asking them to perform an emotion for the lens.

## Eye Level

As established in `VISUAL_LANGUAGE.md`, this world's camera sits at the same height as its subjects. Photography specifically should avoid the aspirational low-angle "hero" shot common in luxury real estate marketing (`WORLD_BUILDING.md`'s rejected cliché #7) — the camera here is a companion, not an admirer looking up.

## Wide Shots vs. Portraits

Both belong, used for different jobs. Wide shots establish a specific place (a particular street, a particular view, `SYMBOL_LIBRARY.md`'s Horizon Line) — they are about geography and belonging to it. Portraits (often environmental, subject still inside their space rather than isolated against a plain background) are about interiority — what a person is feeling, privately, in this specific place. A story usually needs both; neither should dominate a whole campaign on its own.

## Luxury Photography — the One Deliberate Exception

`../advertising-system/visual-system.md` §6.1 already notes that higher-polish, more editorial photographic treatment is reserved exclusively for explicit luxury-segment work (`../brand-intelligence/luxury-framework.md`). Inside this narrative system, that exception is governed the same way: even at its most editorial, luxury photography in this world stays human-first and restrained — the Quiet Achiever archetype (`CHARACTER_ARCHETYPES.md` §6) is never photographed the way a status-symbol property would be. Higher production value, never higher display.

## What This Photography Avoids

- Wide-angle lens distortion exaggerating a room's apparent size
- Perfectly staged, empty "hero" interiors with no human trace
- Posed, unnatural group arrangements (`WORLD_BUILDING.md`'s "no aspirational strangers" rule)
- Oversaturated, high-gloss HDR treatment (already forbidden at the technical level, `visual-system.md` §6.1)
- Any image that could have been taken of any home, for any brand, anywhere in the world

## The Photography Test

Before a single frame is selected for use, ask: *is there one detail in this photograph that could only belong to this specific person, in this specific place?* If every element could be swapped into a different photo of a different home without anyone noticing, the image hasn't earned its place in this world (`WORLD_BUILDING.md`'s One-Sentence World Rule, applied to photography specifically).
