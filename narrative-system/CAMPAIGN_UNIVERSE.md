# Campaign Universe

> **Part of:** [`../NARRATIVE_SYSTEM.md`](../NARRATIVE_SYSTEM.md)
> **Not the campaigns themselves.** [`../advertising-system/campaign-playbook.md`](../advertising-system/campaign-playbook.md) owns objectives, channels, timing, and KPIs for every campaign category below. This document shows something different: that the same big idea — **Everyone's Tuba Is Different** — genuinely changes shape across thirteen very different campaign contexts without ever becoming a different idea. Each entry names the pillar, archetype, and register a future creative team would reach for — not a headline, not a CTA, not a finished concept.

---

## Ramadan

**Narrative angle:** "The Unphotographed Rooms," specifically — the private, gathering-centered rituals of the month, told through *The Family Protector* and *The Neighbor*.
**How the big idea shows up:** everyone's Ramadan looks different — a different iftar table, a different specific gathering, a different private prayer corner — the season is a natural, built-in proof of the platform's core claim.
**Governing rule inherited, not repeated:** financing-term framing over price-cut framing (`../advertising-system/campaign-playbook.md` §1.3) — this document adds nothing to that rule, only supplies the narrative material that rule gets dressed in.

## Eid

**Narrative angle:** "What Gets Passed Down," through *The Family Protector* and *The Returning Son*.
**How the big idea shows up:** the specific rituals a family repeats — who hosts, which recipe, whose chair — dramatized as evidence that belonging is personal, inherited, and never generic.

## National Day

**Narrative angle:** "Building What Outlasts You" combined with "Your Particular Reasons" — national pride expressed through specific, individual stories of building, not collective slogans.
**How the big idea shows up:** rather than a single patriotic image everyone is asked to share, many different, specific "this is my particular Saudi Arabia" stories — directly avoiding the rejected category cliché of generic "we're proud too" National Day content (`../BIG_IDEA_PLATFORM.md` Part One, #8).
**Governing rule inherited, not repeated:** check that year's official government theme before finalizing any concept (`../advertising-system/campaign-playbook.md` §1.1).

## Investment

**Narrative angle:** "Building What Outlasts You," led by *The Patient Investor*, in the Confidence register (`EMOTIONAL_SYSTEM.md`) — data-first, feeling second.
**How the big idea shows up:** even data-led storytelling proves the same claim — every investor's specific reasons, timeline, and risk tolerance are different, and the strongest investment stories show that particularity rather than a universal ROI pitch.

## Luxury

**Narrative angle:** "Your Particular Reasons" and "The Unphotographed Rooms," led by *The Quiet Achiever*, in restraint (`../brand-intelligence/luxury-framework.md`).
**How the big idea shows up:** luxury storytelling here never displays wealth — it shows one specific, unrepeatable detail that matters intensely to one particular person, which is, structurally, the most natural possible home for "everyone's tuba is different" — restraint and personalization were never in tension.

## Family

**Narrative angle:** "What Gets Passed Down" and "Still Becoming," through *The Family Protector*.
**How the big idea shows up:** not one universal image of "family" (`../BIG_IDEA_PLATFORM.md`'s rejected cliché #6) but many specific family configurations — multigenerational, single-parent, newly married, empty-nest — each with a genuinely different definition of what the home needs to hold.

## Youth

**Narrative angle:** "First Times," through *The Optimist* and *The First Home Seeker*.
**How the big idea shows up:** a younger audience's "tuba" is often smaller, more provisional, and no less real for it — one window, one chair, a first solo apartment — the platform's discipline against inflating small moments into grand ones (`WORLD_BUILDING.md`) matters most here.

## Developers

**Narrative angle:** "Building What Outlasts You," through *The Dream Builder*.
**How the big idea shows up:** a developer's own specific reasons for a project's design — a preserved hill, a protected tree, a community-first choice against advisor recommendation — told the same way an individual buyer's particular reasons would be, refusing the property-as-hero trap (`WORLD_BUILDING.md`) even at commercial scale.

## Brokers

**Narrative angle:** "The People Who Carried You There," through *The Guide*.
**How the big idea shows up:** every broker's specific way of helping is different — some patient, some fast, some quietly thorough — proof-of-value told as recurring character rather than generic professionalism claims.

## CSR (Corporate Social Responsibility)

**Narrative angle:** "The People Who Carried You There" and "Building What Outlasts You," through *The Neighbor* and *The Guide*.
**How the big idea shows up:** community initiatives framed around specific, particular need — a specific school, a specific family, a specific street — never generic "giving back" messaging, which would itself be a category cliché this universe was built to avoid.

## Education

**Narrative angle:** "First Times," through *The First Home Seeker*, in the Confidence-building register.
**How the big idea shows up:** educational content (regulatory explainers, financing literacy) framed as one more form of guidance suited to a *specific* person's actual confusion, never generic "here's how buying works" content — directly extending `../advertising-system/copywriting.md` §5.4's plain-language discipline into narrative form.

## Recruitment

**Narrative angle:** "The People Who Carried You There," told from *The Guide*'s own perspective — a rare campaign category where the Guide is briefly the protagonist rather than the supporting presence, because the story here is about *why someone chooses this work*.
**How the big idea shows up:** every recruit's specific reason for wanting to be a guide is different — echoing the same individuality claim, now applied to the people Tuba wants to hire, not just the people Tuba serves.

## Partnerships

**Narrative angle:** "Building What Outlasts You," through *The Dream Builder* and *The Patient Investor* together.
**How the big idea shows up:** a partnership story is about two specific, particular sets of reasons aligning — never a generic "synergy" claim, but the story of why *this* developer and *this* platform, specifically, make sense together.

---

## The Test That Holds All Thirteen Together

Every category above answers the same question differently: *whose particular "tuba" is this story about, and what makes it unrepeatable?* A campaign that could swap its category label with another on this list, unchanged, has failed the platform (the campaign-level expression of `WORLD_BUILDING.md`'s One-Sentence World Rule). Thirteen genuinely different expressions, one idea underneath all of them.
