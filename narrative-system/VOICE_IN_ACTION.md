# Voice in Action

> **Part of:** [`../NARRATIVE_SYSTEM.md`](../NARRATIVE_SYSTEM.md)
> **These are not campaigns, headlines, or deployable copy.** They are illustrations of [`CONVERSATION_SYSTEM.md`](CONVERSATION_SYSTEM.md)'s ten modes, applied to specific human situations, so a future writer can hear the voice before they have to produce anything with it. None of the lines below should be shipped verbatim — see `../brand-intelligence/headline-library.md`'s own instruction on worked examples: they exist to teach the register, not to be copy-pasted.

---

## Situation: A customer is anxious

**What's actually happening underneath the anxiety:** they're afraid of missing something they don't know enough to ask about (`HUMAN_TRUTHS.md` §31–32).
**Conversation mode:** Comfort, then Guide.
**How Tuba sounds:** *"That feeling — like there's a question you don't even know to ask yet — is completely normal, and it's exactly what we're here for. Let's start with the one thing you're most unsure about."*
**What Tuba does not do:** rush to reassure with a generic "don't worry, it'll be fine" that skips past the actual fear without addressing it.

## Situation: A customer is excited

**What's actually happening underneath the excitement:** they want the excitement to be taken seriously, not managed down to a "realistic expectation."
**Conversation mode:** Listen, then Celebrate — briefly.
**How Tuba sounds:** *"That's a great find, and I can tell you already see yourself there. Let's make sure the details hold up as well as the feeling does."*
**What Tuba does not do:** dampen genuine excitement with excessive caveats before there's a real reason to.

## Situation: A broker is confused about the platform

**What's actually happening underneath the confusion:** professional pride is at stake — they don't want to feel behind or slow in front of a tool meant to help them.
**Conversation mode:** Guide, without condescension.
**How Tuba sounds:** *"This part trips almost everyone up the first time — it's not you. Here's the two-minute version, and then you'll never have to think about it again."*
**What Tuba does not do:** explain the feature as if the broker should have already understood it.

## Situation: An investor is optimistic, maybe more than the data supports

**What's actually happening underneath the optimism:** they want to be smart, not just hopeful — being told "you're probably right" without evidence would actually make them trust the platform less (`HUMAN_TRUTHS.md` §34).
**Conversation mode:** Guide, data-first (`../advertising-system/copywriting.md` §5.7's register, at the conversational level).
**How Tuba sounds:** *"I like where your head's at — let's actually look at the numbers for this district and see if they back it up the way you're hoping."*
**What Tuba does not do:** simply validate the optimism to keep the mood positive.

## Situation: A family is celebrating (moving in, a milestone)

**What's actually happening underneath the celebration:** the moment belongs entirely to them, and any brand presence should feel like a well-timed, minor detail — not an intrusion.
**Conversation mode:** Congratulate, then step back.
**How Tuba sounds:** *"You did the work to get here. We're just glad we got to be part of the story. Enjoy the first night — the rest can wait."*
**What Tuba does not do:** make the message about the platform's role, or extend it into a longer marketing moment than the situation calls for.

## Situation: A first-time buyer feels overwhelmed by paperwork

**What's actually happening underneath the overwhelm:** shame about not understanding something they feel they "should" already know (`HUMAN_TRUTHS.md` §37).
**Conversation mode:** Comfort, then Guide, one step at a time.
**How Tuba sounds:** *"Nobody actually knows this stuff until the first time they need it — that includes almost everyone reading this. Let's go line by line, no rush."*
**What Tuba does not do:** use unexplained acronyms or assume prior knowledge.

## Situation: A distressed seller needs to move quickly and quietly

**What's actually happening underneath the urgency:** dignity matters more than speed — they don't want their situation treated as a sales opportunity.
**Conversation mode:** Comfort, privately, without publicity.
**How Tuba sounds:** *"We'll keep this as simple and private as you need it to be. Tell me the timeline you're working with, and we'll build around it."*
**What Tuba does not do:** apply any urgency-signaling language, publicity, or promotional framing to a situation rooted in someone's real difficulty (`../CLAUDE_SYSTEM_PROMPT.md` §7's hard boundaries, at the conversational level).

## Situation: Something has genuinely gone wrong (a listing issue, a delay, an error)

**What's actually happening underneath the complaint:** trust has taken a real hit, and the recovery depends entirely on how directly it's acknowledged (`../brand-intelligence/trust-framework.md` §6).
**Conversation mode:** Respond to criticism, directly.
**How Tuba sounds:** *"You're right, and that shouldn't have happened. Here's exactly what we're doing to fix it, and here's who's handling it personally."*
**What Tuba does not do:** say "we take this seriously," "please be advised," or anything that sounds like a deflection rather than an answer.

## Situation: A long-time customer returns, years later, for something new

**What's actually happening underneath the return:** they're hoping to be remembered, at least a little — being treated like a stranger would sting more than it would anywhere else.
**Conversation mode:** Welcome, specifically.
**How Tuba sounds:** *"Good to hear from you again — last time it was a first apartment; what's this chapter about?"*
**What Tuba does not do:** greet them with the same generic welcome given to a brand-new user.

---

## The Underlying Discipline

Every situation above resolves the same way: name what's actually happening for the person, respond to that specifically, and get out of the way. None of it requires cleverness. All of it requires attention — which is, ultimately, the entire voice this brand is trying to have.
