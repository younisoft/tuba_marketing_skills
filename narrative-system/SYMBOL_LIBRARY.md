# Symbol Library

> **Part of:** [`../NARRATIVE_SYSTEM.md`](../NARRATIVE_SYSTEM.md)
> **Not logos.** [`../advertising-system/iconography.md`](../advertising-system/iconography.md) defines Tuba's *icon system* — production specs, stroke weights, Purple-only color rule, filled/outline treatment. This document defines Tuba's *symbolic vocabulary* — the recurring objects and images a story reaches for because they carry meaning, independent of whether they ever become a literal icon. A symbol here might inspire an icon there, but the two systems answer different questions: iconography.md asks "how is it drawn," this asks "what does it mean."

Sixteen symbols. Each with a meaning, an emotion, a usage rule, and a visual note. Used sparingly, per each entry's own guidance — a story that reaches for five symbols at once has stopped trusting its own specificity.

---

## Door

**Meaning:** the decision point itself — not yet crossed, still a choice.
**Emotion:** anticipation.
**Usage:** appears before a threshold moment, never during or after — its job is to hold a story in the instant just before change.
**Visual expression:** closed, slightly ajar, or mid-swing — never wide open (that belongs to Threshold, below).

## Threshold

**Meaning:** the crossing itself — the exact boundary between outside and inside, before and after.
**Emotion:** the whole emotional register of arrival, compressed into one physical inch.
**Usage:** the single most important recurring symbol in the entire system — nearly every "First Times" and "Long Way Back" story can be told through this one image alone.
**Visual expression:** a foot mid-step, a shadow falling across a doorframe, the specific texture change between outdoor stone and indoor tile.

## Window

**Meaning:** connection without full exposure — seeing and being seen, on one's own terms.
**Emotion:** hope, or watchfulness, depending on which side of it the story sits.
**Usage:** the frame for a specific view that belongs to no other window — never a generic "nice view," always *the* view.
**Visual expression:** light falling through in a particular shape at a particular hour; a specific silhouette of what's visible beyond it (a mountain line, a palm, a neighbor's wall).

## Light (Morning)

**Meaning:** a fresh, unclaimed start — the day, and the chapter, before either has been used up.
**Emotion:** quiet hope.
**Usage:** the default light of "First Times" and "Still Becoming" stories — reserve harsher midday or dramatic sunset light for stories about effort or arrival specifically.
**Visual expression:** long, low, warm light; soft shadow edges, not hard ones.

## Shade

**Meaning:** relief, shelter, rest earned rather than given — the physical expression of the brand's own etymological root (`../BIG_IDEA_PLATFORM.md` Part Two), used structurally, never announced.
**Emotion:** relief.
**Usage:** the most brand-loaded symbol in this library — powerful specifically because it is never explained on-screen. It is felt, not narrated. Never paired with literal religious imagery (`WORLD_BUILDING.md`'s hard rule).
**Visual expression:** dappled light through leaves on a wall; the specific relief of stepping from direct sun into a covered walkway or majlis; a tree's shadow reaching further than expected.

## Tree

**Meaning:** growth over time, and — carefully, structurally — the brand's own namesake resonance. A tree in this world is never a religious symbol on screen; it is a symbol of patience, shade, and roots doing invisible work.
**Emotion:** Growth, quiet Pride.
**Usage:** best used sparingly and specifically — one particular tree a family has watched grow, not decorative greenery.
**Visual expression:** roots visible at the base, canopy providing shade for something specific beneath it (a bench, a play area, a majlis).

## Road

**Meaning:** the search itself, or the return itself — motion toward something not yet arrived at.
**Emotion:** determination, or homesickness, depending on direction.
**Usage:** used for stories mid-journey — the road disappears from frame the moment a story reaches its threshold.
**Visual expression:** a road disappearing into heat-shimmer or distance; a specific, recognizable stretch of road rather than a generic highway.

## Roots

**Meaning:** the invisible foundation beneath a visible life — family history, regulatory trust, unseen preparation.
**Emotion:** Security.
**Usage:** rarely shown literally (roots are, definitionally, unseen) — usually implied through its effects: stability, longevity, a tree or family that has clearly been somewhere a long time.
**Visual expression:** if shown at all, partial and half-buried, never a full anatomical root system — restraint is the entire point.

## Stone

**Meaning:** permanence, the old and enduring — Najdi and Hejazi building traditions specifically, not stone in the abstract.
**Emotion:** Security, Pride.
**Usage:** stories about heritage, "What Gets Passed Down," older family homes.
**Visual expression:** texture close-up — weathering, hand-laid irregularity, the specific warmth of local stone rather than a generic quarried finish.

## Brick / Clay

**Meaning:** building by hand, deliberate construction, the opposite of instant or prefabricated.
**Emotion:** Achievement, patience rewarded.
**Usage:** "Building What Outlasts You" — developer and long-horizon investment stories specifically.
**Visual expression:** materials mid-construction, texture and craft visible, never a finished glass facade standing in for "progress."

## Sky

**Meaning:** scale, possibility, the size of a dream relative to a person.
**Emotion:** Hope.
**Usage:** used to open a story, rarely to close one — the sky is where a story starts looking, not where it ends up.
**Visual expression:** the specific color of a Gulf sky at a specific hour — never a generic blue-sky stock treatment.

## Water

**Meaning:** in a desert climate, an amplified symbol of relief, abundance, and rare, treasured stillness — a fountain in a courtyard, the sea along the Hejaz coast, rain.
**Emotion:** Peace, Gratitude.
**Usage:** geography-specific — coastal stories, courtyard stories, rare-rain stories. Never generic infinity-pool luxury signaling (`WORLD_BUILDING.md`'s rejected cliché).
**Visual expression:** stillness over spectacle — a reflection, a single ripple, condensation on a glass, rather than a dramatic wave or fountain display.

## Coffee (and the ritual around it)

**Meaning:** hospitality, pause, the specific rhythm of Saudi daily and social life.
**Emotion:** Warmth.
**Usage:** the most reliable symbol for "The Unphotographed Rooms" — a small, repeatable ritual any story can use to ground a moment in real, lived texture.
**Visual expression:** the pour, the cup, the specific hand holding it — never a styled "lifestyle" flat-lay.

## Keys

**Meaning:** transferred trust, the literal handover of responsibility and possibility.
**Emotion:** Achievement, Relief.
**Usage:** the single most direct symbol of arrival — use once per story, at the moment it matters most, never as decorative texture.
**Visual expression:** worn metal, a specific keychain or detail that identifies whose keys they are, never a generic stock-photo key-and-lockbox image.

## The Height Chart (or any mark of growth on a wall)

**Meaning:** time made visible inside a single, unmoving space — proof that a home changes because the people in it do.
**Emotion:** Nostalgia, Growth.
**Usage:** "Still Becoming" and "What Gets Passed Down" — a strong closing image for stories about a home across years, not a single moment.
**Visual expression:** pencil marks, dates, a doorframe — small, easy to miss, meant to reward a close look.

## Horizon Line (Desert or Coastal)

**Meaning:** Saudi geography itself as a character — the flatness of Najd, the water's edge in the Hejaz, distance as a physical, felt thing.
**Emotion:** Belonging to a specific place, not place in the abstract.
**Usage:** establishing shots, or the final image of a "Long Way Back" story — the geography a person is specifically tied to, not scenery.
**Visual expression:** wide, quiet, unpopulated — the one symbol in this library allowed to hold a frame without a human figure in it, because the land itself is the subject.

---

## Governing Rule for This Library

No symbol here is exclusive to Tuba — a door, a window, light, are universal. What makes their use *Tuba's* is the discipline of §`WORLD_BUILDING.md`'s One-Sentence World Rule applied to each: never a generic door, always a specific one, with a specific history, belonging to a specific story. The symbol is the vessel; the specificity is what makes it recognizably this brand's.
