# Emotional System

> **Part of:** [`../NARRATIVE_SYSTEM.md`](../NARRATIVE_SYSTEM.md)
> **Not a word bank.** [`../brand-intelligence/emotional-keywords.md`](../brand-intelligence/emotional-keywords.md) supplies the actual vocabulary — English and Arabic words proven to carry each emotion in copy. This document maps the *narrative logic* underneath that vocabulary: which emotion is allowed to be the point of a story, which is only ever felt in the background, and which this brand refuses to use at all, regardless of how effective it might be.

Twelve emotional territories. Each mapped against three questions: when does it lead a story, when does it only support one, and when is it never appropriate.

---

## Hope

**When it leads:** stories at the very beginning of a search — the Optimist archetype's natural home, and the opening beat of almost every "First Times" story.
**When it supports:** almost everywhere, as undertone — even a story primarily about Relief or Peace usually has Hope quietly present as its origin point.
**Never used:** as false reassurance about something genuinely uncertain. Hope in this world is always paired, eventually, with a real reason — never manufactured optimism with nothing underneath it.

## Relief

**When it leads:** arrival and threshold moments — the emotional payoff of "First Times" and the entire premise of the brand's founding promise (`../advertising-dna.md` §2).
**When it supports:** almost every other emotion in this list arrives *after* some quantity of relief has already been felt — it is the most structurally load-bearing emotion in the system.
**Never used:** manufactured through fear first. Relief that only exists because a story invented a threat to relieve is a cheap trick this brand does not use (`../CLAUDE_SYSTEM_PROMPT.md` §7's ban on fear-based urgency extends to narrative structure, not just copy).

## Pride

**When it leads:** rarely, and only quietly — "Your Particular Reasons" and "Building What Outlasts You" stories about The Quiet Achiever or The Patient Investor.
**When it supports:** the closing beat of most stories, present but never announced.
**Never used:** performed for an audience inside the story itself. Pride in this world is private — the moment someone allows themselves to feel it alone, or with one other person, never displayed for status (`WORLD_BUILDING.md`'s hard rule against status display).

## Security

**When it leads:** stories built around The Family Protector, and any "First Times" story where the underlying fear (per `HUMAN_TRUTHS.md` §IV) is being directly, gently addressed.
**When it supports:** the quiet floor beneath almost every family-oriented story — present even when the surface emotion is Joy or Excitement.
**Never used:** built on top of an exaggerated threat. Security that has to invent danger to feel earned isn't security — it's a fear story wearing a reassurance costume.

## Excitement

**When it leads:** rarely, and briefly — a single beat (the moment of a key turning, a first walk-through) rather than a story's entire emotional register.
**When it supports:** the spike inside a larger story that's mostly built on Relief or Hope.
**Never used:** sustained for an entire story. Excitement that doesn't settle into something calmer by the story's end reads as hype, not truth — and this world doesn't deal in hype.

## Peace

**When it leads:** "The Unphotographed Rooms" almost exclusively — the pillar built entirely around this emotion.
**When it supports:** the destination every other emotion in this system is, in some sense, moving toward.
**Never used:** interrupted by an unearned dramatic turn. A Peace-led story that suddenly needs conflict to justify itself has misunderstood what Peace is for.

## Achievement

**When it leads:** "Building What Outlasts You" — The Patient Investor and The Dream Builder's natural territory.
**When it supports:** the second half of almost any long-horizon story, arriving only after real patience has been shown on screen.
**Never used:** as a synonym for status or scale. Achievement here is measured against a person's *own* prior effort, never against comparison to someone else (`HUMAN_TRUTHS.md` §94, §97).

## Belonging

**When it leads:** the primary emotion of the entire brand (`../advertising-dna.md` §4.1) and of "Your Particular Reasons" specifically.
**When it supports:** present under nearly every story in this system, the way a bassline is present under most of a song without being the melody.
**Never used:** claimed generically. A story that states "this is where you belong" without a specific, earned reason has failed at the one emotion this entire narrative universe exists to protect.

## Confidence

**When it leads:** investor and B2B-adjacent stories — The Patient Investor, The Guide, developer and broker narratives.
**When it supports:** the internal shift inside almost every "First Times" story, usually arriving quietly around the story's midpoint.
**Never used:** built from superlatives or unverifiable claims (`../advertising-dna.md` §5). Confidence in this world is always built from a specific, checkable fact or a demonstrated competence, never an assertion.

## Gratitude

**When it leads:** "The People Who Carried You There" — the primary emotional register for stories centered on The Guide or The Neighbor.
**When it supports:** the final beat of almost any story that involved help along the way — present, but rarely dwelt on for long, per Saudi cultural instinct around hospitality received (`CULTURAL_TRUTHS.md`) being acknowledged simply, not effusively.
**Never used:** manufactured through a staged "surprise and delight" moment. Gratitude here is earned across a whole story, not manufactured in its final ten seconds.

## Nostalgia

**When it leads:** "What Gets Passed Down" and "The Long Way Back."
**When it supports:** a single beat inside longer stories — a specific object, smell, or sound (`SYMBOL_LIBRARY.md`) doing the emotional work in one instant, not sustained across an entire narrative.
**Never used:** as pure sentimentality with no forward motion. Nostalgia in this world always connects a past specific detail to a present or future one — it looks back in order to keep moving, not to stay there.

## Growth

**When it leads:** "Still Becoming" exclusively — the pillar this emotion belongs to most.
**When it supports:** the long-arc undertone of any story spanning more than a single moment.
**Never used:** as change for its own sake. Growth here is always growth *toward* something specific a character already, quietly, wanted (`HUMAN_TRUTHS.md` §96) — never disruption presented as inherently virtuous.

---

## The One Rule Above All Twelve

No story in this universe should try to carry more than two of these emotions at meaningful weight at once. A story led by Belonging and supported by Relief is coherent. A story attempting Excitement, Pride, Achievement, and Nostalgia simultaneously is not a story — it's a mood board, and it will not move anyone (the narrative-level expression of `../advertising-dna.md` Principle 4's "one idea per asset," carried up from copy discipline into story discipline).
